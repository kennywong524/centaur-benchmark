Pattaraphon Kenny Wongchamcharoen, Kris Gulati, Min Min Fong, Abhishek Nagaraj · Data Innovation and AI Lab, Haas School of Business, UC Berkeley

Project overview

Benchmarking assistance, not just task completion.

Centaur evaluates LLMs as both task solvers and helpers. Across seven professional tasks, each model either completes the work directly or writes a scaffold for a fixed worker model; rubric-guided judges then compare final outputs. The benchmark produces role- and task-specific model profiles, showing when a model is better suited for automation, augmentation, or cost-aware multi-agent workflows.

Automation

Focal models produce final deliverables directly. This approximates delegating a complete task to an AI system.

Augmentation

Focal models write process scaffolds for GPT-3.5-Turbo, and the fixed worker produces the final deliverable.

Evaluation

LLM judges perform blind pairwise comparisons with task-specific rubric scores and leave-family-out masking.

How To Read Judge Agreement

Spearman. Rank correlation between two judges. Higher means they order models more similarly; 1.0 would be identical ranking.

Within one rank. Share of shared model-task entries where two judges place the model no more than one rank apart.

Mean gap. Average absolute rank difference. Lower means closer agreement.
