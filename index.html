How To Use This Dashboard

1. Choose a rank universe. Use Exclude baselines for paper-ready 1-9 rank-of-ranks figures, or All candidates to audit the full run.

2. Read aggregate patterns. Use the heat maps and role-swap scatter to compare automation and augmentation profiles.

3. Drill into a task. In Rankings, click a model row to update rubric scores for that selected response.

4. Audit judgments. In Qualitative, inspect outputs, scaffolds, scaffold prompts, and pairwise judge rationales with expandable scorecards.

How To Read Judge Agreement

Spearman. Rank correlation between two judges. Higher means they order models more similarly; 1.0 would be identical ranking.

Within one rank. Share of shared model-task entries where two judges place the model no more than one rank apart.

Mean gap. Average absolute rank difference. Lower means closer agreement.
