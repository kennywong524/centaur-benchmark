Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow

1. Requirements Check  
• Begin by thoroughly reviewing the task guidelines and constraints.  
• Identify the key elements required by the task, ensuring each component is acknowledged.  
• Confirm that the lesson segment will adhere to the target audience’s comprehension level, terminology, and format, as outlined by the task.  
• Re-read any provided instructions to ensure no critical detail is overlooked and all hard constraints are clearly understood.

2. Plan & Structure  
• Outline the flow of the lesson segment, determining the order in which components will be presented.  
• Develop an organized structure highlighting sections for the learning objective, explanation, analogies, examples, misconceptions, interactive elements, checks for understanding, and wrap-up.  
• Consider the sequencing and logical transitions needed to keep the segment engaging and accessible for the intended age group, outlining where the detailed content will be inserted later.  
• Review the outline against the requirements to ensure completeness and relevance while maintaining the appropriate tone.

3. Draft -> Self-Review -> Finalize  
• Create an initial draft using the outline as a roadmap, populating each section with placeholder text where necessary.  
• Conduct a self-review focusing on clarity, flow, and adherence to the target audience’s language needs without yet including final content.  
• Evaluate the draft against all task guidelines and constraints, then make adjustments to the structure, ensuring consistency and completeness before finalizing the version for further development.
