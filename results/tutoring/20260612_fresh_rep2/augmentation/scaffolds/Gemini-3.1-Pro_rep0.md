**Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow**

**1. Requirements Check**
- Target audience: 8-year-old students (Grade 3).
- Topic: Improper fractions and mixed numbers.
- Required components: Learning objective, simple explanation, analogy, worked examples, common misconceptions, interactive activity, checks for understanding, and a short wrap-up.
- Tone/Style constraints: Age-appropriate language, classroom-ready format, and avoidance of abstract notation without visual support (always pair numbers with visual descriptions).

**2. Plan & Structure**
- **Introduction:** Draft a clear, student-friendly learning objective. Follow with a simple, relatable analogy to introduce the concept of having "more than one whole."
- **Direct Instruction:** Plan out the simple explanation. Detail how to transition from the analogy to the mathematical terms (improper fractions and mixed numbers). Outline the worked examples, ensuring visual representations are explicitly described.
- **Troubleshooting:** Identify 1-2 common misconceptions 8-year-olds face with this topic and plan how to address them gently.
- **Engagement:** Design an interactive activity that requires minimal materials and keeps young learners engaged. 
- **Assessment & Closure:** Formulate 2-3 quick checks for understanding (e.g., thumbs up/down, whiteboard answers) and a concise, encouraging wrap-up summarizing the lesson.

**3. Draft -> Self-Review -> Finalize**
- *Draft:* Write the lesson segment following the structured plan. Ensure the language is enthusiastic, supportive, and strictly tailored to a third-grade comprehension level.
- *Self-Review:* Check against the requirements. Are all required components present? Is the analogy clear? Are visual supports explicitly mentioned alongside any abstract notation? Is the tone right for 8-year-olds? 
- *Finalize:* Refine the formatting (using bolding, bullet points, or clear headings) to make it highly readable and ready for a teacher to use immediately.
