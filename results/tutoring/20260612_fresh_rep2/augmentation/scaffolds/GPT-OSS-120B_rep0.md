**Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow**

**1. Requirements Check**  
- Verify the target audience (Grade 3, ~8‑year‑olds) and ensure all requested elements are listed: learning objective, simple explanation, analogy, worked examples, misconceptions, interactive activity, comprehension checks, and wrap‑up.  
- Confirm constraints on language level (age‑appropriate, minimal abstract symbols) and the need for visual‑friendly wording.  
- Note any formatting preferences (bullet points, short paragraphs, clear headings) and any required length limits for each component.  
- Identify any missing information (e.g., time allotment, classroom resources) and flag questions for clarification before proceeding.

**2. Plan & Structure**  
- Outline the lesson segment using a logical flow: start with the objective, move to explanation, then analogy, followed by examples, misconceptions, activity, checks, and wrap‑up.  
- Allocate approximate word counts or time blocks to each section to keep the segment balanced for a short class period.  
- Choose an everyday analogy that resonates with 8‑year‑olds (e.g., pizza slices, chocolate bars) and plan visual cues that can be described without actual images.  
- Draft concise, concrete examples that illustrate the transition from improper fractions to mixed numbers, and list common errors in simple terms.  
- Design an interactive activity that encourages hands‑on manipulation or verbal participation, and embed quick formative‑assessment prompts.

**3. Draft → Self‑Review → Finalize**  
- Write a first draft following the planned outline, using short sentences, familiar vocabulary, and explicit instructional cues.  
- Conduct a self‑review: check for age‑appropriate language, completeness of each required element, logical sequencing, and clarity of instructions. Verify that no abstract notation is used without a visual description.  
- Revise based on the review, tightening wording, adding missing details, and ensuring consistency in formatting.  
- Perform a final proofread for spelling, grammar, and adherence to the original constraints before delivering the lesson segment.
