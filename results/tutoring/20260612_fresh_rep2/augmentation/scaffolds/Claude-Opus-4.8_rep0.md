**Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow**

**Phase 1: Requirements Check**
Begin by listing every required component named in the task and confirming none are overlooked. Note the audience (8-year-olds, Grade 3) and the constraints—age-appropriate language and visual support for any notation. Clarify what "classroom-ready" implies for tone, format, and pacing. Identify the deliverable's required elements: objective, explanation, analogy, worked examples, misconceptions, activity, checks, wrap-up. Flag any ambiguity and decide reasonable defaults before writing.

**Phase 2: Plan & Structure**
Map each required component to a section in your output, ordering them in a logical teaching flow that moves from concept introduction toward practice and closure. Decide how much space each section needs given the "segment" scope. Plan where visuals or concrete representations should anchor abstract ideas. Sketch the sequence so the analogy supports the explanation, the worked examples reinforce it, the misconceptions section anticipates errors, and the activity and checks let learners apply skills. Confirm the objective frames everything that follows.

**Phase 3: Draft → Self-Review → Finalize**
Draft each section using vocabulary suited to young learners, keeping sentences short and concrete. After drafting, review against your Phase 1 checklist: is every component present, age-appropriate, and visually supported? Verify internal consistency—does the activity match the objective, do checks assess what was taught? Trim jargon, adjust pacing, and ensure smooth transitions. Read once more from a teacher's perspective for usability, then finalize the polished segment.
