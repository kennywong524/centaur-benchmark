**Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow**

1. Requirements Check  
- Carefully parse the request to identify all mandated components (e.g., objective, explanation, examples, activities, checks, wrap-up).  
- Note audience specifics (Grade 3, 8-year-olds) and style constraints (simple, visual, no abstract notation alone).  
- Highlight any formatting or language guidelines (age-appropriate tone, visuals encouraged).  
- Flag any ambiguities or missing details and formulate clarification questions if needed.  

2. Plan & Structure  
- Sketch a high-level outline that allocates space to each required element in a logical classroom flow (introduction, teaching segment, practice, conclusion).  
- Determine sequencing: start with objective, move to explanation, then analogy, examples, interactive tasks, checks, and wrap-up.  
- Identify places to integrate visuals or manipulatives and note where to embed formative questions.  
- Allocate approximate time or word counts per section to maintain focus and pacing.  
- Draft brief section headers and bullet the core purpose of each (e.g., “Analogy: relatable scenario,” “Check: quick thumbs-up question”).  

3. Draft -> Self-Review -> Finalize  
- Produce an initial draft following the outline, inserting placeholders for visuals or prompts where needed.
