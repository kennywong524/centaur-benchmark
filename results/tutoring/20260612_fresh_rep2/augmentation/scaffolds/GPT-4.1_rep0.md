**Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow**

1. Requirements Check  
- Carefully review the task instructions to identify all required lesson components (e.g., learning objective, explanation, analogy, examples, misconceptions, activity, checks, wrap-up).  
- Confirm the target audience’s grade and age to ensure content is developmentally appropriate.  
- Note constraints such as avoiding abstract notation, using visual support, and maintaining age-appropriate language.  
- Ensure the lesson segment is classroom-ready and sufficiently detailed for practical use.

2. Plan & Structure  
- Outline the lesson segment, allocating a logical sequence for each required component (objective, explanation, analogy, etc.).  
- Decide on the teaching approach, ensuring concepts are broken down simply and supported visually.  
- Plan for engaging elements, such as interactive activities and checks for understanding, that suit 8-year-olds.  
- Organize the content flow to build understanding step-by-step, moving from introduction to wrap-up.  
- Prepare to address common misconceptions and ensure each part aligns with the learning objective.

3. Draft -> Self-Review -> Finalize  
- Write the lesson segment, filling in each section according to the outline and requirements.  
- After drafting, review for clarity, age-appropriateness, and adherence to all constraints (e.g., language, visual aids).  
- Check that all required elements are present and logically connected.  
- Revise as needed for coherence, engagement, and instructional effectiveness before finalizing the lesson segment.
