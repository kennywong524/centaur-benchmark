**Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow**

---

**Phase 1: Requirements Check**

- Identify all explicitly requested components (learning objective, explanation, analogy, worked examples, misconceptions, activity, comprehension checks, wrap-up) and confirm none are omitted.
- Note the audience constraints: age 8, Grade 3, classroom-ready, age-appropriate language, no abstract notation without visual support.
- Flag any ambiguities (e.g., desired lesson length, whether manipulatives are assumed available, print vs. digital format) and resolve them with reasonable assumptions before proceeding.
- Confirm the dual concept scope: both improper fractions AND mixed numbers must be addressed, including the relationship between them.

---

**Phase 2: Plan & Structure**

- Sequence the components in a pedagogically logical order (objective → explanation → analogy → examples → activity → misconceptions addressed → checks → wrap-up).
- Decide how visual support will be integrated alongside any notation (e.g., diagrams, drawings, or described manipulatives).
- Plan differentiation within the activity to keep it accessible and engaging for varied learners.
- Ensure the interactive activity and comprehension checks are distinct and serve different instructional purposes.

---

**Phase 3: Draft → Self-Review → Finalize**

- Write a full draft following the planned structure, maintaining consistent age-appropriate tone throughout.
- Self-review against the checklist: all eight components present, language appropriate for 8-year-olds, no unsupported abstract notation, smooth transitions between sections.
- Revise for clarity, pacing, and classroom usability, then produce the final polished lesson segment.
