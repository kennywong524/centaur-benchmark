## Grade 3 Lesson Segment  
**Topic:** Improper Fractions & Mixed Numbers  
**Age:** 8‑year‑olds (Grade 3)  
**Length:** ≈ 35 minutes  

---

### 1. Learning Objective  
> **By the end of the lesson, students will be able to**  
> 1. Identify when a fraction is “too big” for one whole (an **improper fraction**).  
> 2. Turn that “too‑big” fraction into a **mixed number** (a whole number + a proper fraction).  
> 3. Explain the idea in their own words and show the conversion with pictures.

---

### 2. Materials (teacher & students)

| Teacher | Students |
|--------|----------|
| • Large poster of a pizza split into equal slices (e.g., 4‑slice pizza)  <br>• Two‑sided “fraction‑strip” cards (one side shows a whole, the other side shows 1⁄2, 1⁄3, 1⁄4, 1⁄5) <br>• Whiteboard & markers <br>• Sticky‑note “fraction‑to‑mixed‑number” worksheet (1‑2 problems) | • One “fraction‑strip” card <br>• Small whiteboard or paper & pencil <br>• Stickers for quick‑check (thumbs‑up / thumbs‑down cards) |

---

### 3. Warm‑Up (3 min)

**Teacher:** “Who can tell me what a fraction looks like? Show me with your fingers.”  
- Students hold up pictures like **½** (one finger up) or **⅓** (one finger out of three).  

**Quick link to today:** “Sometimes a fraction is **bigger than one whole**—like having more pizza slices than a whole pizza! That’s what we’ll explore today.”

---

### 4. Simple Explanation (5 min)

1. **What is a proper fraction?**  
   *“A proper fraction is a piece that is **smaller** than a whole. If we cut a pizza into 4 equal slices, each slice is **¼** of the pizza. One slice is less than the whole pizza.”*  

2. **What is an improper fraction?**  
   *“An improper fraction is a piece that is **as big as** or **bigger than** a whole. Imagine we have **5⁄4** of a pizza. That means we have **one whole pizza** **plus** an extra slice.”*  

3. **Mixed number = whole + part**  
   *“We can write that same amount in a friendlier way: **1 and ¼**. That tells us we have **1 whole pizza** and **¼** of another pizza.”*  

> **Key Vocabulary (display on board with pictures):**  
> - *Proper fraction* – smaller than one whole  
> - *Improper fraction* – as big as or bigger than one whole  
> - *Mixed number* – whole number + proper fraction  

---

### 5. Analogy & Visual Story (4 min)

**Analogy:** *“Think of a backpack. A proper fraction is like a small snack that fits inside. An improper fraction is a snack that’s so big it fills the whole backpack **and** spills out onto the floor. The mixed number tells us how many full backpacks we have **plus** what’s left over on the floor.”*  

**Visual:** Draw a backpack on the board, fill it with 4 small squares (representing ¼ each). Show 5 squares → 1 full backpack + 1 extra square → “1 and ¼”.

---

### 6. Worked Examples (8 min)
