### *Understanding Improper Fractions and Mixed Numbers*
**Grade 3 | Math | Approximately 45 minutes**

## 🎯 LEARNING OBJECTIVE

By the end of this lesson, students will be able to:

> **Identify, represent, and convert between improper fractions and mixed numbers using pictures, words, and numbers.**

**In kid-friendly language:**
*"Today we're going to learn about fractions that are bigger than one whole thing — and how to write them two different ways!"*

---

## 🔔 HOOK — The Pizza Problem (5 minutes)

**[Display or draw on the board: 2 pizzas, each cut into 4 slices]**

Say to students:

> *"Okay friends, I have a very important question. It's lunchtime. 🎉 I ordered TWO pizzas for our class pizza party. Each pizza is cut into 4 equal slices. My friend Marcus ate 5 slices. Can someone show me on the board which slices Marcus ate?"*

**Call a volunteer to circle 5 slices across both pizzas.**

Ask:
- *"Did Marcus eat more than one whole pizza or less than one whole pizza?"* → **More than one!**
- *"How many slices did he eat out of each pizza?"* → **5 out of 4... wait, that's more than the whole pizza!**
- *"Is that even allowed in math?"* → **Yes! And today we're going to learn exactly how to write that."*

---

## 📖 DIRECT INSTRUCTION

### PART 1: What Is an Improper Fraction? (8 minutes)

**Write on the board:**

```
     5
    ───    ← This is called an IMPROPER FRACTION
     4
```

**Say:**
> *"Remember, the bottom number — the denominator — tells us how many equal pieces one whole thing is cut into. The top number — the numerator — tells us how many pieces we have."*

> *"In a regular fraction like ¾, we have FEWER pieces than we need to make a whole. But Marcus ate 5 pieces when each pizza only had 4. So his fraction is 5 fourths — and we write it like this."*

**Point to the fraction:**
> *"When the top number is BIGGER than or EQUAL TO the bottom number, we call it an **improper fraction**. Don't let the word 'improper' scare you — it just means the fraction is one whole or MORE than one whole. It's not bad or wrong — it's actually pretty cool!"*

---

### 🍕 THE PIZZA ANALOGY (Keep returning to this all lesson!)

**Draw this on the board or use printed visuals:**

```
PIZZA 1          PIZZA 2
┌────────┐       ┌────────┐
│ ██ ██  │       │ ██  ░░ │
│ ██ ██  │       │ ░░  ░░ │
└────────┘       └────────┘
  4/4 eaten        1/4 eaten
```

> *"Marcus ate ALL 4 slices of Pizza 1, plus 1 slice of Pizza 2. That's 5 slices total, and each pizza has 4 slices. So we write: **5/4**"*

**Anchor Statement — Say it together as a class:**
> *"When the top is bigger than the bottom, we have MORE than one whole!"* 🙌

---

### PART 2: What Is a Mixed Number? (8 minutes)

**Say:**
> *"Now here's the thing. Mathematicians like to have TWO ways to say the same thing — because sometimes one way makes more sense than the other."*

> *"Instead of saying Marcus ate **5/4** of a pizza, I could also say he ate **1 whole pizza AND 1 more slice**. That's another way to write the same amount!"*

**Write on the board:**

```
5         1
─  =  1  ─
4         4
     ↑
  This is called a MIXED NUMBER
  (a whole number MIXED with a fraction)
```

**Point and explain each part:**

```
    1  ← How many WHOLE pizzas
   ───
    1  ← How many EXTRA pieces
   ───
    4  ← How many pieces make one whole
```

```
IMPROPER FRACTION          MIXED NUMBER
