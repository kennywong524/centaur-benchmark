Lesson segment: Improper Fractions and Mixed Numbers
Grade 3 (about 8 years old) — 25–30 minutes

Learning objective
- I can explain what an improper fraction and a mixed number are.
- I can change an improper fraction into a mixed number by making whole groups and drawing them.

Materials
- Paper plates or paper circles (2 per pair)
- Scissors (one pair per student or pair)
- Colored pencils or markers
- Fraction strips or sets of rectangle pieces cut into halves, thirds, fourths (optional)
- Mini whiteboards or scrap paper and pencils
- (Optional) small counters like crayons, blocks, or chocolate squares

Hook (2 minutes)
Show a whole paper plate and ask:
- “If I cut this plate into 4 equal pieces and give you 5 pieces, how many whole plates do you have?”
Get quick answers (expect: 1 whole plate and 1 piece left). Say: “That idea—having more pieces than one whole—helps us understand improper fractions and mixed numbers.”
