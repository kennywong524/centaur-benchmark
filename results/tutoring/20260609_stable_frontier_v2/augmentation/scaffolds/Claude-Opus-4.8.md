# Grade 3 Lesson Segment: Improper Fractions & Mixed Numbers

**Time:** 30–35 minutes
**Topic:** Understanding improper fractions and mixed numbers using pizza!

---

## 🎯 Learning Objective

*Students will be able to:* Show that an improper fraction (like 5/4) is the same amount as a mixed number (like 1 and 1/4) using pictures and real objects.

**I can say it like this:** "I can show how a tricky fraction can also be written as a whole number plus a piece."

---

## 🍕 Simple Explanation

Sometimes we have **more than one whole thing** made of fractions.

- A **mixed number** tells us about **whole things AND extra pieces**.
  Example: *1 and 1/2 pizzas* = one whole pizza plus half a pizza.

- An **improper fraction** counts **ALL the pieces together**, even when there's enough to make a whole.
  Example: If a pizza is cut into 2 pieces and we have 3 pieces, that's *3/2*.

They can be **the same amount**, just written in two different ways!

---

## 🍫 Analogy: The Chocolate Bar

> "Imagine you have chocolate bars, and each bar is split into 4 squares. 
> If you have **6 squares**, that's **6/4** (counting every square). 
> But you could also say you have **1 whole bar and 2 leftover squares** — that's **1 and 2/4**. 
> Same chocolate, two ways to say it!"

*(Tip: Hold up a paper chocolate bar model while explaining.)*

---

## ✏️ Worked Examples

### Example 1 — From improper fraction to mixed number

**Show 5/4 with pictures:**

```
🟦🟦🟦🟦   🟦
[1 whole = 4/4]  [1 extra piece = 1/4]
```

- We have 5 pieces, and 4 pieces make a whole.
- 4 pieces = 1 whole. We have 1 piece left over.
- **5/4 = 1 and 1/4** ✅

### Example 2 — From mixed number to improper fraction

**Show 2 and 1/3:**

```
🟩🟩🟩   🟩🟩🟩   🟩
[3/3]      [3/3]    [1/3]
```

- Count ALL the pieces: 3 + 3 + 1 = 7 pieces.
- Each piece is a third, so we have **7/3**.
- **2 and 1/3 = 7/3** ✅

---

## ⚠️ Common Misconceptions (Watch For These!)

| Misconception | How to Fix It |
|---|---|
| "The bottom number changes too." | Show that the *size of each piece stays the same* — only count more pieces. |
| "5/4 must be wrong because the top is bigger." | Explain: a bigger top just means we have **more than one whole**. That's allowed! |
| Counting wholes as "1" instead of full pieces. | Always recount the pieces in each whole together. |
| Mixing up which number goes on top. | Remind: **top = how many pieces**, **bottom = how the whole is cut**. |
