**Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow**

**1. Requirements Check**
- Target audience: Grade 3 students (approx. 8 years old).
- Topic: Improper fractions and mixed numbers.
- Required elements: Learning objective, simple explanation, analogy, worked examples, common misconceptions, interactive activity, checks for understanding, and a short wrap-up.
- Tone/Style constraints: Age-appropriate language, classroom-ready format, and avoidance of abstract notation without visual support. 

**2. Plan & Structure**
- **Introduction:** Formulate a clear, student-friendly learning objective. Plan an engaging analogy that connects the concept to a familiar real-world scenario (e.g., sharing food).
- **Direct Instruction:** Outline a simple explanation defining both terms. Structure the worked examples to transition smoothly from visual representations to numerical forms.
- **Troubleshooting:** Identify 1-2 common misconceptions typical for this age group and plan how to address them gently.
- **Application:** Design an interactive activity that requires minimal setup and encourages active participation. 
- **Assessment & Conclusion:** Draft questions for the checks for understanding. Plan a concise wrap-up that summarizes the key takeaways and celebrates the students' effort.

**3. Draft -> Self-Review -> Finalize**
- *Draft:* Write the lesson segment following the planned structure. Ensure all explanations are simple, engaging, and heavily rely on described visual supports.
- *Self-Review:* Check against the constraints. Is the language suitable for an 8-year-old? Are all required elements (objective, analogy, misconceptions, activity, wrap-up) present? Is abstract notation avoided or properly supported by visual descriptions?
- *Finalize:* Polish the formatting for readability (e.g., using bolding for key terms, bullet points for steps) to ensure it is truly "classroom-ready" for a teacher to use.
