**Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow**

1) Requirements Check
- Confirm audience specifics: exact grade, typical attention span, language proficiency, class size, and any IEP/ELL considerations.
- Verify constraints: total time available for the segment, materials/tech accessible in-class and at home, school policies, curriculum standards to align with.
- Identify deliverables: one clear learning objective phrased in student-friendly terms, visual supports to create, one interactive activity, assessment/checks for understanding, and a brief wrap-up.
- Note stylistic rules: age-appropriate vocabulary, avoid introducing abstract notation without visuals, cultural sensitivity, and readability targets.

2) Plan & Structure
- Break the segment into timed parts: objective + hook, simple explanation with visual scaffolds, analogy (concrete and relatable), two worked examples (with visual steps), one common-misconceptions block, interactive task, checks for understanding, and wrap-up.
- Decide formats for visuals (manipulatives, drawings, slide images, printable handout) and what the teacher will say vs. show.
- Sketch the interactive activity: group/individual, materials needed, expected student responses, instructions, and differentiation options.
- Design quick formative checks (oral, signal, mini-worksheet) and criteria for success tied to the objective.

3) Draft -> Self-Review -> Finalize
- Draft the scripted segment and accompanying visuals/handouts; annotate teacher prompts and student-facing language.
- Run a self-review checklist: alignment with objective, age-appropriate wording, clarity of visuals, explicit attention to common misconceptions, feasible timing, clear transitions, and assessment alignment.
- Revise based on checklist, prepare materials list and reproducibles, perform a brief rehearsal or peer check, finalize formatting for classroom use.
