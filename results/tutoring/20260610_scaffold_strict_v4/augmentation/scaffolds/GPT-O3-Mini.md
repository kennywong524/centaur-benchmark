Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow

Phase 1: Requirements Check
• Begin by carefully reviewing the assignment criteria, ensuring a full understanding of the teaching subject, student age range, and necessary content components. 
• Verify that all required elements—learning objectives, explanations, analogies, examples, misconceptions, activities, checks, and wrap-up—are clearly identified as targets to address.
• Confirm that the language must be age-appropriate and that any abstract concepts should be supported by concrete or visual-friendly methods.
• Identify any potential constraints or additional criteria regarding lesson structure or content style that need to be integrated in the final draft.

Phase 2: Plan & Structure
• Outline the lesson segment by listing all the required components in a logical sequence. 
• Develop a structure that balances educational content with interactive components, delineating sections for explanation, example, interactive activity, and assessment checkpoints.
• Plan how to integrate key elements, ensuring that each piece naturally leads to the next while maintaining clarity for the target audience.
• Consider strategies for addressing common misconceptions and ensure that the lesson structure allows flexibility for in-class adjustments.

Phase 3: Draft -> Self-Review -> Finalize
• Produce an initial draft based on the established outline, prioritizing clarity and simplicity.
• Conduct a thorough self-review to check that all elements are included, the language is appropriate, and the flow is engaging.
• Revise repeatedly, ensuring consistent adherence to requirements and project goals before the final compilation.
• Finalize the lesson segment with clear annotations as needed for visual aids or interactive remarks, ensuring the final product is coherent and classroom-ready.
