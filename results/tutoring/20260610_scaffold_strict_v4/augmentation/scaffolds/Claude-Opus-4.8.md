**Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow**

**Phase 1: Requirements Check**
Begin by listing every explicit deliverable named in the task and confirm each component is accounted for. Identify the audience age and reading level, the constraints around language complexity, and the requirement to support notation with visuals. Note any pedagogical expectations (objective, explanation, analogy, examples, misconceptions, activity, checks, wrap-up). Flag anything ambiguous so you can make reasonable assumptions and state them.

**Phase 2: Plan & Structure**
Map the required components into a logical teaching sequence, moving from simple to complex. Decide the order in which concepts should be introduced and how each section connects to the stated objective. Allocate rough time or length proportions per segment. Plan where visual support belongs and how the analogy threads through later examples. Sketch how the interactive activity and checks for understanding will reinforce the explanation. Confirm the structure mirrors the full list of requested elements before writing.

**Phase 3: Draft → Self-Review → Finalize**
Draft each section in the planned order, keeping vocabulary and sentence length suitable for the target age. After drafting, review against your Phase 1 checklist: is every component present, is the language age-appropriate, is notation paired with visuals, and does each part align with the objective? Check for internal consistency, smooth transitions, and developmentally appropriate pacing. Revise anything too abstract or wordy. Finalize by confirming completeness, clarity, and tone, then format cleanly for classroom use.
