**Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow**

**1. Requirements Check**  
- Verify the target audience, grade level, and subject matter are clearly identified.  
- Confirm all mandatory components are listed (objective, explanation, analogy, examples, misconceptions, activity, checks for understanding, wrap‑up).  
- Ensure any style constraints are noted (age‑appropriate language, visual‑first presentation, avoidance of abstract symbols).  
- Note any optional preferences (tone, pacing, duration) and flag missing information that may need clarification before proceeding.

**2. Plan & Structure**  
- Outline a logical sequence that moves from simple to complex, aligning each required element with a specific segment of the lesson.  
- Allocate approximate time or length for each segment, keeping the overall flow suitable for the intended age group.  
- Decide on the type of visual support (e.g., drawings, manipulatives) that will accompany each explanation and example.  
- Choose an engaging analogy that maps directly onto everyday experiences of the learners.  
- Draft brief prompts for the interactive activity and the checks for understanding, ensuring they are actionable and measurable.  
- Sketch a concise wrap‑up that reinforces the objective without introducing new content.

**3. Draft → Self‑Review → Finalize**  
- Write the first draft following the planned structure, using clear, simple language and embedding visual cues where appropriate.  
- Conduct a self‑review checklist: Does each required element appear? Is the language age‑appropriate? Are visuals described for later creation? Are misconceptions addressed accurately?  
- Revise for coherence, brevity, and engagement; adjust pacing and wording as needed.  
- Perform a final proofread for consistency, spelling, and alignment with the original constraints, then prepare the polished lesson segment for delivery.
