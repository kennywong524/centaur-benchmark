**Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow**

1. Requirements Check  
Carefully review the task instructions to identify all specified components needed in the lesson segment, such as learning objectives, explanations, analogies, examples, misconceptions, activities, understanding checks, and a wrap-up. Confirm the target audience (Grade 3, 8-year-olds) and note the language and presentation constraints (age-appropriate, visual support, minimal abstract notation). Ensure understanding of the educational context and expectations for classroom readiness.

2. Plan & Structure  
Outline the lesson segment by sequencing each required element logically. Begin with the learning objective, followed by a simple explanation and analogy to introduce the concept. Plan to include clear, visual worked examples. Identify potential student misconceptions to address. Design an interactive, engaging activity suitable for the age group. Decide on methods for checking understanding throughout the lesson. Conclude with a concise wrap-up. Ensure each part aligns with the developmental level and maintains clarity and engagement.

3. Draft -> Self-Review -> Finalize  
Draft the full lesson segment, integrating all planned elements and maintaining age-appropriate language and visuals. After drafting, conduct a self-review to verify completeness, coherence, and adherence to all requirements. Check for clarity, logical flow, and suitability for 8-year-olds, revising as needed. Confirm that all constraints (such as avoiding abstract notation without visuals) are met. Finalize the lesson segment, ensuring it is ready for classroom delivery.
