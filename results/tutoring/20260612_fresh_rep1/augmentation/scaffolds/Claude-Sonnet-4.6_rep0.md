**Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow**

**Phase 1: Requirements Check**
Before writing anything, inventory every explicit deliverable listed in the task and confirm you understand each one. Create a mental checklist: learning objective, explanation, analogy, worked examples, misconceptions, interactive activity, comprehension checks, and wrap-up. Note the audience constraints (Grade 3, age 8, classroom-ready) and the language/visual-support requirement. Flag any ambiguities—e.g., how many worked examples, what kind of interactive activity format—and resolve them by defaulting to what is most practical for a typical classroom setting. Confirm the scope is a "segment," not a full lesson plan, so length and depth should be proportionate.

**Phase 2: Plan & Structure**
Map out a logical sequence for the segment before drafting. Decide the order of components so each builds on the previous one (objective → concept introduction → analogy → examples → misconceptions → activity → checks → wrap-up). Sketch a brief outline with section headers. Consider pacing and transitions between sections. Plan how visual support will be referenced or described in lieu of actual graphics. Decide on tone and vocabulary tier appropriate for the audience.

**Phase 3: Draft → Self-Review → Finalize**
Write a complete first draft following your outline. Then self-review against the original checklist: Are all eight components present? Is language age-appropriate throughout? Are abstract notations paired with described visuals? Does the sequence flow logically? Check for consistency in terminology and tone. Revise any gaps or mismatches, then produce the final, polished segment ready for classroom use.
