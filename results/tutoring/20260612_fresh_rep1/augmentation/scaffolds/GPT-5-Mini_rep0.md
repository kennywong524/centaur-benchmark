**Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow**

1. Requirements Check
- Confirm audience specifics (age, grade level, prior knowledge) and time available for the segment.
- List mandatory components to include: a single clear learning objective, simple explanation, age-appropriate analogy, worked examples, common misconceptions, an interactive activity, checks for understanding, and a short wrap-up.
- Note constraints: language level, visual support requirement, classroom materials available, accessibility needs, and assessment format.
- Identify deliverables: teacher script, student-facing visuals, material list, time estimates, and quick assessment items.

2. Plan & Structure
- Create a high-level sequence that orders components logically (objective → explanation → analogy → examples → activity → checks → wrap-up).
- Allocate approximate minutes for each component and ensure the total fits the time slot.
- Specify types of visuals and manipulatives to support non-abstract presentation; mark where visuals must appear.
- Outline one interactive activity structure (goal, materials, steps, expected student actions) and at least two short formative checks.
- Draft a brief teacher script tone guide: simple vocabulary, short sentences, prompts for questions, and cues for checking comprehension.
- Flag likely misconceptions to address and where to insert corrective phrasing.

3. Draft -> Self-Review -> Finalize
- Produce the initial draft materials (script, visuals, activity worksheet, quick checks).
- Run a review checklist: alignment with objective, age-appropriate wording, visual support present for abstract ideas, clear instructions, time feasibility, and inclusion of misconception fixes.
- Perform a peer or simulated-student read-through; note unclear spots or timing issues.
- Revise for clarity, brevity, and accessibility (large fonts, color contrast, alternatives for manipulatives).
- Finalize package: teacher script, printable visuals, materials list, timing, and quick assessment answer key.
