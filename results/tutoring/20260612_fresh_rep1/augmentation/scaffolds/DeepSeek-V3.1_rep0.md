**Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow**

**1. Requirements Check**
First, parse the task's explicit requirements. Identify all mandatory components listed (e.g., learning objective, explanation, analogy, examples, misconceptions, activity, checks, wrap-up). Verify the target audience constraints: language must be age-appropriate for 8-year-olds, abstract notation must be avoided, and visual support is required. Confirm the final output must be classroom-ready, meaning it is a complete, self-contained segment for immediate use by an educator.

**2. Plan & Structure**
Outline the logical flow of the lesson segment. Determine the optimal pedagogical sequence for the components, ensuring concepts build progressively from simple to more complex. Brainstorm a relatable central analogy suitable for the age group. Plan the interactive activity to be hands-on and collaborative. Structure the checks for understanding as simple, low-stakes questions. Decide on the format for the worked examples, ensuring they are visual and step-by-step.

**3. Draft -> Self-Review -> Finalize**
Compose a first draft following the outline. Use simple, clear, and engaging language throughout. After drafting, conduct a self-review against the initial requirements checklist. Ensure every mandated component is present and effectively addressed. Verify that all language and concepts are accessible to third graders and that visual aids are integrated. Refine the draft for clarity, conciseness, and flow before finalizing the segment.
