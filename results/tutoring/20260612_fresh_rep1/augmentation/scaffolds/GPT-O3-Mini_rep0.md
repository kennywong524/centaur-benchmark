Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow

Phase 1: Requirements Check
• Begin by thoroughly reviewing the task instructions and understanding the audience, context, and necessary components. 
• Verify all features that need to be addressed, such as the learning objective, clear explanation, analogy, worked examples, discussion of common misconceptions, interactive activity, checks for understanding, and wrap-up.
• Ensure awareness of the language and explanation constraints for the specified age group, noting the need for simple, concrete support rather than abstract notation.
• Double-check that all necessary elements are recognized before moving forward.

Phase 2: Plan & Structure
• Create an outline that maps out each required element in a logical sequence, ensuring an engaging progression for a Grade 3 lesson.
• Plan sections for introduction, detailed explanation, relatable analogy, and guided practice through examples.
• Identify spots in the structure where potential student misunderstandings might be addressed, and where interactive and reflective strategies can be incorporated.
• Organize the outline in a way that facilitates age-appropriate language, maintaining clarity and simplicity throughout the learning segment.

Phase 3: Draft -> Self-Review -> Finalize
• Generate a draft based solely on the structured outline, keeping language and content appropriate for the target age.
• Conduct a self-review focusing on clarity, coherence, and adherence to the defined requirements, ensuring the content remains engaging yet simple.
• Adjust the draft according to the self-review findings, finalizing a cohesive structure that aligns with all task constraints and objectives.
