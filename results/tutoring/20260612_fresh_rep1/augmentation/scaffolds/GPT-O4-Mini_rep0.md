**Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow**

1. Requirements Check  
- Carefully parse the assignment to extract every required component (e.g., objective, explanation, analogy, examples, misconceptions, activity, checks, wrap-up).  
- Identify the target audience characteristics (age, reading level, prior knowledge).  
- Note pedagogical constraints (visual support only, avoid abstract notation without visuals).  
- Confirm any formatting or style expectations (tone, language level, length).

2. Plan & Structure  
- Group required components into a logical teaching sequence (for example: objective → explanation → examples → activity → assessment → wrap-up).  
- Sketch brief section headings or slots for each component.  
- Decide on transitions and scaffolding to ensure smooth flow and age appropriateness.  
- Allocate approximate time or emphasis per section to balance depth and engagement.
