**Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow**

1. Requirements Check  
Carefully analyze the task instructions to identify all required lesson components: learning objective, explanation, analogy, worked examples, common misconceptions, interactive activity, checks for understanding, and wrap-up. Confirm the target audience (Grade 3, 8-year-olds), ensuring language and content are age-appropriate. Note constraints such as avoiding abstract notation and ensuring visual support. List out any specific inclusions or exclusions mandated by the prompt.

2. Plan & Structure  
Outline the sequence of the lesson segment, arranging components logically for smooth progression. Decide the order in which to introduce concepts (e.g., objective first, then explanation, analogy, examples, etc.). Determine how to scaffold each section to suit 8-year-olds, integrating visuals and interactive elements where necessary. Map out how to weave checks for understanding and address misconceptions. Prepare to use simple, clear language and select analogies relatable to the age group.

3. Draft -> Self-Review -> Finalize  
Compose the lesson segment following the plan, ensuring each required component is present and clearly labeled. Use concise, child-friendly language and incorporate visual cues or descriptions. After drafting, review for completeness, clarity, and adherence to all requirements (including age suitability and visual support). Revise to simplify explanations, enhance engagement, and correct any oversights. Finalize by ensuring the lesson flows well and meets all outlined criteria.
