**Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow**

**1. Requirements Check**  
- Verify the target audience (grade level, age range) and ensure the content matches their developmental stage.  
- Confirm all mandatory elements are listed (objective, explanation, analogy, examples, misconceptions, activity, checks for understanding, wrap‑up).  
- Identify any constraints on language, symbols, or visual aids that must be respected.  
- Note any formatting or length limits that could affect the final segment.  

**2. Plan & Structure**  
- Draft an outline that orders the required components in a logical flow for a short lesson segment.  
- Allocate approximate time or space to each part, keeping the overall segment concise and age‑appropriate.  
- Decide on the pedagogical approach for each element (e.g., storytelling for analogies, step‑by‑step walkthrough for examples).  
- Choose an interactive activity that aligns with the learning objective and can be executed quickly in a classroom setting.  
- Plan simple, visual‑friendly language and identify where supporting visuals will be needed, even if placeholders are used.  

**3. Draft → Self‑Review → Finalize**  
- Write the first draft following the outline, focusing on clarity, tone, and adherence to the requirements checklist.  
- Conduct a self‑review: check for completeness, age‑appropriate wording, logical transitions, and the absence of unnecessary abstract notation.  
- Revise based on the review, tightening language, ensuring each component is present, and confirming the segment is ready for classroom delivery.  
- Perform a final proofread for grammar, consistency, and compliance with any formatting guidelines before submission.
