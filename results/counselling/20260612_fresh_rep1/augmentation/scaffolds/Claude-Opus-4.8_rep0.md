**Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow**

**Phase 1 — Requirements Check**
Begin by parsing the task into its explicit components. Identify every required element (validation, cautious pattern identification, framework-based framing, concrete next steps, safety note, encouraging close) and note any constraints around tone, format, and scope. Flag the boundaries you must respect—for example, distinguishing supportive language from clinical diagnosis. List what "done" looks like before writing anything. Confirm the intended audience and the single-session framing.

**Phase 2 — Plan & Structure**
Decide the ordering and proportion of each required element so the response flows naturally rather than reading as a checklist. Sketch a brief skeleton mapping each requirement to a section. Plan transitions so empathy precedes interpretation, interpretation stays tentative, and practical steps feel actionable but not prescriptive. Consider where each named framework fits and how to keep language plain. Decide where the safety/limits note belongs and how to integrate it without alarming tone. Note placeholders for the closing so it reinforces the established themes.

**Phase 3 — Draft → Self-Review → Finalize**
Write a complete draft following your skeleton. Then review against the Phase 1 checklist: is each element present, balanced, and appropriately cautious? Check that nothing minimizes feelings, that no diagnostic claims appear, and that next steps are specific yet flexible. Verify the safety note and the hopeful close are both included. Refine word choice for warmth and clarity, trim redundancy, confirm consistent tone, and ensure the response stands as a coherent whole before finalizing.
