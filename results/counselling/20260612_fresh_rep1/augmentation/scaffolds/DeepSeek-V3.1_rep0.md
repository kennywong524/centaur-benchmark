**Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow**

1.  **Requirements Check**
    Systematically verify the task instructions against a checklist to ensure all six explicit content mandates are accounted for. Confirm the required tone (supportive, counseling-style) and format (single-session response). Identify any implicit constraints, such as maintaining a non-diagnostic, non-minimizing stance and using plain language for evidence-informed concepts.

2.  **Plan & Structure**
    Outline a logical flow for the response that sequentially incorporates each mandated element. Determine how to transition smoothly from one component (e.g., empathic validation) to the next (e.g., cautious pattern identification). Plan the integration of theoretical frameworks (CBT, motivational interviewing, positive psychology) into the prescribed content areas, ensuring they are translated into accessible language. Structure the "concrete next steps" to be actionable and discrete.

3.  **Draft -> Self-Review -> Finalize**
    Compose a draft adhering to the created outline. Perform a self-review against the initial requirements checklist to ensure no element is missing, overstated, or misaligned with the counseling tone. Scrutinize language for clarity, empathy, and consistency with the non-diagnostic, agency-reinforcing approach. Refine the draft into a cohesive, final response that meets all specified criteria.
