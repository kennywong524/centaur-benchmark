Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow

1. Requirements Check  
• Begin by carefully reviewing the detailed task instructions to identify all necessary components and tone elements.  
• Ensure clarity on the need for an empathic, supportive counseling style while acknowledging the client's emotional state, work anxieties, and life uncertainties.  
• Confirm which therapeutic approaches are to be represented (e.g., CBT, motivational interviewing, positive psychology) without overstepping into diagnostic territory.  
• Identify all required elements including validating feelings, discussing possible patterns cautiously, providing next steps, and including a safety note and encouraging close.

2. Plan & Structure  
• Delineate the response into clear sections that mirror the task elements, planning an introduction that empathetically validates the client’s feelings and contextualizes their emotional state.  
• Organize supportive commentary that hints at potential behavioral and cognitive patterns subtly and respectfully, while framing insights using evidence-based interventions in lay language.  
• Map out a sequence that includes actionable strategies for stress regulation, reframing thoughts, clarifying values, and setting near-term goals, ensuring seamless transitions between ideas.  
• Outline a concise safety/limits note and an uplifting conclusion that reinforces possibility and self-efficacy.

3. Draft -> Self-Review -> Finalize  
• Produce an initial draft based on the outlined sections, ensuring all required elements
