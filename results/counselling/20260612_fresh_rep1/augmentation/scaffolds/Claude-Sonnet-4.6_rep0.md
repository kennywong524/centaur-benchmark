**Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow**

---

**Phase 1: Requirements Check**

Before writing, carefully audit all six required components listed in the task. Confirm you understand the tone constraints (supportive, non-diagnostic, plain language) and the audience (a distressed individual in a single-session context). Note any tensions—for example, balancing pattern identification with avoiding clinical diagnosis, or being concrete without being prescriptive. Flag any component that might conflict with another and decide how you will navigate that tension before proceeding.

---

**Phase 2: Plan & Structure**

Map each of the six required components to a distinct section or natural transition point in your response. Decide on a logical sequence that mirrors how a real supportive conversation would flow (e.g., validation before pattern-naming, psychoeducation before action steps). Determine appropriate length and tone for each section. Identify which therapeutic frameworks you will draw on and how you will translate them into accessible, non-jargon language. Sketch a rough outline of transitions between sections so the response reads as cohesive rather than list-like.

---

**Phase 3: Draft → Self-Review → Finalize**

Write a full draft following your outline. Then conduct a structured self-review: check each of the six requirements against your draft, verify tone remains warm and non-pathologizing throughout, confirm no section inadvertently diagnoses or minimizes, and ensure the safety note and closing land appropriately. Revise any gaps or tone inconsistencies. Read the full response once more as if you are the client receiving it, then finalize.
