**Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow**

**1. Requirements Check**  
- Re‑read the prompt to confirm every mandatory element (e.g., empathic validation, pattern identification, evidence‑informed framing, concrete steps, safety note, encouraging close).  
- Note any stylistic constraints (single‑session, supportive counseling tone, plain‑language, non‑diagnostic language).  
- Verify length expectations and any formatting rules (ordered list, paragraph flow, etc.).  
- Flag any ambiguous terms (e.g., “possible patterns”) and decide on a brief internal definition to stay consistent.  

**2. Plan & Structure**  
- Sketch a high‑level outline that maps each required component to a logical sequence (e.g., opening empathy → validation → gentle pattern spotting → CBT/MI/positive‑psychology framing → actionable steps → safety disclaimer → hopeful closing).  
- Allocate approximate sentence or paragraph counts for each section to maintain balance and readability.  
- Identify the core techniques you will reference (CBT thought‑record, MI reflective listening, positive‑psychology strengths) and decide on plain‑language equivalents.  
- Prepare a checklist of tone criteria (warm, non‑judgmental, collaborative) and content checks (no diagnosis, no minimization, clear next steps).  

**3. Draft → Self‑Review → Finalize**  
- Write the first draft following the outline, ensuring each checklist item appears once and in the intended order.  
- Conduct a self‑review using the checklist: verify empathy depth, confirm pattern language is cautious, ensure framing is evidence‑based yet accessible, and that safety language is brief and appropriate.  
- Edit for flow, conciseness, and tone consistency; remove any inadvertent jargon or overly clinical phrasing.  
- Perform a final read‑through to catch typos, verify compliance with all constraints, and confirm the closing leaves the client feeling empowered.  
- Submit the polished response.
