**Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow**

1. Requirements Check  
   - Review the full task prompt and list each deliverable component.  
   - Highlight mandatory elements (e.g., empathic tone, cautious pattern identification, evidence-informed frameworks, concrete steps, safety note, hopeful close).  
   - Note constraints (single session style, plain language, non-diagnostic stance, professional referral).  
   - Confirm style requirements (supportive counseling tone, brevity, clarity).

2. Plan & Structure  
   - Sketch a high-level outline mapping each required element to a separate section.  
     • Opening: empathic validation  
     • Middle: pattern exploration + evidence-informed framing  
     • Action: concrete strategies  
     • Safety note  
     • Closing: encouragement and agency  
   - Determine logical flow and transitions between sections.  
   - Assign approximate word counts or proportions to ensure balanced coverage.  
   - Identify where to weave in CBT, motivational interviewing, and positive psychology principles without jargon.  
   - Plan signals for non-judgmental language and prompts for professional support.

3. Draft → Self-Review → Finalize  
   - Draft: Write each section according to the outline, focusing on tone and required components.  
   - Self-Review: Use a checklist of the six mandated elements and stylistic guidelines to verify completeness.  
   - Edit for coherence, plain language, and non-diagnostic framing.  
   - Finalize: Ensure the response is polished, concise, and meets all constraints before submission.
