**Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow**

**1. Requirements Check**
- Confirm the output is a single-session supportive counseling-style response.
- Ensure the tone is empathic and validating, avoiding any minimization of the client's emotional exhaustion, anxiety, or uncertainty.
- Verify the inclusion of a cautious exploration of potential patterns (e.g., burnout, perfectionism) without making formal clinical diagnoses.
- Check that the framing uses evidence-informed concepts (CBT, motivational interviewing, positive psychology) translated into plain, accessible language.
- Confirm the presence of concrete next steps covering stress regulation, thought reframing, values clarification, and near-term goal setting.
- Ensure a safety/limits note is included, advising professional help for severe distress.
- Check for an encouraging closing statement that emphasizes the client's agency and hope.

**2. Plan & Structure**
- **Opening:** Draft an empathic, validating statement acknowledging the client's feelings of being drained, anxious, and uncertain.
- **Exploration:** Introduce gentle observations about possible underlying patterns (e.g., burnout, value mismatch) using non-diagnostic language.
- **Framework Application:** Integrate principles from CBT, motivational interviewing, or positive psychology to help frame the client's experience and potential for change.
- **Actionable Steps:** Outline clear, manageable strategies for regulating stress, reframing unhelpful thoughts, clarifying personal values, and setting small, immediate goals.
- **Safety Note & Closing:** Insert a brief disclaimer about seeking professional support if needed, followed by an empowering and hopeful conclusion.

**3. Draft -> Self-Review -> Finalize**
- Draft the response following the structured sections, maintaining a warm, supportive, and professional tone throughout.
- Review the draft against all constraints: Is the language plain and non-diagnostic? Are the actionable steps concrete? Is the safety note present?
- Refine the transitions between empathy, exploration, and action to ensure a natural, conversational flow.
- Finalize the text for delivery.
