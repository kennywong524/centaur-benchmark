**Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow**

**Phase 1 — Requirements Check**
Begin by parsing the task into its explicit deliverables and constraints. Identify every required component the response must contain, the intended tone, the format (single-session, supportive), and any boundaries (e.g., non-diagnostic stance, safety/limits expectations). Note which elements are mandatory versus stylistic. List any ambiguities and resolve them with reasonable, conservative assumptions. Confirm you understand who the audience is and what "done" looks like before writing anything.

**Phase 2 — Plan & Structure**
Map each required element to a logical section or beat so nothing is dropped. Decide a sequence that flows naturally for the reader—typically opening, middle development, and close. For each section, jot a one-line intention (purpose, not content). Plan how to honor cautionary constraints throughout (tentative language, scope limits) and where the safety note and closing belong. Check that the structure balances coverage with brevity and that transitions will feel cohesive rather than checklist-like.

**Phase 3 — Draft → Self-Review → Finalize**
Write a full draft following your plan, keeping tone and constraints consistent. Then self-review against the Phase 1 requirement list: confirm each component is present, the tone matches, prohibited moves are avoided, and the framing stays within stated limits. Check for clarity, accuracy of approach, appropriate hedging, and a coherent, encouraging ending. Trim redundancy, smooth transitions, and verify length is appropriate. Make final edits, then confirm the response fully satisfies the task before delivering.
