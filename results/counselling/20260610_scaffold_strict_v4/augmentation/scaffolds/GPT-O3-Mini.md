Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow

Phase 1: Requirements Check
• Begin by reviewing all task constraints and goals. Confirm that the response must be supportive, empathetic, and clearly structured without providing any diagnoses or definitive conclusions.
• Identify the target tone, ensuring that the empathy and validation of the client's emotional experiences are prioritized while avoiding technical or overly complex language.
• Verify that the integration of evidence-informed approaches is necessary, and note the essential themes of stress regulation, cognitive reframing, values clarification, and goal setting alongside a brief safety note.
• Ensure that the plan accounts for a closing message that reinforces hope and the client’s inherent agency.

Phase 2: Plan & Structure
• Outline the major components that will structure the counseling response. Think of how to sequence empathic acknowledgment, identification of patterns, and CBT, motivational interviewing, or positive psychology framing.
• Determine where to include actionable next steps and incorporate clear, supportive instructions for the client.
• Plan logical transitions between these sections to maintain a cohesive flow while respecting the boundaries of not offering direct counseling.
• Keep a checklist of required themes and neutral language markers to ensure consistency with evidence-informed principles.

Phase 3: Draft -> Self-Review -> Finalize
• Develop an initial draft following the planned structure without finalizing any specific recommendations.
• Conduct a self-review to ensure that all sections meet the task criteria, maintain sensitivity, and adhere strictly to the outlined constraints.
• Finalize the response by refining the language for clarity and empathetic tone, while ensuring the response remains supportive and non-diagnostic.
