**Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow**

**1. Requirements Check**  
- Verify that the prompt calls for a single‑session, supportive counseling style response.  
- Confirm the required elements: empathetic validation, cautious pattern spotting, evidence‑based framing, actionable steps, a safety/limits disclaimer, and an uplifting closing.  
- Ensure tone aligns with a non‑clinical, client‑centered voice and that language remains plain, accessible, and free of diagnostic labels.  
- Note any constraints on length, format, or the inclusion of specific therapeutic models (e.g., CBT, motivational interviewing, positive psychology).  

**2. Plan & Structure**  
- Outline a logical flow that moves the client from feeling heard to gaining insight and then to concrete next actions.  
- Allocate brief sections for each required component, deciding the order that feels most natural (e.g., start with validation, then pattern reflection, followed by evidence‑based framing, actionable steps, safety note, and close).  
- Draft concise bullet‑point or short‑paragraph templates for each section, ensuring each fulfills its purpose without overlap.  
- Identify where to embed gentle language that signals possibilities rather than diagnoses, and where to insert plain‑language explanations of therapeutic concepts.  

**3. Draft → Self‑Review → Finalize**  
- Write the full response using the planned structure, keeping sentences short and supportive.  
- Conduct a self‑review checklist: (a) all required elements present? (b) tone empathetic and non‑judgmental? (c) therapeutic references explained simply? (d) actionable steps realistic and specific? (e) safety disclaimer clear and brief? (f) closing reinforces agency and hope?  
- Revise any ambiguous phrasing, eliminate jargon, and tighten word count to meet any length limits.  
- Perform a final proofread for grammar, flow, and adherence to the original constraints before submitting.
