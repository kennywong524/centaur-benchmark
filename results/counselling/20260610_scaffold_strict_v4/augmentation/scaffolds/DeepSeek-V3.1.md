**Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow**

1.  **Requirements Check**
    Systematically verify the task instructions against the response draft to ensure all six explicit content requirements are addressed. Confirm that the tone remains consistently supportive and counseling-appropriate, avoiding any language that could be construed as a formal diagnosis. Ensure all evidence-informed frameworks are translated into plain, accessible language for the client.

2.  **Plan & Structure**
    Outline a logical flow for the single-session response, beginning with establishing rapport and validation. Plan the integration of the required elements, ensuring cautious pattern identification leads naturally into the evidence-informed framing. Structure the concrete next steps to be actionable and directly tied to the previously mentioned patterns and frameworks. Allocate space for the required safety note and a closing that emphasizes client agency.

3.  **Draft -> Self-Review -> Finalize**
    Compose the draft according to the outlined structure. In the self-review, critically assess whether the language is consistently empathic and non-minimizing. Check that all technical terms from psychological modalities are adequately simplified and that the suggested next steps are genuinely concrete. Ensure the safety note is present and appropriately positioned. Finalize the response by refining language for clarity, warmth, and encouragement.
