**Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow**

1) Requirements Check
- Read the prompt closely and extract mandatory elements to include (empathic validation, cautious pattern identification, evidence-informed framing, concrete next steps across specified domains, safety/limits note, encouraging close).
- Note style constraints: single-session supportive counseling tone, plain language, non-diagnostic wording, concise and actionable.
- Identify scope limits (do not provide long-term therapy, formal diagnosis, or crisis management beyond referral prompts).
- Flag any missing constraints (word/character limits, audience features) before proceeding.

2) Plan & Structure
- Decide a clear sequence (e.g., brief empathic opening → gentle pattern exploration → concise evidence-informed framing → concrete, manageable next steps grouped by domain → safety/limits note → encouraging close).
- Allocate approximate space/time for each section to keep response balanced.
- Choose language strategies: reflective statements, validation, tentative phrasing for patterns, simple explanations of relevant approaches without jargon, concrete action suggestions framed as options.
- Build transition sentences to keep flow and maintain supportive tone.
- Prepare a short checklist of what counts as “concrete next steps” and what should remain high-level so the reply stays session-appropriate.

3) Draft -> Self-Review -> Finalize
- Draft the response following the plan, using plain, empathetic language and non-diagnostic phrasing.
- Run the checklist: confirm inclusion of each required component, verify tone, ensure safety/limits wording is present, and check that suggested steps are realistic and within single-session scope.
- Edit for clarity, brevity, and warmth; remove any clinical claims or definitive diagnoses.
- Finalize by doing a final read for coherence and confirm the close reinforces agency and hope.
