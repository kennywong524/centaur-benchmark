**Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow**

**Phase 1: Requirements Check**
- Verify the persona: Single-session supportive counseling style.
- Confirm the target audience: A client experiencing emotional exhaustion, work anxiety, and life uncertainty.
- Ensure all six required elements are accounted for: Empathic validation, cautious pattern identification (no diagnosing), evidence-informed framing (CBT, MI, Positive Psychology in plain language), concrete next steps (regulation, reframing, values, goals), safety/limits note, and an encouraging closing.
- Check tone constraints: Supportive, validating, non-minimizing, and empowering.

**Phase 2: Plan & Structure**
- **Opening:** Plan a warm, validating introduction that acknowledges the emotional weight of the client's experience.
- **Exploration & Framing:** Outline how to gently introduce potential patterns (e.g., burnout, value mismatch) using plain-language concepts from the specified therapeutic frameworks.
- **Actionable Steps:** Structure the intervention section to cover the four required areas: stress regulation, cognitive reframing, values clarification, and near-term goals. Ensure these are practical and accessible.
- **Closing & Safety:** Draft the placement of the safety/limits note (often best near the end) and plan a final statement that reinforces hope and the client's agency.

**Phase 3: Draft -> Self-Review -> Finalize**
- Draft the response following the structured outline, maintaining a compassionate and professional tone throughout.
- Review the draft against the requirements: Is the validation genuine? Are the patterns suggested without crossing into diagnosis? Are the therapeutic concepts translated into accessible language? Are all four concrete steps included? Is the safety note present?
- Refine the language to ensure smooth transitions between empathy, psychoeducation, and action planning. Finalize the response.
