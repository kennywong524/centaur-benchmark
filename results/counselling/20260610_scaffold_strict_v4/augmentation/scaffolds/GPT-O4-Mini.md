**Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow**

1. Requirements Check  
   • Carefully review the user’s request to identify all explicit deliverables and stylistic requirements.  
   • Note constraints such as tone, structure, and any professional or ethical boundaries.  
   • Flag any ambiguities or potential conflicts and plan to resolve them before drafting.

2. Plan & Structure  
   • Outline a logical flow that accommodates each required component in a coherent sequence.  
   • Decide on section headings or transition markers to guide the reader smoothly from one idea to the next.  
   • Allocate space proportionally, ensuring empathy and validation lead into reflection, then practical guidance, and finally closure.  
   • Ensure balance between evidence-informed principles and accessible language.

3. Draft → Self-Review → Finalize  
   • Draft the full response, focusing on voice consistency and alignment with the identified requirements.  
   • Conduct a self-review checklist:  
     – Verify inclusion of each needed element without overstepping boundaries.  
     – Confirm tone remains supportive and nonjudgmental.  
     – Check clarity, coherence, and plain-language usage.  
   • Refine wording based on the checklist, correct any inconsistencies, and tighten transitions.  
   • Perform a final read-through for flow and completeness, then prepare the response for delivery.
