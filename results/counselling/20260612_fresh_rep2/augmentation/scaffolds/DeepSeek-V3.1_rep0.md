**Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow**

1.  **Requirements Check**
    Systematically verify the task instructions against the provided response. Confirm the response will contain all six mandated components. Ensure the tone remains supportive and counseling-appropriate, avoiding any diagnostic or prescriptive language. Check that all theoretical frameworks (e.g., CBT) are translated into plain language and that suggested steps are concrete and actionable.

2.  **Plan & Structure**
    Outline a logical flow for the single-session response. Begin by mapping the required elements to a natural conversational sequence: opening with validation, moving to pattern identification, then introducing frameworks, followed by actionable steps, and concluding with a safety note and encouraging close. Allocate emphasis to ensure the response feels balanced and client-centered, not like a mechanical checklist.

3.  **Draft -> Self-Review -> Finalize**
    Compose a draft adhering to the structure. Then, conduct a strict self-review against the initial requirements checklist. Scrutinize language for empathy, neutrality, and avoidance of minimization or diagnosis. Ensure all technical concepts are explained accessibly and that next steps are practical. Verify the safety note is present and appropriately cautious. Finalize the text to ensure it is cohesive, hopeful, and reinforces client agency.
