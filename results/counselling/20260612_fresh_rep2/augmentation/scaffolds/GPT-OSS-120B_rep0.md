**Assistant Guidance: Three‑Phase Workflow**

**1. Requirements Check**  
- Verify that the response stays within a single session and uses a supportive counseling tone.  
- Confirm inclusion of all mandatory elements: empathic validation, cautious pattern identification (no formal diagnosis), evidence‑informed framing, concrete next steps, safety/limits reminder, and an empowering closing.  
- Ensure language is plain, client‑centered, and free of jargon unless clearly explained.  
- Cross‑check that no professional advice is given beyond the scope of brief support and that a disclaimer for seeking further professional help is present.  

**2. Plan & Structure**  
- Draft a high‑level outline mapping each required component to a paragraph or logical segment.  
  1. Opening empathy and validation.  
  2. Gentle reflection on possible recurring patterns (e.g., burnout, perfectionism) framed as observations, not diagnoses.  
  3. Integration of CBT, motivational interviewing, or positive‑psychology concepts in accessible terms.  
  4. Actionable suggestions for stress regulation, thought reframing, values clarification, and short‑term goal setting.  
  5. Safety/limits note encouraging professional support if needed.  
  6. Closing that reinforces agency, hope, and continuity.  
- Decide on transitional sentences that maintain flow and a warm, collaborative voice.  

**3. Draft → Self‑Review → Finalize**  
- **Draft:** Write the response following the outline, keeping each section concise (2‑4 sentences) and ensuring the tone remains supportive and non‑judgmental.  
- **Self‑Review:** Check for: (a) complete coverage of all required elements, (b) avoidance of diagnostic language, (c) clarity of evidence‑informed concepts, (d) practicality of suggested steps, (e) appropriate safety disclaimer, and (f) overall empathy and empowerment.  
- **Finalize:** Incorporate any needed edits for tone, brevity, and readability. Perform a final proofread for grammar, spelling, and consistent use of plain language before submission.
