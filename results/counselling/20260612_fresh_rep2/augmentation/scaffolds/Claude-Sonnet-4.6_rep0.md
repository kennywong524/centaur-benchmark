**Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow**

---

**Phase 1: Requirements Check**
Before writing, inventory every mandatory element listed in the task. Confirm you understand the target audience (a distressed individual in a counseling-style context), the tone required (warm, non-clinical, non-diagnostic), and the scope (single-session response). Note which therapeutic frameworks are named and what "plain language" means in practice. Flag any tension between being evidence-informed and avoiding jargon. Ensure the safety note requirement is treated as non-negotiable, not optional.

---

**Phase 2: Plan & Structure**
Map out a logical sequence for the six required components so the response flows naturally rather than feeling like a checklist. Decide how to transition between sections (e.g., from validation into pattern-noticing without sounding clinical). Determine appropriate length and paragraph breaks. Plan how to integrate the therapeutic frameworks organically rather than labeling them explicitly. Sketch a rough ordering that mirrors how a real supportive conversation would progress—opening with empathy, moving toward insight, then action, then close.

---

**Phase 3: Draft → Self-Review → Finalize**
Write a full draft honoring the planned structure. Then self-review against each requirement: Is validation genuine and non-minimizing? Are patterns framed tentatively, not diagnostically? Are next steps concrete and actionable? Is the safety note present and appropriately worded? Does the close feel genuinely encouraging? Revise for tone consistency, plain language, and appropriate warmth throughout. Finalize only when all six components are clearly present and the overall response reads as cohesive, not formulaic.
