**Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow**

**1. Requirements Check**
Begin by parsing the task into its explicit components and confirm how many distinct elements the response must contain. Note constraints on tone (supportive, non-diagnostic), required frameworks to reference, and any boundary or safety expectations. Identify the intended format—a single, cohesive response rather than a list—and the implied audience. Flag anything that must be handled cautiously, such as language that avoids labeling or overstating certainty. Make a checklist you can return to later.

**2. Plan & Structure**
Decide on a logical flow that moves naturally from opening connection through observation, framing, actionable guidance, limits, and closing. Map each required element to a place in that flow so nothing is dropped. Consider proportion: how much space each section deserves relative to its importance. Plan transitions so the piece reads as one continuous response, not disconnected parts. Decide where cautionary or boundary language fits most naturally without disrupting tone. Keep the framing approachable and jargon-free.

**3. Draft → Self-Review → Finalize**
Write a complete first draft following your structure, prioritizing warmth and clarity. Then review against your requirements checklist: confirm every element is present, the tone stays validating and non-diagnostic, frameworks are explained plainly, next steps are concrete and actionable, and a limits/safety note appears. Check for minimizing language, overpromising, or accidental diagnosis. Trim redundancy, smooth transitions, and verify the close reinforces agency and hope. Finalize once all checklist items are confirmed.
