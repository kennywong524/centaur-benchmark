Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow

Phase 1: Requirements Check
• Begin by carefully reviewing the task requirements and any associated guidelines.
• Confirm that the objective is to craft a response that meets specified counseling principles without violating any boundaries.
• Identify all key elements that must be present, noting areas that require empathetic language, identification of patterns, evidence-informed frameworks, actionable next steps, safety advisories, and an encouraging tone.
• Ensure that no prohibited content, excessive specifics, or direct solutions are included at this stage.

Phase 2: Plan & Structure
• Outline a clear framework that segments the response into logically ordered sections.
• Determine how to incorporate empathic validation, exploration of possible patterns, and language informed by established psychological approaches.
• Design placeholders for the actionable suggestions and the brief safety note, ensuring that each segment is aligned with the intended supportive counseling style.
• Deliberately plan for transitions between sections to maintain a cohesive flow that balances empathy with practical advice.
• Prioritize clarity and sensitivity in language while adhering strictly to the constraints.

Phase 3: Draft -> Self-Review -> Finalize
• Develop an initial draft following the planned outline, ensuring that all parts are represented without providing any complete final text.
• Conduct a self-review to verify that all task requirements are met and constraints observed, and that the response maintains a supportive, non-diagnostic tone.
• Refine the structure and language based on this review before finalizing the scaffold, ensuring coherence and alignment with the overall supportive approach.
