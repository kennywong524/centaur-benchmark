**Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow**

**Phase 1: Requirements Check**
- Confirm the tone: supportive, counseling-style, empathic, and non-diagnostic.
- Verify the required elements: empathic validation, cautious pattern identification (burnout, perfectionism, etc.), evidence-informed framing (CBT, MI, positive psychology in plain language), concrete next steps (stress regulation, reframing, values, goal setting), a safety/limits note, and an encouraging close.
- Ensure the response is structured as a single-session response directed to a client experiencing emotional drain, work anxiety, and life uncertainty.

**Phase 2: Plan & Structure**
- **Introduction:** Plan an opening that focuses purely on empathic validation, acknowledging the heavy emotional load and uncertainty the client is carrying.
- **Exploration/Pattern Identification:** Outline a section to gently suggest potential underlying patterns (e.g., burnout or value misalignment) using tentative, non-diagnostic language.
- **Evidence-Informed Framing:** Decide how to integrate concepts from CBT, MI, or positive psychology seamlessly into the conversation to normalize their experience.
- **Actionable Steps:** Structure a clear, manageable list of concrete strategies addressing stress regulation, cognitive reframing, values clarification, and small goal setting.
- **Conclusion & Safety:** Draft a closing section that includes the required safety/limits disclaimer and ends on a hopeful, empowering note reinforcing the client's agency.

**Phase 3: Draft -> Self-Review -> Finalize**
- Draft the response following the planned structure, ensuring a warm, professional, and accessible tone.
- Self-Review against the constraints: Is the validation genuine? Are the patterns identified without diagnosing? Is the theoretical framing in plain language? Are all four concrete next step categories covered? Is the safety note present?
- Finalize the text for flow, clarity, and emotional resonance.
