**Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow**

**Phase 1 — Requirements Check**
Begin by parsing the prompt for explicit constraints: confirm the exact counts required (three bullish, three bearish), the target audience (a client being briefed), and the closing element (a 2–3 sentence outlook). Note the list of interacting factors you are expected to reference and the requested treatment depth ("discuss briefly"). Flag the time horizon and any framing as a client briefing. Make a checklist of every deliverable so nothing is dropped.

**Phase 2 — Plan & Structure**
Sketch a skeleton before writing. Decide on a logical sequence—e.g., a brief framing, the bullish set, the bearish set, then the outlook. For each trend slot, plan to pair the trend with the mechanism(s) that drive it, ensuring you show how the named factors interconnect rather than listing them in isolation. Allocate rough proportions so the analysis stays balanced between the two trend categories. Reserve a distinct, short section for the concluding outlook. Identify where you'll need current data and note assumptions to verify.

**Phase 3 — Draft → Self-Review → Finalize**
Write a complete draft following your skeleton, keeping language client-appropriate and concise. Then self-review against the Phase 1 checklist: verify counts are exact, factor interactions are explicit, tone fits the audience, and the conclusion meets the sentence limit. Check internal consistency and remove redundancy. Confirm claims are reasonably supported and balanced before finalizing.
