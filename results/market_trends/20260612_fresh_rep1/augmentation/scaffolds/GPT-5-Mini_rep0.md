**Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow**

1) Requirements Check
- Confirm the deliverable: a client-facing briefing on the U.S. energy market in 2026 that includes exactly three bullish trends and exactly three bearish trends affecting natural gas prices, a short discussion of interacting factors, and a 2–3 sentence overall outlook for investors/companies.
- Identify the audience, desired tone, length limit, and any regulatory or compliance constraints (e.g., avoid personalized investment advice; include disclaimers if required).
- List required source types (official agencies, market reports, reputable energy analysts) and note freshness cutoff for data. Flag any translations, visuals, or citations needed.

2) Plan & Structure
- Create a clear outline: brief intro (context), three bullish trend entries, three bearish trend entries, interaction section (supply/demand/weather/storage/LNG/infrastructure/policy), and a 2–3 sentence conclusion for investors.
- For each trend slot, set a targeted sentence count (e.g., 2–3 sentences) and a single-line headline. Reserve a short paragraph to explain causal links and confidence level.
- Allocate word budget per section and order content for narrative flow: trends → interactions → synthesis → investor outlook.

3) Draft -> Self-Review -> Finalize
- Draft each section to the planned length, using neutral, client-appropriate language; avoid recommendations that imply personalized advice.
- Self-review checklist: verify exactly three bullish and three bearish items; ensure interactions explicitly reference the required factors; check internal consistency and no unsupported claims; confirm citation attribution.
- Finalize by polishing tone, adding disclaimers, formatting for the client (headlines, bullets), and delivering within the agreed word/length constraints.
