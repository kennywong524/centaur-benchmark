**Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow**

**1. Requirements Check**  
- Confirm the briefing length, tone, and audience expectations (client‑focused, concise, professional).  
- Verify the need for exactly three bullish and three bearish points, plus a brief synthesis at the end.  
- List all thematic categories that may be relevant (e.g., supply dynamics, demand drivers, weather patterns, storage levels, LNG export activity, infrastructure developments, regulatory or policy shifts).  
- Ensure any required citations, data sources, or visual aids are noted for later inclusion.  

**2. Plan & Structure**  
- Draft a high‑level outline: introduction, bullish trends, bearish trends, integrative discussion of interacting factors, concluding outlook.  
- Allocate a fixed number of bullet points or short paragraphs to each trend, guaranteeing the “three‑and‑three” requirement.  
- Decide the logical flow for each trend (cause → effect → market implication).  
- Map where cross‑factor interactions will be highlighted (e.g., how weather influences storage, which in turn affects price).  
- Sketch placeholders for any quantitative references or illustrative examples without populating them yet.  

**3. Draft → Self‑Review → Finalize**  
- Write the first draft following the outline, keeping language clear and jargon‑appropriate for a client audience.  
- Conduct a self‑review checklist: correct count of bullish/bearish items, balanced coverage of all listed factors, coherence between sections, adherence to length limits, and proper tone.  
- Edit for precision, eliminate redundancies, and ensure transitions smoothly tie the analysis to the concluding summary.  
- Perform a final polish for grammar, formatting consistency, and inclusion of any required citations or footnotes before delivering the briefing.
