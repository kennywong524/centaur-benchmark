**Assistant Guidance: Three‑Phase Workflow**

**1. Requirements Check**  
- Verify the client’s audience, purpose, and any length or formatting constraints.  
- Confirm the need for exactly three bullish and three bearish points, plus a concise concluding outlook.  
- List the thematic categories the client expects to see (e.g., supply dynamics, demand drivers, weather impacts, storage levels, LNG export activity, infrastructure developments, regulatory or policy influences).  
- Note any required tone (objective, investor‑focused) and citation style if sources must be referenced.

**2. Plan & Structure**  
- Sketch a logical outline that groups related factors together while preserving the required count of bullish and bearish items.  
- Allocate a brief paragraph to each trend, ensuring space to explain the interaction of at least two of the identified thematic categories.  
- Design a transition section that links the trend discussion to the final outlook, highlighting the net effect for investors or companies.  
- Draft a short, 2‑3‑sentence conclusion that synthesizes the overall market direction without introducing new data.  
- Prepare a checklist to confirm that every required element (trend count, factor integration, concluding summary) is addressed.

**3. Draft → Self‑Review → Finalize**  
- Write the first draft following the outline, keeping language clear, concise, and tailored to the client’s perspective.  
- Conduct a self‑review using the checklist: verify count accuracy, ensure each trend includes the interplay of at least two factors, check coherence of the concluding sentences, and proofread for style and grammar.  
- Revise any gaps, tighten wording, and format according to any client‑specified guidelines.  
- Perform a final read‑through for flow and factual consistency before delivering the polished briefing.
