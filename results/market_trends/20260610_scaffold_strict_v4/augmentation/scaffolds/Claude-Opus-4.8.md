**Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow**

**Phase 1 — Requirements Check**
Begin by parsing the prompt into explicit deliverables. Note the exact counts required (three bullish, three bearish trends) and verify you understand the persona (briefing a client) and the target audience (investors or companies). List the factor categories the task asks you to weave in, and confirm the required closing element and its length constraint. Flag any ambiguities and resolve them with reasonable assumptions before writing.

**Phase 2 — Plan & Structure**
Sketch an outline that maps each required component to a section. Decide on an order—e.g., framing, bullish set, bearish set, interaction discussion, summary. For each trend slot, plan to show how the named factors connect rather than listing them in isolation. Choose a register suited to a client briefing: concise, professional, decision-relevant. Plan placeholders for where evidence or reasoning belongs, and ensure balance so neither side dominates. Decide how to keep the conclusion within its sentence limit.

**Phase 3 — Draft → Self-Review → Finalize**
Write a first pass following your outline without over-editing. Then review against Phase 1: confirm the exact trend counts, that each factor is addressed, that interactions are explained, and that the conclusion meets its length. Check tone consistency, remove redundancy, and verify claims are framed as analysis rather than certainty. Tighten language, confirm logical flow from trends to outlook, and finalize only after every explicit requirement is satisfied.
