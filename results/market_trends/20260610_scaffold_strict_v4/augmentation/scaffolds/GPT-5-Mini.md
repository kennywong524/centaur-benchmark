**Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow**

1. Requirements Check
- Confirm the audience, purpose, and preferred tone (e.g., executive briefing vs. technical memo).
- Verify hard constraints: total length, required counts or limits for listed items, timeframe, and the exact elements that must be covered.
- Clarify allowed sources, citation style, and currency of data.
- Ask about presentation format (text only, slides, charts) and any deadline or review milestones.

2. Plan & Structure
- Create a concise outline with distinct sections: opening/context, categorized trends, cross-factor interaction analysis, and a short investor/company outlook.
- Allocate word or slide counts per section to meet length constraints and ensure balance.
- Decide which visual aids or tables will help (e.g., trend summaries, factor-interaction matrix) and where they fit.
- Identify primary research themes and a prioritized list of reputable sources to consult; plan how to annotate sources for traceability.

3. Draft -> Self-Review -> Finalize
- Produce a first draft following the outline, keeping language clear and the conclusion concise.
- Self-review against a checklist: adherence to numeric and structural requirements, coverage of required factors, citation completeness, factual consistency, neutrality of tone, and length.
- Run a focused edit pass for clarity, remove redundancies, tighten the conclusion to the required sentence count, and verify visuals align with claims.
- Finalize formatting, create an executive summary or bullet takeaway, and prepare a brief verification note listing key sources and checks performed.
