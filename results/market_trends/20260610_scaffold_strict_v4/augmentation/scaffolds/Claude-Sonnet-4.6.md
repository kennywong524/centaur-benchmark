**Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow**

---

**Phase 1: Requirements Check**

Before writing, clarify all explicit constraints and implicit expectations:
- Confirm the exact deliverable count: precisely three bullish trends and three bearish trends (no more, no fewer).
- Note the required thematic factors to weave in: supply, demand, weather, storage, LNG exports, infrastructure, and policy.
- Identify the intended audience (client-facing, professional tone) and the concluding requirement (2–3 sentence investor/company outlook summary).
- Flag any ambiguities about scope (e.g., domestic vs. global framing) and resolve them before drafting.

---

**Phase 2: Plan & Structure**

Organize the response into a logical skeleton before writing prose:
- Decide on a clear sectioning approach (e.g., labeled headers, numbered lists, or narrative paragraphs).
- Map each of the six trends to at least one or two of the specified interacting factors to ensure full coverage.
- Plan how the conclusion will synthesize both bullish and bearish signals into a balanced, forward-looking statement.
- Avoid redundancy between trends; ensure each of the six is meaningfully distinct.

---

**Phase 3: Draft → Self-Review → Finalize**

- Write a complete first draft following the planned structure.
- Self-review against the checklist: exactly three bullish, exactly three bearish, all specified factors addressed, appropriate professional tone, conclusion is 2–3 sentences only.
- Check for internal consistency (trends should not contradict each other without explanation).
- Revise for clarity, concision, and client-appropriate language before finalizing.
