**Assistant Guidance: Three‑Phase Workflow**

**1. Requirements Check**  
- Verify the deliverable type (briefing, report, memo) and target audience (client, investor, internal stakeholder).  
- Confirm the exact number of items requested for each category (e.g., three bullish and three bearish factors).  
- List all thematic elements that must appear (supply‑side dynamics, demand drivers, weather impacts, storage considerations, LNG export trends, infrastructure developments, policy/regulatory influences).  
- Note any formatting constraints (headings, bullet points, length limits, concluding summary).  
- Ensure any required citations, data sources, or disclaimer language are identified.

**2. Plan & Structure**  
- Draft an outline that allocates a dedicated section for bullish trends and another for bearish trends, each containing three concise points.  
- Within each point, plan a brief explanation of the underlying driver and its interaction with the other thematic elements.  
- Design a “Interaction Overview” subsection that weaves together supply, demand, weather, storage, LNG, infrastructure, and policy to show how they collectively shape price movements.  
- Reserve a short concluding paragraph (2‑3 sentences) that synthesizes the overall outlook for the intended audience.  
- Decide on consistent formatting (e.g., bolded trend titles, bullet‑point explanations) and placeholder slots for data or examples.

**3. Draft → Self‑Review → Finalize**  
- Write the first draft following the outline, inserting placeholders where specific data or citations will be added later.  
- Conduct a self‑review focusing on: completeness of required items, logical flow between trends and interaction discussion, clarity for the target audience, and adherence to length/format constraints.  
- Verify that each trend is distinct, supported by the identified factors, and that the conclusion succinctly captures the net perspective.  
- Incorporate any missing data, refine language for precision, and perform a final proofread for grammar, consistency, and proper citation formatting before delivering the finished briefing.
