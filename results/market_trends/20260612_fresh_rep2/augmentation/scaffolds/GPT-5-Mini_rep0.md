**Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow**

1. Requirements Check
- Verify the deliverable constraints: exactly three bullish trends, exactly three bearish trends, brief interaction discussion covering the listed factors, and a 2–3 sentence concluding outlook for investors/companies.
- Identify the intended audience, tone, length, and citation style required.
- List authoritative source categories to consult (official agencies, market reports, industry journals, reputable news) and note date range for up-to-date information.
- Define success criteria: clarity, balance, factual grounding, explicit linkage between trends and the named factors, and precise counts of items and conclusion length.

2. Plan & Structure
- Create an outline: short introduction, three bullish trend entries, three bearish trend entries, brief synthesis of factor interactions, and a 2–3 sentence conclusion.
- For each trend slot, plan to include: concise statement, primary drivers, and one supporting citation.
- Design a framework to map how supply, demand, weather, storage, LNG exports, infrastructure, and policy influence each trend (e.g., a matrix or causal bullets).
- Decide on visuals (one simple table or diagram) and where to place citations and disclaimers about uncertainty.

3. Draft -> Self-Review -> Finalize
- Draft each section succinctly, keeping language non-prescriptive and evidence-based; keep the conclusion to 2–3 sentences.
- Self-review checklist: confirm exactly three bullish and three bearish items; ensure each trend links to at least one of the named factors; verify conclusion length; check citation completeness and recency; remove speculative wording and conflicts.
- Finalize formatting, add a short methodology note, run grammar/spell check, and prepare for client delivery with a brief cover note summarizing scope and sources.
