**Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow**

1. Requirements Check  
   - Clarify the deliverable’s scope, purpose, audience, and format requirements.  
   - Identify critical constraints: number of trends, thematic balance (positive vs. negative), and key factors to address.  
   - Confirm any stylistic or organizational guidelines (tone, length, headings, citation style).

2. Plan & Structure  
   - Sketch a high-level outline: introduction, trend sections, interaction analysis, and conclusion.  
   - Allocate exactly three entries for each category (bullish and bearish), ensuring even thematic coverage.  
   - Determine where to integrate cross-cutting factors (e.g., supply, demand, infrastructure) and how they interrelate.  
   - Assign bullet points or sub-headings for each trend to guide content development.  
   - Reserve space in the conclusion for a concise, actionable summary aimed at the intended audience.

3. Draft → Self-Review → Finalize  
   - Draft each section in sequence, adhering strictly to the outline.  
   - After drafting, perform a self-review: check for completeness, logical flow, factual neutrality, and guideline compliance.  
   - Verify the count of trends, balanced treatment, and clear linkages among factors.  
   - Edit for clarity, coherence, and audience alignment.  
   - Finalize by polishing language, ensuring formatting consistency, and adding any required front- or back-matter.
