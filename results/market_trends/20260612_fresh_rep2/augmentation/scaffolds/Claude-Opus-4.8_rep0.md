**Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow**

**1. Requirements Check**
Begin by parsing the task for all explicit constraints. Note the required counts: exactly three bullish and exactly three bearish trends—no more, no fewer. Identify the analytical lens (a client briefing on natural gas prices for a specific year) and the mandatory factor categories you must weave in (supply, demand, weather, storage, LNG exports, infrastructure, policy). Confirm the required closing element (a 2–3 sentence outlook for investors/companies) and its length limit. List these as a checklist to verify against later.

**2. Plan & Structure**
Sketch a skeleton before writing. Decide on a logical flow: framing intro, bullish section, bearish section, interaction discussion, and concluding outlook. For each trend slot, plan to pair it with the relevant interacting factors rather than treating factors as a separate list. Consider whether to balance bullish/bearish in parallel structure for readability. Reserve space to show how factors reinforce or offset one another. Keep the briefing tone professional and audience-appropriate (a client). Allocate rough proportions so the conclusion stays brief.

**3. Draft → Self-Review → Finalize**
Write a full draft following your skeleton. Then audit against your checklist: confirm the exact 3-and-3 counts, verify each named factor appears and is connected to a trend, and check the conclusion length. Review for balance, internal consistency, and that claims logically support the stated outlook. Trim redundancy, tighten transitions, and ensure clarity for a non-expert client before finalizing.
