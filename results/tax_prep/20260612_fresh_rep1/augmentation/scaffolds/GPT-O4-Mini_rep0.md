**Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow**

1. Requirements Check  
   a. Clarify objectives  
      - Determine exactly what the end deliverable must include (e.g., discrepancy report, rule explanations, recalculations, form adjustments, missing‐info notices).  
      - Verify tax year and jurisdiction context.  
   b. Inventory client materials  
      - List all provided documents and data points.  
      - Flag any missing or ambiguous items needed to complete calculations or verifications.  
   c. Compliance and scope  
      - Confirm any professional standards or reference materials to use.  
      - Ensure understanding of statutory rules for both federal and state.

2. Plan & Structure  
   a. Outline major sections  
      1. Data validation and completeness check  
      2. Identification of discrepancies  
      3. Explanation of applicable rules  
      4. Recalculation framework and assumptions  
      5. Form and schedule correction recommendations  
      6. Missing data identification and estimation methodology  
   b. Define sub‐tasks and sequence  
      - Assign order (e.g., validate income before exploring deductions).  
      - Note dependencies (e.g., expense details needed to compute self‐employment tax).  
   c. Specify output format  
      - Decide how to present findings (narrative sections, tables, checklist).  
      - Allocate space for explanatory footnotes and references.

3. Draft -> Self-Review -> Finalize  
   a. Draft  
      - Populate each outlined section with structured placeholders.  
      - Insert prompts for where calculations, rule citations, and form references will go.  
   b. Self‐Review  
      - Cross‐check that all requirements are addressed.  
      - Validate logical flow and completeness of the outline.  
      - Ensure no task‐specific content is pre‐filled.  
   c. Finalize  
      - Refine headings and transitions.  
      - Confirm readiness for a detailed solution draft.
