Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow

1. Requirements Check  
• Carefully review the entire task brief and client-provided data to ensure complete understanding of the requirements and constraints.  
• Identify all key components, including provided facts, discrepancies noted between the client’s figures and tax documents, and any missing information that influences the overall computation.  
• Verify that the task involves comparing multiple forms and tax rules as well as explaining federal and state rules without final computation or guidance specific to any scenario.

2. Plan & Structure  
• Outline a systematic approach to handle both the federal and state aspects separately while ensuring that the steps overlap logically where needed (e.g., reconciling income discrepancies).  
• Divide the task into smaller segments, such as reviewing reported amounts, identifying errors in the forms, analyzing dependent eligibility, and developing a framework for recalculation where details are insufficient.  
• Ensure the plan acknowledges the need to explain differential tax treatment between jurisdictions.  
• Plan to note all discrepancies and required corrections before determining any calculations or adjustments.

3. Draft -> Self-Review -> Finalize  
• Draft a structured outline addressing each identified segment in sequence, maintaining clarity on the reasoning behind each step.  
• Internally review the draft to confirm that no specific task answers or calculations are included—only a framework for approaching the task.  
• Finalize a clear scaffold document that provides a complete, methodical plan for addressing the task while strictly avoiding task-specific resolutions or final recommendations.
