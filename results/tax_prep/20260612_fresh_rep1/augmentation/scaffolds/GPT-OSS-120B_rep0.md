**Assistant Guidance: Three‑Phase Workflow**

**1. Requirements Check**  
- Compile a checklist of all source documents that must be present (e.g., wage statements, 1099s, expense receipts, mortgage statements, property‑tax records, withholding statements, dependent information).  
- Verify that the filing status and dependent eligibility criteria have been evaluated against the client’s personal circumstances.  
- Identify any missing data points (e.g., unreconciled expense totals, supporting schedules, state‑specific worksheets) and note them for follow‑up.  
- Confirm that the appropriate tax year and jurisdiction are applied, and that the client’s chosen filing status aligns with the rules for that year.  

**2. Plan & Structure**  
- Map out the review sequence: start with gross income reconciliation, then adjust for self‑employment considerations, followed by deduction analysis (standard vs. itemized) and credit eligibility.  
- List the specific federal and state forms/schedules that may be required (e.g., Schedule C, Schedule SE, Schedule A, state‑specific adjustments).  
- Develop a calculation framework: define how to recompute adjusted gross income, taxable income, and tax liability using the corrected figures and applicable rates.  
- Outline a brief explanatory narrative for each discrepancy, referencing the governing tax provisions without performing the actual computation.  

**3. Draft → Self‑Review → Finalize**  
- Draft a structured response that includes: (a) identified gaps, (b) step‑by‑step correction plan, (c) required forms/schedules, and (d) a clear statement of any remaining uncertainties and the information needed to resolve them.  
- Conduct a self‑review focusing on completeness, logical flow, adherence to tax rules, and clarity of explanations; verify that no calculations are inadvertently performed.  
- Incorporate any revisions from the self‑review, ensure the document is concise and professional, and prepare the final version for delivery to the client.
