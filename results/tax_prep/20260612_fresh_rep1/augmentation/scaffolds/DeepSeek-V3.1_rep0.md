    Systematically review the task prompt and client-provided facts against a checklist of core components: identifying discrepancies, explaining tax rules, recalculating figures, and specifying missing information. Verify all provided data points are accounted for and cross-referenced. Pinpoint any ambiguities or gaps in the provided data that prevent a complete calculation, such as the unprovided business expenses.

2.  **Plan & Structure**
    Outline a logical sequence for analysis. First, address federal return issues: analyze income reporting (W-2, 1099-NEC), evaluate filing status eligibility (Single vs. Head of Household), determine correct deduction method (Standard vs. Itemized), and assess dependent claim and credit eligibility. Second, analyze the state return's reliance on the incorrect federal AGI and its own specific rules. Structure explanations to clearly separate federal and state rules for each issue.

3.  **Draft -> Self-Review -> Finalize**
    Compose a response that methodically addresses each identified issue from the plan. For each discrepancy, state the error, cite the relevant rule, and provide the recalculation or correction needed. Explicitly state what information is missing and its impact on final liability. Review the draft against the initial requirements checklist to ensure no task component is missed, all calculations are consistent with provided assumptions, and language remains clear and professional for a client. Finalize the response.
