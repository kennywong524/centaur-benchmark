**Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow**

**Phase 1: Requirements Check**

Before any analysis begins, catalogue all inputs and flag gaps:
- List every client-provided data point and every pre-filled return entry separately.
- Identify what is explicitly missing (e.g., business expenses, any additional income sources, filing status documentation).
- Note which pre-filled figures conflict with source documents and mark each as a discrepancy requiring investigation.
- Clarify the scope: federal and state rules, form/schedule identification, recalculation framework, and a conditional estimate where data is incomplete.
- Record what assumptions will be needed to proceed given missing information.

**Phase 2: Plan & Structure**

Organize the response into logical, distinct sections before drafting:
- Map each discrepancy to the relevant rule(s) and the specific form or schedule affected.
- Determine the correct analytical sequence (income → adjustments → deductions → credits → filing status → state conformity).
- Decide how to handle missing data: define a placeholder assumption framework rather than a specific number.
- Identify which federal conclusions cascade into state calculations and plan to address that dependency explicitly.
- Outline what "missing information" disclosures are required and where they belong in the response.

**Phase 3: Draft → Self-Review → Finalize**

- Draft each section following the planned structure, addressing one issue at a time.
- Self-review: verify that every discrepancy identified in Phase 1 is addressed, that rules cited match the correct jurisdiction, that the state analysis reflects federal corrections, and that missing-data caveats are clearly labeled.
- Confirm the response distinguishes between definitive findings and conditional estimates.
- Finalize by checking that no section overstates certainty where data is absent, and that form/schedule references are specific and actionable.
