1. Income Understatement and Self-Employment Tax  
 Discrepancy: You reported only $2,400 of the $4,200 shown on the 1099-NEC and omitted Schedule C and Schedule SE entirely.  
 Federal rule: All self-employment income must be reported on Schedule C (or C-EZ) to compute net profit (or loss). Net profit is subject to self-employment tax on Schedule SE at 15.3% of 92.35% of net profit; you then get an “above-the-line” deduction equal to half the SE tax.  
 Assumption: No business expenses.  
 Recalculation:  
 • Schedule C gross receipts: $4,200; expenses: $0; net profit: $4,200.  
 • Schedule SE: net earnings = $4,200 × 0.9235 = $3,880; SE tax = $3,880 × 15.3% ≈ $594; deduction for AGI = $594 ÷ 2 ≈ $297.
