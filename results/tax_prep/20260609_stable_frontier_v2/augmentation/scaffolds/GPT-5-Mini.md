What federal and California rules apply (short form)
- 1099‑NEC / Schedule C: All nonemployee compensation (1099‑NEC) must be reported on Schedule C (or Schedule C‑EZ if allowed) showing gross receipts and business expenses. Net profit/loss flows to Form 1040 and becomes part of gross income.
- Schedule SE (self‑employment tax): Net earnings from self‑employment are subject to self‑employment (SE) tax. The SE tax is computed on Schedule SE; one‑half of SE tax is an above‑the‑line deduction on federal Form 1040 (Schedule 1).
- Itemized vs standard deduction: Mortgage interest (Form 1098), property taxes and state income taxes are itemized deductions on Schedule A (subject to SALT $10,000 cap for state/local taxes). Mortgage interest is not the standard deduction.
- Head of Household: HOH filing status is available if the taxpayer paid more than half the cost of keeping up a home for a qualifying person who lived with them more than half the year (a full‑time student under age 24 who lived with the taxpayer more than half the year can be a qualifying child).
- Dependency and credits: A 22‑year‑old full‑time student can be a qualifying child (for dependency) if under 24 and full‑time and other tests are met, but is not eligible for the Child Tax Credit (CTC), which requires the child be under age 17. If the child qualifies as a dependent, the taxpayer may be eligible for the $500 Credit for Other Dependents (ODC) on the federal return. California has its own dependent exemption/credit rules that must be checked and updated on Form 540/Schedule CA.
- California differences: California starts with federal AGI but requires additions/subtractions on Schedule CA for items that California does not allow (examples: add back one‑half SE tax deduction, disallowance of certain federal deductions). Also California does not follow the federal SALT deduction rules in the same way — you must prepare Schedule CA to reconcile items.
