# Tax Return Review — 2025 Tax Year (California Resident)

Below is a structured review of the discrepancies, applicable rules, recalculations, and the forms/schedules requiring correction.

## 1. Freelance Income Understated

**Discrepancy:** Return reports $2,400; the 1099-NEC shows $4,200.

**Rule:** All self-employment income must be reported on **Schedule C**, regardless of whether a 1099 was issued. The IRS receives a copy of the 1099-NEC, so the $1,800 understatement is an automatic matching flag.

**Correction needed:**
- Prepare **Schedule C** reporting gross receipts of **$4,200**.
- Deduct legitimate business expenses (currently missing — see below).

## 2. Missing Schedule C and Business Expenses

**Issue:** No Schedule C exists, and expenses were not provided.

**Why it matters:** Expenses reduce both income tax *and* self-employment tax. Without them, the client overpays SE tax.

**Information missing:** A list of ordinary and necessary business expenses (supplies, software, mileage, home office, etc.).

**Framework:**
- Net SE profit = $4,200 − (expenses).
- I will proceed assuming **$0 expenses** for a conservative estimate but flag this as an item to gather.

## 3. Missing Self-Employment Tax (Schedule SE / Schedule 2)

**Rule:** Net SE earnings ≥ $400 trigger self-employment tax (15.3% on 92.35% of net profit).

**Calculation (assuming $0 expenses):**
| Step | Amount |
|---|---|
| Net SE profit | $4,200 |
| × 92.35% | $3,878.70 |
| SE tax (15.3%) | **≈ $593** |
| Deduction for ½ SE tax (above-the-line) | **≈ $297** |

**Corrections needed:**
- **Schedule SE** to compute the ~$593.
- **Schedule 2** to carry SE tax into total tax.
- **Schedule 1** to claim the ½ SE-tax adjustment (~$297).

## 4. Line 12 Deduction Error (Critical Misclassification)

**Discrepancy:** Line 12 shows **$9,800 labeled "standard deduction."** This is wrong on two counts:

1. The 2025 standard deduction for **Single** is **$15,000**, not $9,800.
2. The $9,800 appears to actually be the **mortgage interest** — an *itemized* deduction mistakenly entered as the standard deduction.

**Rule:** A taxpayer takes the **greater** of the standard deduction or itemized deductions (Schedule A).

**Itemized deduction analysis (Schedule A):**
| Item | Amount |
|---|---|
| Mortgage interest (1098) | $9,800 |
| State/local taxes (SALT) — property $4,800 + CA withholding $2,900 = $7,700, but **capped at $10,000** | $7,700 |
| **Total itemized** | **$17,500** |

> Note: For 2025, the SALT cap is generally $10,000. The $7,700 here is under the cap, so it's fully deductible.

**Comparison:**
- Standard (Single): **$15,000**
- Itemized: **$17,500** ✅

**Itemizing wins** — but only if Single. (See HOH analysis below, which changes this.)

**Correction needed:** Prepare **Schedule A**; correct Line 12.
