**Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow**

**1. Requirements Check**  
- Verify that all source documents (W‑2, 1099‑NEC, 1098, state withholding statements, expense logs, dependent information) are present and legible.  
- Confirm the client’s filing year, state, and filing status, and note any discrepancies between the client’s selections and eligibility rules.  
- Identify missing data (e.g., unreconciled expense details, supporting schedules, dependent support calculations) and list the specific items needed before proceeding.  

**2. Plan & Structure**  
- Outline the logical sequence: reconcile income figures, compute correct self‑employment amounts, determine the appropriate deduction method (standard vs. itemized), assess eligibility for credits and filing‑status options, and then calculate federal and state tax liabilities.  
- Map each required IRS and state form/schedule to the corresponding data point (e.g., Schedule C for freelance income, Schedule SE for self‑employment tax, Schedule A for itemized deductions, Schedule 8812 for child‑related credits).  
- Create a checklist of rule references (e.g., qualifying dependent criteria, head‑of‑household eligibility, deduction limits) to ensure every applicable regulation is addressed.  

**3. Draft → Self‑Review → Finalize**  
- Draft a concise report that first summarizes identified discrepancies, then explains the governing rules, followed by recalculated figures using the client’s assumptions. Include a clear indication of which forms/schedules need correction or addition.  
- Conduct a self‑review: cross‑check each calculation against source data, verify that all required forms are referenced, and ensure missing information is explicitly noted with a request for clarification.  
- Incorporate any revisions from the self‑review, polish language for clarity, and produce the final deliverable ready for client review or filing.
