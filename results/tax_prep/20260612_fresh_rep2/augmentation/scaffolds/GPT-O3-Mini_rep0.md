Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow

1. Requirements Check  
• Begin with a careful review of the task’s core objectives, ensuring a complete understanding of the discrepancies, the explanations required for federal and state rules, and the recalculations that need to be addressed.  
• Confirm that all critical components such as the provided tax data, filing status issues, and potential inconsistencies are identified.  
• Verify that all specified hard constraints and limitations on content are noted and will be adhered to.

2. Plan & Structure  
• Develop a high-level outline that segments the review process logically, including sections for discrepancy identification, rule explanation, recalculation guidance, and a discussion of required corrections.  
• Create a structured framework that clearly distinguishes between the process of reviewing the provided information and the steps required to address any encountered issues.  
• Ensure the plan explicitly addresses how to handle insufficient data by identifying missing elements and establishing a method for estimating tax liability without relying on specific computations.

3. Draft -> Self-Review -> Finalize  
• Draft an initial version of the response based solely on the outlined structure, ensuring that the content remains process-focused and generic without specific task answers.  
• Conduct a thorough self-review to verify that all sections adhere to the required constraints and that the instructions remain clear and organized.  
• Finalize the scaffold by making necessary adjustments, confirming that the guidance is comprehensive and correctly segmented into the three defined phases while avoiding any task-specific content.
