**Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow**

1. Requirements Check  
Begin by carefully reviewing the task instructions and the client-provided facts. Identify all the data points, forms, and schedules mentioned. Note any missing information, incomplete forms, or inconsistencies between the provided figures and supporting documents (e.g., discrepancies between 1099-NEC and return entries). List all federal and California-specific requirements relevant to the scenario, including filing status eligibility, dependent qualifications, and the treatment of deductions and credits. Ensure you understand which forms and schedules should be involved for both federal and state returns.

2. Plan & Structure  
Map out a step-by-step approach for reviewing the returns. Organize your review to first verify income reporting (W-2, 1099-NEC) and then check for omitted or misapplied deductions/credits (e.g., standard vs. itemized deduction, business expenses, child tax credit, head of household status). Prepare to address each discrepancy and missing item by referencing the relevant federal and California tax rules. Outline how you will recalculate key figures using only the provided data and clearly state any assumptions required due to missing information. Plan to identify which forms/schedules need correction or completion and how to communicate these needs.

3. Draft -> Self-Review -> Finalize  
Draft your findings in a clear, organized format:  
- Summarize each discrepancy or issue found.  
- Explain applicable rules and why corrections are needed.  
- Present recalculated or estimated figures, making explicit any assumptions.  
- List missing information and describe what is needed to complete the returns accurately.  
- Specify which forms and schedules require correction or completion.  
After drafting, self-review for completeness, clarity, and adherence to instructions before finalizing your response.
