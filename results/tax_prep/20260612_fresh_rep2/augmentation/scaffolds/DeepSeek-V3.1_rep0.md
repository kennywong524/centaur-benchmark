**Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow**

**1. Requirements Check**
First, systematically catalog all provided client data against the pre-filled returns. Identify every explicit data point (e.g., income sources, payments, dependent details) and cross-reference them with the corresponding entries on the forms. Flag all inconsistencies between the provided facts and the client's prepared documents. Note any missing information crucial for accurate calculation, such as the unprovided business expenses.

**2. Plan & Structure**
Next, outline the necessary analytical steps based on the flagged discrepancies. Structure the analysis by tax domain: income reporting (W-2, 1099), deductions (standard vs. itemized), dependents/credits, and filing status. For each domain, list the specific federal and California rules that must be applied (e.g., qualification for Head of Household, deduction phase-outs, self-employment tax). Determine the sequence of recalculations, ensuring federal AGI is corrected first as it flows to the state return.

**3. Draft -> Self-Review -> Finalize**
Compile the findings into a clear discrepancy report. For each issue, draft a concise explanation of the rule, the identified error, and the required corrective action (e.g., form/schedule to add or amend). If data is missing, explicitly state what is needed and outline the calculation framework for an estimate. Finally, review the entire response to ensure it is comprehensive, avoids unsupported conclusions, and clearly separates fact from assumption.
