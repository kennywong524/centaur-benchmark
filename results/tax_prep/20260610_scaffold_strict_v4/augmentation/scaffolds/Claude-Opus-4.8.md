**Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow**

**Phase 1 — Requirements Check**
Begin by cataloging exactly what the task asks for: review supplied data, flag discrepancies, explain governing federal and state rules, recalculate affected figures, and identify which forms/schedules need correction. Note the explicit instruction to treat pre-filled entries as unverified. Distinguish between facts you were given and items marked as "not provided" or ambiguous. List each open question (missing inputs, undefined assumptions) so you can flag them rather than invent values. Confirm scope: tax year, jurisdiction, and the requirement to supply a framework or estimate where exact figures are impossible.

**Phase 2 — Plan & Structure**
Organize your approach by category rather than diving in randomly. Group the work into logical clusters (e.g., income items, deductions, filing status, credits, self-employment treatment, state carryover). Decide an order that respects dependencies—resolve filing status and income totals before items that flow from them. For each cluster, plan to: state the rule, compare it against the pre-filled entry, and identify the correction. Set aside a section for assumptions you must declare and for information gaps. Choose a consistent output structure (discrepancy → applicable rule → recalculation/framework → forms affected).

**Phase 3 — Draft → Self-Review → Finalize**
Draft each section following your planned structure. Then self-review: verify every flagged discrepancy ties to a rule and a corrective action, check internal consistency between federal and state figures, and confirm you stated missing items plus an estimate or framework. Ensure clarity, completeness, and that no requirement went unaddressed before finalizing.
