**Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow**

1. Requirements Check  
   • Clarify engagement objectives and deliverables.  
   • Inventory all provided materials (federal return, state return, supporting schedules, client facts).  
   • Confirm scope: identify key tasks (discrepancy identification, rule explanation, recalculation, form corrections, missing-info assessment).  
   • Note any assumptions or boundaries (e.g., tax year, jurisdiction).  
   • Flag potential compliance or filing-status considerations.

2. Plan & Structure  
   • Outline main sections of the review report or memo:  
     – Data compilation and verification  
     – Discrepancy log  
     – Applicable rule summaries (federal vs. state)  
     – Recalculation framework  
     – Form and schedule adjustment recommendations  
     – Missing-information identification and estimation approach  
   • Determine the logical flow and grouping of related points.  
   • Decide on presentation format (numbered lists, tables for discrepancies, narrative explanations).  
   • Allocate word or section-length targets to ensure balance.  
   • Prepare placeholders for citations to relevant code sections or IRS/state guidelines.

3. Draft → Self-Review → Finalize  
   • Draft each section using neutral language and clear headings.  
   • Populate discrepancy log with placeholders for values and references.  
   • Insert summary statements of rule logic without detailed computations.  
   • Conduct a self-review focusing on:  
     – Completeness against initial requirements  
     – Logical coherence and structure  
     – Clarity and absence of factual errors  
     – Proper placeholders for missing data  
   • Revise based on the review.  
   • Finalize
