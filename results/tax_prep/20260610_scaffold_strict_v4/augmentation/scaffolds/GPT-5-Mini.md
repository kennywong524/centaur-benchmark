**Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow**

1. Requirements Check
- Inventory all source documents and the submitted federal and state return files. Mark any missing sources needed to verify entries.
- Create a checklist to compare each reported line item against its document(s): wages, other income, withholding, reported deductions/credits, and any carryovers to state.
- Flag apparent omissions (schedules or worksheets not present) and items that do not reconcile with source documents.
- Identify which eligibility determinations must be made (filing status options, dependent tests, credit qualifications) and list the legal criteria or authoritative references you will consult.

2. Plan & Structure
- Outline the sequence of corrections: reconcile income, determine business/self-employment reporting, recompute adjustments, decide itemize vs. standard, then recalc federal AGI, tax, and credits before updating state return.
- Define assumptions to use for any missing client data, and state how you will document and label those assumptions in calculations.
- List all forms, schedules, and worksheets likely needed to implement corrections and the order to prepare them. Allocate workpapers for each recalculation and for carryover items to state return.

3. Draft -> Self-Review -> Finalize
- Draft corrected entries and supporting schedules on separate workpapers, showing step-by-step calculations and citations for applied rules.
- Perform a self-review checklist: arithmetic accuracy, consistency across forms, proper flow of AGI and taxable income to state, correct application of filing status/dependent rules, and clear documentation of assumptions/missing items.
- Prepare a concise summary for the client: what was changed, what remains missing, how estimates were made, and next steps or documents required. Include versioned files and a sign-off readiness check before delivering.
