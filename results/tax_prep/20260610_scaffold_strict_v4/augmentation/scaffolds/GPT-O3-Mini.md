Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow

Phase 1: Requirements Check  
• Begin by identifying all the primary data points and constraints provided in the assignment.  
• List out the client facts, the credited forms, and the discrepancies flagged between client data and pre-filled returns.  
• Confirm the specific tax year and state requirements, and note any assumptions that must be validated.  
• Ensure you recognize missing supporting documentation or unclear information that may affect the analysis.  

Phase 2: Plan & Structure  
• Outline the steps required to review the available data and determine the corresponding discrepancies.  
• Identify areas where federal versus state treatment diverges and plan to detail the relevant rules without computing final outcomes.  
• Develop a structure that segments the work by form discrepancies, recalculation frameworks, and identification of missing information.  
• Consider how to clearly articulate the scope of review for each area, ensuring your reasoning process remains clear and logical.  

Phase 3: Draft -> Self-Review -> Finalize  
• Create an initial draft that follows the structured plan outlined in Phase 2, carefully describing the analytical approach without providing the final computed results.  
• Conduct a self-review to ensure all constraints have been met and that no solution-level content is present.  
• Refine the draft for clarity, ensuring each step logically flows and the framework is comprehensive for the intended review process before finalizing your guidance.
