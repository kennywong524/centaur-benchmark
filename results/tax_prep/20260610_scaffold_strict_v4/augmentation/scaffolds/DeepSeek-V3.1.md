**Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow**

**1. Requirements Check**
First, systematically catalog all provided client data against the task's explicit instructions. Identify every data point given, such as income sources, expenses, and pre-filled return entries. Then, cross-reference this list with the task requirements to pinpoint every mandatory deliverable, including discrepancy identification, rule explanation, recalculation, form correction, and handling of insufficient information. Flag any immediate gaps where client data is absent (e.g., missing business expenses) versus where pre-filled data is present but potentially incorrect. This phase is purely about inventorying what is provided and what is explicitly required, without performing any analysis.

**2. Plan & Structure**
Develop a structured outline to organize the response logically. This should begin with a high-level summary of the major issues discovered. Subsequent sections should be dedicated to each key area: income reporting, deductions, filing status, dependents/credits, and state-specific implications. For each section, plan to first state the discrepancy, then cite the applicable rule (federal or state), followed by the necessary recalculation based on provided assumptions, and finally, note the specific forms or schedules requiring amendment. Ensure the outline includes a dedicated segment to explicitly state what information is missing to prevent an exact calculation and to provide a framework for estimation.

**3. Draft -> Self-Review -> Finalize**
In the drafting stage, populate the outline from Phase 2. For each planned section, write concise explanations, ensuring all task requirements are addressed sequentially. Adhere strictly to a neutral, explanatory tone, avoiding any language that could be construed as final advice. In the self-review, rigorously check that no final tax liability figure is calculated or stated due to missing data, and confirm that all client-provided facts are addressed. Verify that explanations of rules are generic and that all recalculations are presented as illustrative frameworks only. Finalize the response by ensuring it is a guidance document, not a completed return.
