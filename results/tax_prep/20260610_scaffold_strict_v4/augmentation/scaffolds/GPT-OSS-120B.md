**Assistant Guidance: Three‑Phase Workflow**

**1. Requirements Check**  
- Verify that all source documents referenced by the client are present (e.g., wage statements, 1099s, mortgage statements, property‑tax receipts, withholding statements).  
- Cross‑reference each figure on the draft returns with the underlying documents; flag any mismatches or omissions.  
- Identify any missing information that prevents a complete liability calculation (e.g., unreconciled expense details, supporting schedules, dependent support documentation).  
- Compile a checklist of applicable federal and state filing rules that may affect filing status, deductions, credits, and self‑employment tax obligations.  

**2. Plan & Structure**  
- Outline the response in logical sections: (a) Summary of client‑provided data, (b) Discrepancy identification, (c) Applicable tax rules and status considerations, (d) Revised calculations with assumptions, (e) Required forms/schedules and corrections, (f) Gaps in information and suggested estimates or frameworks, (g) Final recommendations.  
- For each discrepancy, note the specific line or schedule affected and the rule that governs the correct treatment.  
- Determine where alternative calculations (e.g., itemized vs. standard deduction, filing‑status options) should be presented, and decide on a clear format for comparative figures.  

**3. Draft → Self‑Review → Finalize**  
- Draft each section using concise, professional language; embed citations to the relevant IRS/California statutes or publications.  
- Conduct a self‑review checklist: accuracy of cross‑references, consistency of numbers across sections, completeness of required forms, clarity of explanations, and adherence to the client’s filing status options.  
- Verify that any estimates are clearly labeled as such and that missing data is explicitly listed.  
- Incorporate any revisions identified in the review, then produce a polished final document ready for client delivery.
