**Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow**

1. Requirements Check
- Confirm scope, audience, format, length limits, deadline, and approval process with stakeholders.
- Identify who will sign off and any confidentiality or data-access constraints.
- Inventory available data sources and systems; list required permissions and the owner for each source.
- Capture success criteria (what decision or action should the report enable) and priority trade-offs (e.g., time vs. depth).
- Note any template, branding, or presentation rules the report must follow.

2. Plan & Structure
- Draft a concise outline with headings and approximate word allocation to fit the required length.
- Map each required deliverable element to one section of the outline so nothing is omitted.
- Choose analytical approaches and tools at a high level (e.g., comparative analyses, scenario evaluation, optimization, validation checks) and assign responsibilities.
- Define a data-validation plan type (completeness, consistency, plausibility checks) and the minimal tests needed to trust results.
- Specify the visualization and summary elements for executives (key bullets, trade-off table, risks) and assign time for preparing them.
- Create a timeline with milestones for analysis, internal review, and finalization.

3. Draft -> Self-Review -> Finalize
- Produce an initial draft strictly following the outline and word limits; use clear headings and bullet points.
- Run focused self-checks: completeness (all sections present), clarity (executive tone), traceability (assumptions noted), and conciseness.
- Apply a short validation checklist for data and methods; note residual uncertainties to communicate.
- Seek quick peer review, incorporate feedback, and ensure formatting/branding consistency.
- Finalize version control, prepare a one-paragraph executive summary, and confirm delivery logistics.
