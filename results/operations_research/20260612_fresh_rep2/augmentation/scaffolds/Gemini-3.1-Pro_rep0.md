**Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow**

**1. Requirements Check**
*   **Role:** Operations Research (OR) Analyst at a national logistics firm.
*   **Audience:** Senior management/executives.
*   **Format:** Concise internal report (300-400 words) using headings and bullet points.
*   **Key Deliverables:**
    *   Define data collection and validation methods.
    *   Outline an analytical/optimization framework (delivery schedules/warehouse allocations).
    *   Recommend at least two practical solutions balancing cost, service, and resources, including trade-offs and risks.
    *   Specify KPIs to monitor and the communication strategy for results.
*   **Tone:** Professional, objective, data-driven, and clear.

**2. Plan & Structure**
*   **Introduction:** Briefly state the purpose of the report (addressing delays and fuel costs).
*   **Section 1: Data Strategy:** Plan the specific data points needed and the validation process to ensure accuracy.
*   **Section 2: Analytical Framework:** Structure the explanation of the optimization model or simulation approach to be used for evaluating schedules and allocations.
*   **Section 3: Proposed Solutions & Trade-offs:** Outline two distinct, practical strategies. For each, map out the expected benefits against the associated risks and trade-offs.
*   **Section 4: Monitoring & Communication:** List the specific KPIs to track success and describe the reporting cadence/method to keep executives informed.

**3. Draft -> Self-Review -> Finalize**
*   **Draft:** Write the report following the structure, ensuring OR terminology is used correctly but remains accessible to executives. Keep explanations concise.
*   **Self-Review:** Check the word count (must be 300-400 words). Verify all four prompt requirements are explicitly addressed. Ensure headings and bullets are used effectively for readability. Assess if the tone is appropriate for an executive audience.
*   **Finalize:** Polish the language for clarity, impact, and flow. Ensure no task-specific content is missing.
