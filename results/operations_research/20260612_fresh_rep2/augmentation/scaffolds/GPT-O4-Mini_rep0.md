**Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow**

1. Requirements Check  
   • Verify deliverable specifications  
     – Report length, audience, format (headings, bullets)  
     – Key content blocks: data definition/validation, analytical framework, solutions with trade-offs, KPIs & communications  
   • Clarify objectives  
     – Executive focus: concise, high-level insights  
     – Tone and level of detail appropriate for senior management  
   • Identify constraints  
     – Word count limit, balance among sections, internal circulation only  

2. Plan & Structure  
   • Outline report skeleton  
     – Introduction: scope and purpose  
     – Section A: Data collection & validation plan  
     – Section B: Analytical/optimization framework  
     – Section C: Operational recommendations with trade-offs  
     – Section D: KPIs and communication strategy  
     – Conclusion: summary and next steps  
   • Allocate word budget per section  
     – Ensure proportional emphasis on solutions and risks  
   • Decide on formatting  
     – Headings for each section  
     – Bullet points for lists, trade-off tables or concise comparisons  
   • Map key points to structure  
     – Align data needs, methodologies, recommended actions, and metrics under corresponding headings  

3. Draft -> Self-Review -> Finalize  
   • Draft phase  
     – Write each section using the outline as a guide  
     – Insert placeholders where specific data or models will be cited  
   • Self-Review phase  
     – Check compliance with requirements and word count  
     – Ensure logical flow, executive-level clarity, and balanced coverage  
     – Verify consistency in terminology and formatting  
   • Finalize phase  
     – Refine language for conciseness and impact  
     – Confirm headings, bullet formatting, and overall readability  
     – Perform final proofread before submission
