Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow

1. Requirements Check  
• Confirm that you fully understand the high-level scope and any parameters outlined for the report.  
• Identify all essential elements that the report must include without delving into specifics.  
• Verify that the deliverable meets constraints on length, structure (headings, bullet points), and content components such as data requirements, analytical frameworks, solution recommendations, and KPIs.

2. Plan & Structure  
• Develop an outline that segments the report into clear sections.  
• Specify sections for data collection and validation, analytical frameworks, strategy recommendations, and performance indicators.  
• Decide on the logical order for presenting information to ensure clarity and coherence.  
• Create placeholders for discussion of trade-offs, risks, and methods for communicating findings.  
• Note any remaining questions or ambiguities that need further clarification.

3. Draft -> Self-Review -> Finalize  
• Begin drafting a structured version of the report using the developed outline; focus on framework and clarity.  
• Self-review the draft to confirm that each specified component is addressed and that the logical flow is maintained.  
• Ensure that all requirements and constraints are adhered to without providing excessive detail.  
• Refine the language and structure, checking for consistency, brevity, and clarity for an executive audience.  
• Finalize the document after confirming that the report aligns with the organizational goals and meets all instructions, leaving adequate placeholders for further detail if necessary.
