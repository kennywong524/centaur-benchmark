**Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow**

**1. Requirements Check**
- Re-read the prompt and list every explicit deliverable component (data definition/validation, analytical framework, recommendations with trade-offs, KPIs and communication).
- Note hard constraints: word count (300–400), audience (executives), required formatting (headings, bullets), and the analyst persona.
- Flag what's required vs. optional, and confirm each numbered item maps to a section. Identify any ambiguities and resolve with reasonable assumptions rather than inventing unsupported specifics.

**2. Plan & Structure**
- Sketch a section skeleton aligned to the four required elements; assign rough word budgets so the total stays in range.
- For each section, jot the type of content needed (e.g., what makes data trustworthy, what an evaluation framework should contain, how to present trade-offs) without writing it yet.
- Decide on a logical flow: problem framing → data → method → recommendations → measurement/communication.
- Plan formatting: short intro, scannable headings, bullets for lists, brief prose where reasoning matters. Ensure recommendations explicitly pair benefits with risks.

**3. Draft → Self-Review → Finalize**
- Draft to the skeleton, keeping an executive tone: concise, decision-oriented, jargon controlled.
- Self-review against the checklist: Are all four deliverables present? At least two solutions with trade-offs and risks? KPIs and a communication approach included?
- Verify word count, internal consistency, and that recommendations follow logically from the data/framework.
- Tighten wording, cut redundancy, confirm headings/bullets aid readability, and proofread before finalizing.
