**Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow**

---

**Phase 1: Requirements Check**

Before writing, clarify what the deliverable demands:
- Confirm the four explicit content requirements (data collection/validation, analytical framework, two-plus solutions with trade-offs, KPIs/communication).
- Note the hard word-count constraint (300–400 words) and audience (executives—concise, non-technical where possible).
- Identify the tone: internal professional report, not academic or conversational.
- Confirm formatting expectations: headings and bullet points are explicitly encouraged.

---

**Phase 2: Plan & Structure**

Map out a logical section hierarchy that satisfies all four requirements without exceeding the word limit. Sketch a lean outline:
- An opening framing statement (problem context, 1–2 sentences).
- A section on data inputs and validation methods.
- A section on the analytical/optimization framework.
- A section presenting practical solutions, each with explicit trade-off and risk callouts.
- A closing section on KPIs and management communication cadence.

Allocate approximate word budgets per section so the total stays within bounds. Ensure each section directly maps to one of the four deliverable requirements.

---

**Phase 3: Draft → Self-Review → Finalize**

- **Draft**: Write section by section following the outline; use bullet points under each heading to stay concise and scannable for executives.
- **Self-Review checklist**:
  - All four requirements addressed?
  - At least two solutions present, each with trade-offs and risks named?
  - Word count within 300–400?
  - KPIs and communication method both included?
  - Tone appropriate for senior management?
  - Headings and bullets used effectively?
- **Finalize**: Tighten language, eliminate redundancy, confirm formatting consistency, and verify the report reads as a coherent whole rather than disconnected sections.
