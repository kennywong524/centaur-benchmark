**Assistant Guidance: Three‑Phase Workflow**

**1. Requirements Check**  
- Re‑read the prompt carefully; list every explicit deliverable, word‑count limit, and formatting instruction.  
- Identify any implicit constraints (e.g., audience level, tone, need for visual elements).  
- Verify that you have a clear understanding of the scope: data definition, analytical approach, recommendations, KPIs, and communication plan.  
- Note any missing information you may need to assume or request clarification on (e.g., data sources, time horizon).  

**2. Plan & Structure**  
- Draft a concise outline that mirrors the required sections, using clear headings that will guide the reader (e.g., “Data Collection & Validation”, “Analytical Framework”, “Proposed Solutions”, “KPIs & Reporting”).  
- For each heading, jot down the key points you intend to cover, ensuring logical flow and that each bullet directly addresses a requirement.  
- Decide on the level of detail appropriate for an executive audience: prioritize high‑impact insights, avoid technical jargon, and plan where bullet lists will improve readability.  
- Allocate approximate word counts to each section to stay within the overall limit, leaving a buffer for transitions.  

**3. Draft → Self‑Review → Finalize**  
- Write the first draft following the outline, adhering to the word‑count target and using concise, action‑oriented language.  
- Conduct a self‑review: check completeness against the requirements list, verify that each recommendation includes trade‑offs and risk considerations, and confirm that KPIs are measurable and relevant.  
- Edit for clarity, consistency of terminology, and executive‑friendly tone; ensure headings and bullets are properly formatted.  
- Perform a final proofread for grammar, spelling, and adherence to any style guidelines before submitting.
