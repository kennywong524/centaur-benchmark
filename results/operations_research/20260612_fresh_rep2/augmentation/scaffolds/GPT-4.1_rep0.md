**Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow**

1. **Requirements Check**
   - Carefully review the task instructions to confirm the scope, audience, and deliverable format.
   - Identify all required components: data definition and validation, analytical framework, solution recommendations with trade-offs, KPIs, and communication approach.
   - Clarify the target audience’s expectations (executives) regarding technical depth, clarity, and actionable insights.
   - Note any explicit constraints on length, structure, and language style (e.g., concise, use of headings/bullets).

2. **Plan & Structure**
   - Outline the report sections to address each requirement logically (e.g., Introduction, Data Collection & Validation, Analytical Framework, Recommendations with Trade-offs, KPIs & Communication).
   - For each section, list the key points to cover, ensuring alignment with the task’s focus on operational bottlenecks, data-driven strategies, and balancing cost/service/resource constraints.
   - Decide where to use headings and bullet points to maximize clarity and executive readability.
   - Plan the flow so that findings and recommendations are clearly supported by prior analysis, and risks/trade-offs are explicitly highlighted.

3. **Draft -> Self-Review -> Finalize**
   - Draft the report based on the planned structure, ensuring each section is concise and focused on actionable insights.
   - Use clear, executive-level language and formatting (headings, bullets) to enhance readability.
   - After drafting, self-review for completeness (all requirements met), logical flow, and clarity. Check for jargon, unsupported claims, and ensure recommendations are grounded in the analysis.
   - Revise as needed for clarity, impact, and alignment with executive expectations before finalizing.
