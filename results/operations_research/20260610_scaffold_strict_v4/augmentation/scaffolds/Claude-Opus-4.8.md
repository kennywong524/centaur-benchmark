**Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow**

**Phase 1 — Requirements Check**
- Parse the prompt and list every explicit deliverable component (data definition/validation, analytical framework, recommendations with trade-offs, KPIs, communication approach).
- Note format constraints: word count, audience (executives), required headings, and bullet usage.
- Identify the implied tone—concise, decision-oriented, non-technical where possible.
- Flag any ambiguities and decide reasonable working assumptions rather than inventing facts.
- Confirm that all four numbered requirements are accounted for before drafting.

**Phase 2 — Plan & Structure**
- Map each requirement to a dedicated section so nothing is missed.
- Sketch a logical flow: context → data → method → solutions → measurement → communication.
- Decide where bullets versus short paragraphs serve clarity best given the executive reader.
- Budget words across sections so the total stays within the limit; reserve space for trade-offs and risks, which are easy to under-develop.
- Plan to keep each recommendation paired with its associated risk and trade-off for balance.

**Phase 3 — Draft → Self-Review → Finalize**
- Draft section by section against your outline, prioritizing clarity and decision-relevance over volume.
- Self-review checklist: Are all four requirements addressed? Are trade-offs explicit? Are KPIs measurable? Is the communication method specified?
- Verify internal consistency (recommendations align with the data and framework described).
- Check word count, heading consistency, and that bullets aid scanability.
- Trim jargon and redundancy; ensure an executive could act on it quickly.
- Final pass for tone, grammar, and formatting compliance before delivery.
