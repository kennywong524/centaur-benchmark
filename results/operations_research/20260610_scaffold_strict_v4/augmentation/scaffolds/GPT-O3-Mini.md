Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow

1. Requirements Check  
• Begin by reviewing the task objectives and constraints. Clearly identify what the final report must include while confirming the word count and specific deliverable elements.  
• Ensure comprehension of the overall problem context without delving into specific operational details. Focus on understanding the need for data collection, validation strategies, analytical frameworks, practical solutions with trade-offs, and KPI tracking.  
• Note any formatting guidance such as the use of headings and bullet points, and double-check that all hard constraints regarding not solving or detailing specifics are adhered to.

2. Plan & Structure  
• Outline the key sections of the report, mapping each section to one of the required deliverable components.  
• Create a framework that details where analysis of data collection, validation processes, and proposed methodologies will be addressed.  
• Sketch a high-level outline that includes placeholders for defining criteria, potential evaluation frameworks, identification of solution approaches, and monitoring strategies.  
• Determine an introduction and conclusion structure that connects all sections logically while remaining concise.

3. Draft -> Self-Review -> Finalize  
• Develop an initial draft by filling in the scaffold with generic descriptors that guide the eventual inclusion of required report elements, without adding task-specific content.  
• Critically review the draft to ensure it meets the logical flow and all task requirements while avoiding prohibited specifics.  
• Finalize the process document by verifying adherence to all guidelines and ensuring clarity and coherence throughout the scaffold.
