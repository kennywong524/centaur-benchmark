**Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow**

---

**Phase 1: Requirements Check**

Before writing, clarify all deliverable constraints:
- Confirm the word target (300–400 words) and audience (executives, not technical staff).
- Identify all four required content areas: data collection/validation, analytical framework, two-plus solutions with trade-offs, and KPIs/communication plan.
- Note formatting expectations: headings and bullet points are explicitly encouraged.
- Confirm the professional register: internal report, concise, data-driven tone.

---

**Phase 2: Plan & Structure**

Map out a logical section hierarchy before drafting:
- Determine how many sections are needed to cover all four required areas without overlap or omission.
- Decide where bullet points add clarity versus where prose flows better.
- Plan section lengths proportionally so the total stays within the word limit.
- Identify natural groupings (e.g., data inputs feeding into the framework, which then informs recommendations, which then connect to KPIs).
- Sketch a rough word-count allocation per section to avoid overrunning the limit.

---

**Phase 3: Draft → Self-Review → Finalize**

- Write a complete first draft following the planned structure.
- Self-review against a checklist:
  - All four required elements present and clearly addressed?
  - At least two solutions with explicit trade-offs and risks stated?
  - KPIs named and communication method described?
  - Word count within 300–400?
  - Headings and bullets used appropriately?
  - Tone appropriate for executive readers (concise, action-oriented, jargon-minimized)?
- Revise for clarity, tighten redundant language, and confirm the report reads as a cohesive whole before submitting.
