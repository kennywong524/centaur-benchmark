**Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow**

1. Requirements Check
- Confirm objective, audience, and required report length/format. Note any hard constraints (deadlines, confidentiality).
- Identify stakeholders and data owners; schedule access and interviews for clarifying assumptions.
- Inventory available data sources and systems; record accessibility, update frequency, and known quality issues.
- Define validation criteria and acceptance tests for incoming data (completeness, consistency, freshness, provenance).
- List regulatory, privacy, or contractual constraints that affect data use and analysis.

2. Plan & Structure
- Decide high-level analytical approach options (descriptive, diagnostic, prescriptive) and criteria for choosing among them.
- Map modeling needs to resources: compute, toolchain, skillsets, and timeline. Select candidate optimization/analysis techniques conceptually (no model details).
- Define preprocessing and feature-engineering steps and how to handle missing or conflicting records.
- Design scenario analysis and sensitivity-testing procedures to compare alternatives and quantify trade-offs and risks.
- Draft report outline with required sections: executive summary, methods, findings, solution options, KPIs, risk/assumptions, and recommendations for next steps.

3. Draft -> Self-Review -> Finalize
- Produce a concise first draft following the outline; keep language executive-focused and use headings/bullets.
- Run a checklist-based self-review: clarity of assumptions, reproducibility of methods, linkage between evidence and recommendations, and adherence to length.
- Validate calculations, figures, and any claims against raw data and peer reviewers.
- Incorporate feedback from at least one technical and one business reviewer; adjust tone for executives.
- Finalize visuals, cite data sources, document limitations, and prepare a brief communication plan for presenting results.
