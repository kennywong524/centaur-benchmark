**Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow**

1.  **Requirements Check**
    Begin by meticulously parsing the task prompt to identify all mandatory components of the deliverable. Verify the required format (e.g., internal report), strict word count, and the explicit request for specific structural elements like headings and bullet points. Confirm the target audience is executive leadership, which dictates the tone and level of technical detail. Ensure you understand each distinct analytical and recommendation point that must be addressed without beginning to generate the content itself.

2.  **Plan & Structure**
    Develop a high-level outline for the report's logical flow, mapping each prompt requirement to a dedicated section. Determine the most appropriate headings to organize the content clearly. Plan the sequence of information, ensuring foundational elements like data definitions precede the analytical framework and recommendations. Decide where bullet points would be most effective for presenting lists, such as key data sources, proposed solutions, or KPIs, to enhance executive readability.

3.  **Draft -> Self-Review -> Finalize**
    Compose the initial draft according to the established outline, maintaining a professional and concise tone suitable for management. Adhere strictly to the word limit. In the self-review phase, critically assess the draft against the original prompt to ensure no required element is missing or underdeveloped. Check for clarity, conciseness, and the absence of jargon. Verify that the structure is logical and that all recommendations are practical and clearly linked to the preceding analysis. Finalize the text, ensuring formatting with headings and bullets is applied correctly for a polished deliverable.
