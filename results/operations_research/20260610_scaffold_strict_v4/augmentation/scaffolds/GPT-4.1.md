**Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow**

1. Requirements Check  
   - Clarify the target audience and their expectations for the report’s tone, length, and technical depth.  
   - Identify the specific operational challenges to address (e.g., delivery delays, fuel costs) and confirm the scope (inventory, delivery, warehouse allocation).  
   - Review the deliverable’s format requirements, including word count and use of headings/bullets.  
   - Ensure understanding of the need to define data requirements, propose analytical frameworks, recommend solutions with trade-offs, and specify KPIs and communication methods.

2. Plan & Structure  
   - Outline the main sections of the report based on the requirements (e.g., Data Collection & Validation, Analytical Approach, Recommendations with Trade-Offs, KPIs & Communication).  
   - For each section, decide on the key points to address, ensuring logical flow and concise coverage.  
   - Determine where to use bullet points and headings to enhance clarity and readability for executive stakeholders.  
   - Plan how to present trade-offs and risks for each recommended solution in a balanced, non-technical manner.  
   - Identify the types of data, models, and KPIs relevant to logistics efficiency, without detailing them yet.

3. Draft -> Self-Review -> Finalize  
   - Draft the report section by section, adhering to the planned structure and maintaining clarity and conciseness.  
   - After drafting, review the report for completeness: check if all requirements are addressed, and if explanations are accessible to a non-technical executive audience.  
   - Edit for brevity, coherence, and professionalism.  
   - Ensure the use of clear headings and bullet points.  
   - Finalize the report, confirming it fits within the specified word count and meets all deliverable criteria.
