**Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow**

**1. Requirements Check**  
- Verify the scope: confirm word count, audience, and deliverable format (concise internal report).  
- List all mandatory sections the client expects (e.g., data definition, analytical framework, recommendations, KPIs, communication plan).  
- Identify any constraints (budget, timeline, confidentiality) and required deliverable attributes (headings, bullet points, executive‑friendly tone).  
- Clarify any ambiguous terms with the requester before proceeding.

**2. Plan & Structure**  
- Draft an outline that mirrors the required sections, allocating approximate word counts to stay within the total limit.  
- For each section, note the key points to cover: what information is needed, which methods or tools will be referenced, and how findings will be presented.  
- Decide on the logical flow (e.g., start with data foundation, then methodology, followed by solutions, and end with monitoring).  
- Choose visual aids (tables, bullet lists) that enhance readability without exceeding length constraints.  
- Anticipate potential gaps in data or analysis and plan brief mitigation notes.

**3. Draft → Self‑Review → Finalize**  
- Write the first draft strictly adhering to the outline, using clear headings and concise bullet points.  
- Conduct a self‑review focusing on: completeness of required elements, adherence to word count, clarity for an executive audience, and logical coherence.  
- Check for consistency in terminology, proper citation of any frameworks, and that trade‑offs and risks are explicitly stated.  
- Revise based on the review, then perform a final proofread for grammar, formatting, and alignment with the client’s style preferences before submission.
