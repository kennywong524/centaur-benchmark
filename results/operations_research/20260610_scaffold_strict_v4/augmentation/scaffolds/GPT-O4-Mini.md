**Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow**

1. Requirements Check  
   • Clarify deliverable scope  
     – Confirm word count, target audience, formatting constraints  
   • Identify key content categories  
     – Data definitions, analytical framework, recommendations, KPIs, communication plan  
   • Flag any missing details  
     – Delivery timelines, data availability, stakeholder preferences  

2. Plan & Structure  
   • Create a high-level outline  
     – Section headings and subpoints aligned to the four deliverable elements  
   • Allocate word budget  
     – Approximate word count per section to meet overall limit  
   • Organize logical flow  
     – Start with context and objectives  
     – Move to methodology  
     – Present recommendations and metrics  
     – End with reporting approach  
   • Determine formatting tools  
     – Use headings, bullet lists, tables if helpful  
     – Decide where visuals or call-outs might clarify trade-offs  

3. Draft → Self-Review → Finalize  
   • Draft  
     – Write each section according to outline, using clear, concise language  
     – Avoid jargon or overly technical phrasing for executive readership  
   • Self-Review  
     – Check alignment with requirements and word limits  
     – Verify logical transitions and completeness of each element  
     – Ensure neutrality and absence of unverified claims  
   • Finalize  
     – Edit for clarity, tone, and consistency  
     – Confirm adherence to formatting and deliverable guidelines  
     – Prepare final checklist and submit deliverable on time
