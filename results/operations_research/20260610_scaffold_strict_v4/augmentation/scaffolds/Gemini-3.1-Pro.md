**Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow**

**1. Requirements Check**
*   **Role:** Operations Research (OR) Analyst at a national logistics firm.
*   **Audience:** Senior management/executives.
*   **Format:** Concise internal report (300-400 words) using headings and bullet points.
*   **Key Elements Required:**
    *   Data collection and validation methods.
    *   Analytical/optimization framework for schedules/allocations.
    *   Two practical solutions balancing cost, service, and constraints, including trade-offs and risks.
    *   KPIs for monitoring and a communication plan for results.
*   **Tone:** Professional, objective, data-driven, and clear.

**2. Plan & Structure**
*   **Introduction:** Briefly state the purpose of the report (addressing delays and fuel costs).
*   **Section 1: Data Strategy:** Outline the types of data needed and the process for ensuring data integrity.
*   **Section 2: Analytical Framework:** Describe the OR methodologies or modeling techniques to be used for evaluating schedules and warehouse allocations.
*   **Section 3: Proposed Solutions:** Detail two specific, actionable strategies. For each, explicitly state the expected benefits, trade-offs, and associated risks.
*   **Section 4: Performance Monitoring & Communication:** List the specific KPIs to track success and describe the cadence and format for reporting back to executives.

**3. Draft -> Self-Review -> Finalize**
*   **Draft:** Write the report following the planned structure. Ensure the language is appropriate for an executive audience, focusing on high-level strategy and OR principles rather than getting bogged down in overly technical jargon. Use headings and bullets to enhance readability.
*   **Self-Review:** Check the word count to ensure it falls strictly within the 300-400 word limit. Verify that all four prompt requirements are addressed. Confirm that trade-offs and risks are clearly stated for both proposed solutions.
*   **Finalize:** Polish the text for clarity, flow, and professional tone. Ensure formatting is clean and consistent.
