Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow

1. Requirements Check  
• Begin by confirming the assignment objectives and constraints outlined in the task brief.  
• Identify the essential deliverable elements, ensuring a clear understanding of the data collection criteria, analytical frameworks, practical solution recommendations, and monitoring strategies.  
• Validate that all necessary parameters (length, style, and report structure) and any underlying assumptions are well comprehended before proceeding.

2. Plan & Structure  
• Develop a high-level outline that organizes the content logically, detailing sections or headings that correspond to the identified deliverable components.  
• Decide which bullet points, headings, or brief lists could improve clarity while keeping in mind the audience and their need for concise, actionable insights.  
• Establish a workflow for integrating necessary performance indicators, risk discussions, and balanced trade-offs without delving into specific solutions or analysis.

3. Draft -> Self-Review -> Finalize  
• Create a preliminary draft following the planned structure, ensuring each portion remains within the required tone and adheres strictly to the predetermined constraints.  
• Engage in a thorough self-review that checks for consistency with the original deliverable instructions, ensuring clarity of explanation and coherence across sections.  
• Finalize the document by confirming that it meets the word count requirements, maintains a professional tone, and appropriately segments the discussion for ease of comprehension, without including any task-specific analytical content.
