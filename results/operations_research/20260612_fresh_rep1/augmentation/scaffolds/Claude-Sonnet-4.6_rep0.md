**Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow**

---

**Phase 1: Requirements Check**

Before writing, clarify all deliverable constraints:
- Confirm the word count target (300–400 words) and audience (executives, not technical staff).
- Identify all four mandatory content areas: data collection/validation, analytical framework, two-plus solutions with trade-offs, and KPIs/communication plan.
- Note formatting expectations: headings and bullet points are explicitly encouraged.
- Confirm the professional register: internal report, concise, data-driven tone.

---

**Phase 2: Plan & Structure**

Outline a logical section flow before drafting:
- Determine a natural heading sequence that covers all four required areas without overlap.
- Decide how many bullet points versus prose sentences each section warrants, keeping executive readability in mind.
- Plan how to distribute the word count across sections so no required element is crowded out.
- Identify where trade-offs and risks must appear explicitly (tied to the solutions section).
- Decide how KPIs and communication methods will be presented distinctly from the solutions.

---

**Phase 3: Draft → Self-Review → Finalize**

Follow a disciplined three-step writing loop:
- **Draft:** Write each section in order, using the structure from Phase 2; keep language concise and jargon-minimal for an executive audience.
- **Self-Review:** Check that all four deliverable requirements are addressed; verify word count falls within 300–400; confirm trade-offs and risks are explicitly stated for each solution; ensure KPIs are measurable and communication methods are actionable.
- **Finalize:** Tighten language, remove redundancy, confirm headings and bullets are consistently formatted, and verify the report reads as a cohesive whole rather than disconnected sections.
