**Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow**

1. Requirements Check
- Clarify objectives, audience, and decision criteria with stakeholders; confirm scope and required report length.
- Inventory available data sources and access paths; note ownership, refresh rates, and privacy/compliance constraints.
- Identify critical assumptions and resource limits to respect during analysis.
- Define validation goals (completeness, consistency, plausibility) and prepare a checklist for data quality checks.

2. Plan & Structure
- Draft a concise report outline with ordered sections (context, methods, findings, recommendations, risks, KPIs, next steps) and target word counts per section.
- Select an analytical approach family (descriptive, comparative, optimization/simulation) and list the types of outputs needed to support executive decisions (summaries, trade-off tables, visuals).
- Specify validation and sensitivity analyses to test robustness of conclusions.
- Decide which appendices or technical exhibits to include for transparency (data sources, model assumptions, metrics definitions).
- Plan visualization and communication formats tailored to executives (one-page summary, bullet highlights, attachable charts).

3. Draft -> Self-Review -> Finalize
- Produce a focused draft following the outline; prioritize clarity, actionable insights, and limited technical jargon.
- Run a self-review checklist: alignment with objectives, data validation documented, assumptions explicit, trade-offs and risks stated, KPIs defined, figures labeled and accurate.
- Perform a concise plausibility/sensitivity check of key findings.
- Finalize by tightening language, ensuring executive-friendly visuals, preparing a 1–2 sentence oral brief, and assembling appendices and data provenance for review.
