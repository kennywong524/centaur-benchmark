**Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow**

**1. Requirements Check**
*   **Role:** Operations Research (OR) Analyst at a national logistics firm.
*   **Audience:** Senior management/executives.
*   **Format:** Concise internal report using headings and bullet points.
*   **Length:** 300-400 words.
*   **Core Elements Required:**
    *   Data collection and validation plan.
    *   Analytical/optimization framework for schedules/allocations.
    *   Two practical solutions balancing cost, service, and resources (including trade-offs and risks).
    *   KPIs for monitoring and a communication plan for results.
*   **Tone:** Professional, objective, data-driven, and executive-ready.

**2. Plan & Structure**
*   **Introduction:** Briefly state the purpose of the report (addressing delays and fuel costs).
*   **Section 1: Data Strategy:** Outline the specific data points needed and the methods for ensuring data accuracy.
*   **Section 2: Analytical Framework:** Describe the OR methodologies (e.g., modeling, simulation) to be used for evaluating schedules and warehouse allocations.
*   **Section 3: Proposed Solutions & Trade-offs:** Present two distinct, practical strategies. For each, explicitly state the expected benefits, potential drawbacks, and associated risks.
*   **Section 4: Monitoring & Communication:** List the specific KPIs to track success and describe the cadence and format for reporting back to the executive team.

**3. Draft -> Self-Review -> Finalize**
*   **Draft:** Write the report following the structured sections. Ensure OR terminology is used correctly but remains accessible to executives.
*   **Self-Review:** Check against the constraints: Is it between 300 and 400 words? Are there exactly two solutions with trade-offs? Are the data, framework, and KPI sections fully addressed? Ensure formatting (headings, bullets) is clean.
*   **Finalize:** Polish for clarity, conciseness, and executive impact before final output.
