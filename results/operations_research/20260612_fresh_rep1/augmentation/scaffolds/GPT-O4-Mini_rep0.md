**Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow**

1. Requirements Check  
   • Clarify deliverable format and constraints (length, audience, style).  
   • Identify key task elements to address (data needs, analytical framework, solution criteria, KPIs, communication).  
   • Confirm scope boundaries (must be concise; internal report; executive focus).  
   • Note any formatting instructions (headings, bullet lists).  
   • Ensure understanding of performance measures: what high-level outcomes management expects.

2. Plan & Structure  
   • Outline major report sections in logical order (e.g., Introduction, Data Strategy, Analysis Framework, Recommendations, KPIs & Communication).  
   • Under each section, list subpoints or bullet topics to cover.  
   • Allocate approximate word counts to each section to stay within total limit.  
   • Determine which data-validation and modeling steps to describe, and where to include trade-off discussion.  
   • Sketch transition sentences or linking phrases to maintain flow between sections.  
   • Prepare a mini checklist of required elements (two solutions, risks/trade-offs, validation methods, KPI table, summary).

3. Draft → Self-Review → Finalize  
   • Draft each section using the planned headings and bullets—keep language clear and executive-focused.  
   • After drafting, perform a self-review:  
     – Verify all required components are present and aligned with scope.  
     – Check word count per section and overall.  
     – Ensure clarity, consistency, and avoidance of jargon.  
     – Confirm formatting guidelines (headings, bullets) are met.  
   • Apply edits based on review, tighten language, correct structure.  
   • Perform a final read-through for coherence and completeness before submission.
