**Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow**

**1. Requirements Check**
- Re-read the prompt and list every explicit deliverable component (data definition/validation, analytical framework, recommendations with trade-offs, KPIs/communication).
- Note hard constraints: word count (300–400), internal-executive audience, required headings and bullet points.
- Confirm the persona (OR Analyst) and tone (concise, professional, data-driven).
- Flag any ambiguity and decide on reasonable assumptions to state briefly rather than leave blank.

**2. Plan & Structure**
- Map each of the four required elements to its own clearly labeled section so nothing is dropped.
- Decide where bullets add clarity versus where short prose fits the executive register.
- Allocate a rough word budget per section to stay inside the limit while keeping coverage balanced.
- Sketch logical flow: problem framing → data → method → recommendations → measurement/communication.
- Ensure recommendations section is built to surface explicit trade-offs and risks, since that is required.

**3. Draft → Self-Review → Finalize**
- Draft each section against the plan, keeping language concrete and decision-oriented.
- Self-review checklist: Are all four deliverables present? Is each recommendation paired with trade-offs/risks? Are KPIs measurable and a communication method named? Are validation steps included for the data?
- Verify formatting (headings, bullets) and confirm total length lands in range; trim redundancy first.
- Check audience fit: would executives quickly grasp the takeaways?
- Proofread for clarity, consistency, and that assumptions are stated. Finalize once every requirement is verifiably satisfied.
