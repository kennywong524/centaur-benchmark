**Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow**

**1. Requirements Check**  
- Verify the word‑count limit and audience (executive‑level internal report).  
- Confirm all mandatory sections: data definition/validation, analytical framework, solution recommendations with trade‑offs, KPI selection, and communication plan.  
- Identify any constraints (e.g., brevity, use of headings/bullets, tone).  
- List any required deliverables beyond the report (e.g., data collection plan, model assumptions).  
- Ensure understanding of the domain context (operations, logistics) without delving into specific content.

**2. Plan & Structure**  
- Draft a high‑level outline matching the required sections, assigning a concise heading to each.  
- Allocate approximate word counts to each section to stay within the total limit.  
- Decide on the level of detail for each bullet point (e.g., one‑line description of data sources, key steps of the analytical method).  
- Choose a logical flow: start with data, move to methodology, then to recommendations, and finish with KPIs and communication.  
- Identify placeholders for any tables, figures, or quantitative examples that will be inserted later.

**3. Draft → Self‑Review → Finalize**  
- **Draft:** Write the report following the outline, using clear executive language, headings, and bullet points. Keep sentences short and avoid jargon unless defined.  
- **Self‑Review:** Check for completeness of each required element, adherence to word count, consistency of terminology, and logical coherence. Verify that trade‑offs and risks are explicitly mentioned.  
- **Finalize:** Refine wording for clarity and impact, ensure proper formatting, and add any necessary citations or references. Perform a final proofread for grammar, spelling, and style before submission.
