**Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow**

1. Requirements Check  
- Carefully review the task instructions and deliverable expectations.
- Confirm the target audience (executives) and the required report length.
- Identify all requested report components: data definition/validation, analytical framework, solution recommendations with trade-offs, and KPI/communication plans.
- Note any constraints on tone, clarity, structure, or supporting details (e.g., use of headings, bullet points).
- Ensure understanding of the context: logistics operations, delivery delays, fuel costs, and efficiency improvement.

2. Plan & Structure  
- Outline a clear structure for the report, using logical headings that align with each requirement.
- Decide on the order of content presentation for maximum clarity (e.g., start with data, then analysis, solutions, and KPIs).
- Allocate approximate word counts to each section to stay within the total word limit.
- Plan to use concise language, bullet points, and executive-friendly formatting.
- Determine what types of data, frameworks, solutions, and KPIs are generally relevant to logistics and operations research, without specifying details yet.
- Identify where to highlight trade-offs and risk considerations in solution recommendations.

3. Draft -> Self-Review -> Finalize  
- Draft the report according to the planned structure, ensuring each section addresses its specific requirement.
- Use clear, professional language suitable for executive readers.
- After drafting, review the report for completeness, logical flow, and adherence to the word limit.
- Check for clarity, conciseness, and effective use of headings and bullet points.
- Ensure all requirements are addressed and that recommendations are balanced and well-supported.
- Make necessary revisions before finalizing the report for submission.
