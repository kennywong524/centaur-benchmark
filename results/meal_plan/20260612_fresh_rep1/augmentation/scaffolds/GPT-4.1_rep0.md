**Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow**

1. Requirements Check  
Begin by thoroughly identifying all constraints, preferences, and must-avoid ingredients or flavors. Confirm dietary restrictions (allergies, intolerances), flavor sensitivities, and disliked foods. Note required inclusions (meal types, cuisines, snacks, grocery list, prep notes). Ensure understanding of the need for simplicity, affordability, and ingredient accessibility in an Australian context.

2. Plan & Structure  
Outline the meal plan’s organization: determine a clear daily structure covering breakfast, lunch, dinner, and snacks for each of the seven days. Map out how to rotate or vary proteins, carbohydrates, and cuisines while adhering to restrictions and preferences. Decide on the format for presenting the meal plan, grocery list, and prep notes for clarity and ease of use. Plan how to keep meals simple, budget-friendly, and mild in seasoning, with options for adjustability.

3. Draft -> Self-Review -> Finalize  
Draft each component: populate the meal plan, compile the grocery list, and write concise preparation notes. After drafting, cross-check every meal and ingredient for compliance with all dietary restrictions and preferences. Review for variety, simplicity, and cost-effectiveness. Ensure all instructions are clear, concise, and suitable for the intended user. Make necessary revisions, then finalize the content for completeness and user-friendliness.
