**Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow**

**Phase 1: Requirements Check**
Before generating any content, systematically audit all constraints from the task brief. Create a checklist covering: allergens to exclude (and their hidden sources), intolerances to exclude, flavor preferences to incorporate, demographic/regional context, budget and simplicity targets, sensory sensitivity considerations, and required output components (meals per day, snacks, grocery list, prep notes). Flag any potential conflicts or ambiguities between constraints (e.g., flavor profiles that commonly use excluded ingredients) so these can be proactively resolved during planning.

**Phase 2: Plan & Structure**
Map out the document architecture before writing any meal content. Decide how to organize the 7-day structure (table, day-by-day sections, etc.). Identify which flavor profiles will be assigned to which days to ensure variety. Establish a shortlist of compliant ingredient categories that satisfy all restrictions simultaneously. Determine how the grocery list will be organized (by category, by day, etc.) and how prep notes will be formatted (inline, appended, per-meal). Confirm the structure covers all four daily meal slots plus snacks for every day.

**Phase 3: Draft → Self-Review → Finalize**
Write the full meal plan following the planned structure. Then conduct a dedicated self-review pass: re-check every ingredient against the allergen/intolerance checklist, verify flavor variety across days, confirm budget and simplicity goals are met, and ensure seasoning guidance reflects supertaster sensitivity. Adjust any flagged items. Then finalize the grocery list and prep notes for completeness and consistency with the meals as written.
