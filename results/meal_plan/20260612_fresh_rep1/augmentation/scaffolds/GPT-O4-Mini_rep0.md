**Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow**

1. Requirements Check  
   - Collect all high-level instructions and constraints.  
   - Identify dietary restrictions, sensitivities, and flavor preferences without enumerating specifics.  
   - Note desired structure elements (daily slots, supplementary lists, annotations).  
   - Confirm any budgetary or simplicity requirements.  
   - Flag areas needing clarifications or assumptions.

2. Plan & Structure  
   - Outline the overall framework, dividing content into uniform segments (e.g., daily segments, meal categories, additional components).  
   - Sketch a modular template that can be repeated or adjusted per segment.  
   - Map out supporting materials (e.g., consolidated list, brief prep notes) by grouping related items.  
   - Allocate word counts or space to each section to ensure balance and completeness.  
   - Identify where reminders about adjustable seasoning and taste sensitivity will be inserted in the template.

3. Draft → Self-Review → Finalize  
   - Draft content strictly following the template, ensuring no omitted constraints.  
   - Perform a structured self-review:  
     • Check adherence to all identified restrictions and preferences.  
     • Verify consistency in formatting, terminology, and section headings.  
     • Confirm that supporting materials align with main content.  
   - Make revisions to address any gaps or formatting issues.  
   - Conduct a final quality check for clarity, completeness, and compliance.
