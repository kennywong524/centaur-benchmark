**Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow**

**Phase 1 — Requirements Check**
Begin by extracting every explicit constraint from the task and sorting them into categories: hard exclusions (allergens and intolerances), sensory limits (avoid extremes, accommodate heightened taste sensitivity), flavor and food preferences, structural deliverables (meals per day, snacks, grocery list, prep notes), and contextual factors (cost, simplicity, regional setting, single adult). Flag any ambiguity—such as overlap between substitution categories—and decide reasonable default assumptions. Build a quick exclusion checklist you can reuse as a filter in later phases.

**Phase 2 — Plan & Structure**
Decide the output format before writing: a day-by-day layout covering each required meal slot, followed by consolidated supporting sections. Plan how you will ensure variety across days while keeping ingredients overlapping enough to control cost and reduce waste. Map preferred cuisines and food types across the week so no single style dominates. Establish a verification routine—each planned item must pass the exclusion checklist and the sensory limits. Sketch where prep notes and the shopping list will draw from the daily plan so they stay internally consistent.

**Phase 3 — Draft → Self-Review → Finalize**
Draft the full plan following your structure. Then self-review systematically: re-run the exclusion checklist against every ingredient, confirm all required slots are filled, verify seasoning guidance supports adjustability, and check that the grocery list matches what the plan actually uses. Confirm tone is clear and practical, units suit the regional context, and nothing contradicts cost or simplicity goals. Correct gaps, then finalize.
