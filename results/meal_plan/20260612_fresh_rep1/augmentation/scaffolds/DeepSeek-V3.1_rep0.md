**Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow**

**1. Requirements Check**
Systematically parse the user's query to catalog all constraints and preferences. Identify dietary restrictions (shellfish, gluten, lactose, onion), flavor profiles (Mexican, Thai, Indian), and aversions (salty, sweet, super-taster sensitivity). Verify that every required output component is noted: a 7-day plan with three meals and snacks per day, a grocery list, and prep notes. This phase is purely about creating a checklist of 'must-haves' and 'must-avoids' to prevent errors in subsequent steps.

**2. Plan & Structure**
Design a flexible framework for the response. First, structure the document with clear sections for the weekly meal plan, the aggregated grocery list, and general prep notes. For the meal plan itself, develop a reusable daily template (Breakfast, Lunch, Dinner, Snacks) to ensure consistency. Plan to build meals around core compliant ingredients (e.g., rice, potatoes, specific meats, approved vegetables and seasonings) that can be flavored differently to meet the requested cuisines. This high-level structuring ensures logical flow and complete coverage before any content is generated.

**3. Draft -> Self-Review -> Finalize**
In the drafting stage, use the framework from Phase 2 to populate each section. Adhere strictly to the constraint checklist from Phase 1 for every ingredient choice and recipe idea. Then, conduct a meticulous self-review against the same checklist, scrutinizing the grocery list for any banned items and confirming flavors are mild and adjustable. Finally, polish the language for clarity and conciseness, ensuring the final output is well-organized and easy for the user to follow.
