**Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow**

**1. Requirements Check**  
- Carefully reread the prompt and list every explicit constraint (dietary restrictions, flavor preferences, nutritional balance, meal count, format of output, etc.).  
- Identify any implicit expectations such as readability, cultural relevance, or budget considerations.  
- Verify that no prohibited ingredients or preparation methods appear in the brief.  
- Confirm the required deliverables (daily meals, snack ideas, grocery list, prep notes) and the level of detail for each section.  
- Note any style guidelines (tone, brevity, use of headings) to ensure the final product aligns with the user’s expectations.

**2. Plan & Structure**  
- Outline a logical hierarchy: an overview, then a day‑by‑day schedule, followed by a consolidated grocery list and preparation tips.  
- Decide on consistent formatting for each day (e.g., Breakfast, Lunch, Dinner, Snack) and for the ancillary sections.  
- Map out how to incorporate the flavor profiles and dietary limits without violating constraints, planning for interchangeable seasoning options.  
- Sketch a rough allocation of protein, carbohydrate, and vegetable components to maintain variety and cost‑effectiveness across the week.  
- Determine the most efficient way to aggregate ingredients for the grocery list, grouping by store sections or usage frequency.

**3. Draft → Self‑Review → Finalize**  
- Write the first draft following the established outline, ensuring each meal respects the listed restrictions and preferences.  
- Conduct a thorough self‑review: cross‑check every ingredient against the allergy/intolerance list, verify portion balance, and confirm that seasoning suggestions are mild and adjustable.  
- Check for consistency in formatting, completeness of the grocery list, and clarity of prep notes.  
- Revise any omissions or ambiguities, then perform a final proofread for spelling, grammar, and adherence to the original constraints before delivering the polished output.
