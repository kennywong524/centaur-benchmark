**Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow**

1. Requirements Check  
- Carefully parse the user’s instructions to extract all high-level constraints (dietary restrictions, intolerances, flavor preferences, budget and simplicity goals, output components).  
- Verify that you understand the required deliverables (daily meal entries, snack suggestions, grocery list, prep notes).  
- Confirm any formatting or organizational guidelines (number of days, meal categories per day, list sections).  
- Flag any ambiguities or missing clarifications before proceeding.

2. Plan & Structure  
- Outline the overall document framework: define sections for each day, meals, snacks, and supporting lists/notes.  
- Sketch a template for a single day, ensuring placeholders for breakfast, lunch, dinner, snack, grocery items, and prep tips.  
- Ensure a modular approach so each day follows the same structure.  
- Map dietary constraints and preferences to each placeholder without filling in actual recipes.  
- Allocate space for brief prep guidance and a consolidated grocery list at the end.

3. Draft -> Self-Review -> Finalize  
- Draft content systematically by populating the template day by day, adhering strictly to constraints.  
- After drafting, perform a self-review checklist: check for compliance with all restrictions, consistent formatting, balanced meal distribution, and clarity.  
- Revise any sections that conflict with requirements or lack cohesion.  
- Finalize by ensuring the output is organized, clearly labeled, and ready for user consumption.
