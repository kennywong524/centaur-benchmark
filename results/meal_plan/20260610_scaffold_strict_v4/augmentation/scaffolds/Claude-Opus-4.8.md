**Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow**

**Phase 1 — Requirements Check**
Begin by extracting every explicit constraint from the task and listing them in categories: hard exclusions (allergens/ingredients to avoid), intolerances, flavor preferences, sensory cautions, budget/simplicity goals, and structural deliverables. Flag any potential conflicts (e.g., preferences that brush against restrictions) and note how you'll resolve them. Confirm the required scope: number of days, meals per day, and the supplementary outputs requested. Mark anything ambiguous so you handle it consistently rather than guessing inconsistently.

**Phase 2 — Plan & Structure**
Decide the overall organization before writing: a per-day layout for meals plus separate sections for the grocery list and prep notes. Choose a repeatable template for each day so formatting stays uniform. Plan a strategy for honoring all exclusions simultaneously and for keeping cost and effort low (e.g., reusing ingredients across days). Sequence your work so the meal entries come first, then aggregate ingredients into the list, then derive prep notes. Map preferences and sensory cautions to how seasoning will be presented as adjustable.

**Phase 3 — Draft → Self-Review → Finalize**
Draft each section against your template. Then self-review with a checklist: scan every item for prohibited ingredients, verify each day has all required meals plus snacks, confirm the grocery list matches what the plan actually uses, and check seasoning notes reflect the sensitivity guidance. Verify tone, simplicity, and budget alignment. Correct gaps, remove redundancy, and finalize only after all constraints from Phase 1 are satisfied.
