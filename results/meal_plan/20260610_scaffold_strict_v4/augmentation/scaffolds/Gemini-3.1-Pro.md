- Target audience: 31-year-old Australian adult.
- Dietary restrictions (strict): No shellfish, no gluten, no lactose, no onion.
- Flavor/Ingredient preferences: Meat, potatoes, Mexican, Thai, Indian.
- Sensory/Taste constraints: Avoid very salty, avoid very sweet, accommodate super-taster sensitivity (mild, adjustable seasonings).

- Required outputs: 7-day meal plan (breakfast, lunch, dinner, simple snacks daily), a consolidated grocery list, and brief prep notes.

- Outline the 7-day schedule. For each day, create placeholders for Breakfast, Lunch, Dinner, and Snacks.

- Map out the flavor profiles across the week, ensuring a rotation of Mexican, Thai, and Indian inspirations, adapted to be mild and low in salt/sugar.
- Structure the grocery list by category (Produce, Meat, Pantry, etc.) to ensure cost-effectiveness and ease of shopping in an Australian context.
- Plan a brief section for meal prep notes, focusing on time-saving strategies and batch cooking.

**3. Draft -> Self-Review -> Finalize**
- Draft the 7-day meal plan, populating each meal with specific, compliant recipes or food combinations.
- Draft the grocery list and prep notes based on the meal plan.
- Self-Review: Check every single meal and ingredient against the strict allergy and intolerance list (no gluten, lactose, shellfish, or onion). Verify that the flavors match the requested cuisines, the seasonings are described as mild/adjustable, and the overall plan feels budget-friendly and simple.
- Finalize the output for clarity, ensuring all requested components are present and clearly formatted.
