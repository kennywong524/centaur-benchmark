**Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow**

**1. Requirements Check**  
- Re‑read the prompt carefully and extract every explicit constraint (dietary restrictions, flavor preferences, nutritional limits, format expectations, etc.).  
- Create a checklist that pairs each requirement with a verification point (e.g., “no prohibited ingredient appears in any meal”).  
- Identify any implicit needs such as balance across meals, variety, or budget considerations, and note them for later reference.  
- Confirm the required deliverables (daily meal schedule, snack ideas, grocery list, prep notes) and the expected level of detail for each.

**2. Plan & Structure**  
- Draft a high‑level outline that maps the week (7 days) to the required sections (breakfast, lunch, dinner, snack).  
- Decide on a consistent template for each day (e.g., meal name, brief description, key ingredients, simple preparation steps).  
- Allocate the allowed flavors and protein sources across the week to ensure variety while respecting the intolerance and allergy list.  
- Sketch a master grocery list organized by category (produce, proteins, pantry staples, spices) and note any items that need substitution.  
- Outline brief prep notes that cover batch cooking, storage tips, and seasoning adjustments for a sensitive palate.

**3. Draft → Self‑Review → Finalize**  
- Populate the template with placeholder meals, ensuring each entry aligns with the checklist from Phase 1.  
- Perform a systematic self‑review: cross‑check every meal against the restriction list, verify the grocery list covers all ingredients, and ensure the language is concise and user‑friendly.  
- Adjust seasoning guidance to be mild and adaptable, and confirm snack options are simple and inexpensive.  
- Incorporate any necessary edits, then produce the clean, final version that meets all stipulated requirements.
