**Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow**

**1. Requirements Check**

Before generating any content, systematically audit all constraints from the task brief. Create a mental checklist separating:
- Hard exclusions (allergens, intolerances, prohibited ingredients)
- Soft preferences (cuisine styles, flavor profiles, texture sensitivities)
- Format requirements (meal types per day, supplementary outputs like grocery list and prep notes)
- Practical parameters (budget, simplicity, target demographic)

Cross-reference each constraint category against the others to identify potential conflicts or edge cases (e.g., certain cuisine staples that may contain hidden excluded ingredients). Flag any ambiguities before proceeding.

**2. Plan & Structure**

Establish a clear organizational framework before writing any meal content. Decide on:
- How days will be laid out (table, list, headers)
- How variety across cuisine preferences will be distributed across seven days
- How the grocery list will be organized (by category, by day, alphabetically)
- How prep notes will be scoped (brief, actionable, non-repetitive)

Map out structural slots first without filling in content, ensuring all required components (breakfast, lunch, dinner, snacks, grocery list, prep notes) are accounted for across the full output.

**3. Draft → Self-Review → Finalize**

Write a complete first draft following the structure. Then conduct a dedicated review pass specifically checking:
- Every ingredient against the exclusion checklist
- Seasoning guidance aligns with super-taster sensitivity
- Cost and simplicity remain consistent throughout
- No structural components are missing

Revise any flagged items, then produce the final clean output.
