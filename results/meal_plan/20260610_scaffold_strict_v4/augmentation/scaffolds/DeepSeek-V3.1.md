**Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow**

**Requirements Check**
First, meticulously parse the user's constraints. Identify all absolute prohibitions: shellfish, gluten, lactose, onion. Note the flavor preferences (Mexican, Thai, Indian) and the requirement for mild, adjustable seasoning. Confirm the required outputs: a 7-day plan covering all meals and snacks, a grocery list, and prep notes. Verify that all criteria, including budget and simplicity, are understood before proceeding to the planning stage.

**Plan & Structure**
Next, design a framework for the response. Structure the output into clear sections: a weekly calendar of meals, a consolidated grocery list organized by category (e.g., produce, proteins, pantry), and brief preparation instructions. For the meal plan, brainstorm dishes that align with the allowed cuisines while using safe ingredients (e.g., corn tortillas, coconut milk, specific spices). Ensure variety across the week and that each meal component adheres to the dietary restrictions.

**Draft -> Self-Review -> Finalize**
Draft the content according to the structure. Systematically review the draft against each user constraint, line by line, to catch any accidental inclusion of allergens or non-compliant ingredients. Verify that seasoning suggestions are mild and adjustable. Check for completeness, ensuring all meals, snacks, the list, and notes are present. Finalize the text only after this rigorous validation pass.
