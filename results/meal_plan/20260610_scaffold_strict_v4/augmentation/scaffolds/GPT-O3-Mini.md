Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow

1. Requirements Check
• Begin by thoroughly reviewing the provided task details and identifying all hard constraints and specific conditions. 
• List out key requirements such as dietary allergies, flavor preferences, and ingredient exclusions. 
• Ensure all restrictions, including flavor guidelines and preparation simplicity, are clearly captured.
• Verify that any regulatory or common dietary considerations are noted without delving into specifics.

2. Plan & Structure
• Develop a high-level outline that organizes the overall approach into separate sections (e.g., daily meals, snack options, grocery list, and prep notes).
• Define how to structure each day’s entry, ensuring consistency across the seven-day framework.
• Establish placeholders for major components such as breakfast, lunch, dinner, and snacks, and plan for segmented notes on grocery and prep.
• Identify where to incorporate checks for flavor intensity, seasoning adjustments, and allergen avoidance, ensuring the plan remains flexible.

3. Draft -> Self-Review -> Finalize
• Begin by drafting an outline that mirrors the planned structure, keeping all constraints in focus.
• Conduct a self-review to compare the draft against the initial requirements checklist, making sure every instruction and restriction is addressed in a skeletal form.
