**Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow**

1. Requirements Check
- Read the brief carefully and extract all constraints: dietary restrictions, intolerances, flavor preferences, budget and simplicity goals, and output components required.
- Identify missing or ambiguous items to clarify (portion sizes, exact budget limit, cooking equipment available, preferred meal timing).
- Define non-negotiables to enforce during creation (items to exclude, intolerance handling, sensitivity to strong seasonings).
- Set success criteria and acceptance checks (compliance, cost threshold, ease of prep).
- Decide deliverable format and file structure (daily layout, grocery list grouping, prep notes length).

2. Plan & Structure
- Create a reusable daily template: slots for breakfast, lunch, dinner, and snacks; short prep notes and portion indicators.
- Outline high-level strategies: rotation of flavors, batch-cooking opportunities, simple swaps for allergens, and mild/adjustable seasoning notes.
- Design the grocery list format: grouped by store section, with quantity estimates and optional substitutions.
- Plan time-saving prep steps and storage guidance placeholders to fill during drafting.
- Prepare a checklist of items to flag (potential cross-contamination risks, ingredient substitutions, seasoning adjustments).

3. Draft -> Self-Review -> Finalize
- Draft all seven days using the template; fill grocery list and prep notes concurrently to keep quantities consistent.
- Perform a structured self-review using the checklist: verify all exclusions, check for clarity of prep steps, ensure seasoning is described as adjustable, confirm grocery completeness and cost-awareness, and validate variety across days.
- Iterate edits for clarity, brevity, and compliance; produce final files (meal plan, grocery list, prep notes) and add a short summary of how to adjust seasonings/portions.
