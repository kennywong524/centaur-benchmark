Below is a cheap, simple 7‑day meal plan that avoids shellfish, gluten, lactose and onion. It uses meat, potatoes and Mexican / Thai / Indian–style flavors, keeps seasoning mild and adjustable for super‑taster sensitivity, and avoids very salty or very sweet foods. Where a normally common ingredient could contain gluten, lactose or onion (for example regular soy sauce, stock cubes or spice mixes), I’ve noted label checks or alternatives. Each day lists breakfast, lunch, dinner and one or two simple snacks.

Quick notes before the plan
- Always check labels: look for “gluten‑free” on oats, tortillas, soy sauce/tamari, miso and stock; check “lactose‑free” if buying dairy replacements. Avoid anything listing wheat, barley, rye or malt.
- No onion or onion powder anywhere. I also avoid shallots/leeks/green onion to be safe.
- Keep seasoning mild: start with very small amounts of salt and spice, taste, and add more at the table if needed. Offer chili flakes, extra tamari or lime at the table for extra heat/umami.
- Use gluten‑free tamari or coconut aminos instead of regular soy sauce. Use canned coconut milk for Thai/Indian dishes.
- If you want garlic flavor but don’t tolerate alliums, you can omit garlic or use a small amount of garlic‑infused oil (not suitable if you’re sensitive to all alliums). I leave garlic optional for each recipe. Asafoetida (hing) is a traditional onion/garlic substitute for some Indian dishes — only use if you can obtain a labeled gluten‑free product.

Day 1 (Mexican, simple)
- Breakfast: Overnight oats (gluten‑free oats + plant milk, 1 tsp chia, sliced banana, cinnamon); add small handful of berries if available.
- Lunch: Roast chicken thigh (leftover) + potato salad (roasted potato cubes, chopped cucumber, chopped tomato, chopped cilantro, lime & 1 tsp olive oil, pepper). Light salt—taste first.
- Dinner: Mild beef mince tacos: brown minced beef with ground cumin, smoked paprika, mild chili powder (very small pinch), salt to taste; serve on warmed gluten‑free corn tortillas with tomato‑cilantro salsa (tomato + cilantro + lime + pepper + pinch salt if wanted) and mashed avocado.
- Snacks: Apple; handful of roasted peanuts or mixed nuts.

Day 2 (Thai light)
- Breakfast: Scrambled eggs with baby spinach and tomato; small sprinkle of black pepper.
- Lunch: Leftover tacos as a bowl: warmed rice + leftover minced beef + avocado + tomato.
- Dinner: Mild coconut chicken curry: diced chicken thighs, 1 can coconut milk, diced potato, sliced carrots, grated ginger (~1 tsp), turmeric (¼–½ tsp), ground coriander & cumin (each ¼ tsp), salt to taste; simmer until potatoes tender. Serve with rice. Add lime at table.
- Snacks: Banana; rice crackers (gluten‑free).

Day 3 (Indian style)
- Breakfast: Potato & spinach hash: diced pan‑fried potato with turmeric (pinch), cumin (small pinch), pepper; top with a fried egg.
- Lunch: Chickpea salad: canned chickpeas (rinsed), chopped cucumber, tomato, coriander, lemon juice, a drizzle of olive oil, ground cumin (very small pinch). Mild seasoning.
- Dinner: Keema‑style mince: minced beef or lamb, tomato, turmeric, ground cumin & coriander, garam masala (low amount), fresh ginger; simmer and serve over rice or with roasted potatoes. Optional: a pinch of hing (asafoetida) if you use it.
- Snacks: Plain plant‑yogurt (unsweetened) with a few sliced banana or berries.

Day 4 (Mexican / roast)
- Breakfast: Toasted gluten‑free corn tortilla with mashed avocado, squeeze lime, and a soft‑boiled or fried egg.
- Lunch: Leftover keema over rice or as a salad with lettuce and tomato.
- Dinner: Lime‑paprika baked chicken drumsticks, roast potatoes and steamed green beans (season lightly with pepper and small pinch of salt).
- Snacks: Carrot sticks with a small pot of garlic‑free hummus (or mashed avocado).

Day 5 (Thai / peanut)
- Breakfast: Smoothie: plant milk + banana + a small spoon peanut butter + spinach (optional) — keep tastes mild.
- Lunch: Rice noodle salad (use rice noodles): shredded poached chicken, grated carrot, cucumber ribbons, chopped cilantro, lime juice and 1 tsp gluten‑free tamari or coconut aminos, a light drizzle of oil. Keep dressing minimal; add more at table if wanted.
- Dinner: Mild peanut satay: make sauce from peanut butter + coconut milk + lime + small tamari splash + grated ginger; stir into cooked chicken pieces and steamed broccoli; serve with rice. Start with 1 tbsp peanut butter per 1 cup coconut milk and adjust, keep it mild.
- Snacks: Boiled egg; small handful of almonds.

Day 6 (Indian / hearty)
- Breakfast: Chickpea “scramble”: mashed chickpeas, turmeric, cumin and tomato, warmed; serve on roasted potato slices or gluten‑free toast.
- Lunch: Leftover peanut chicken over rice or with salad.
- Dinner: Aloo (potato) & beef curry: chunky potatoes, minced beef or diced beef, canned tomatoes, turmeric, cumin, coriander, garam masala (low), water to simmer; serve with rice. Taste gently and add salt sparingly.
- Snacks: Popcorn (air‑popped, light salt) or an orange.

Day 7 (simple & flexible)
- Breakfast: Warm porridge (gluten‑free oats) with plant milk, cinnamon, and sliced banana.
- Lunch: Leftover curry or chicken in a gluten‑free wrap with lettuce, grated carrot and a smear of mashed avocado.
- Dinner: Grilled steak or beef patties (lightly seasoned with pepper, herbs), simple mashed potato (with plant milk and olive oil instead of butter), and steamed greens (broccoli or spinach) with lemon.
- Snacks: Sliced apple with peanut butter; plain plant‑yogurt if desired.
