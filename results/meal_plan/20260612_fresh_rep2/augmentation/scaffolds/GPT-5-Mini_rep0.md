**Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow**

1. Requirements Check
- Parse the brief into explicit constraints (dietary exclusions, intolerances, flavor preferences, sensory sensitivity, budget, simplicity, 7-day scope, meals per day, grocery list, prep notes).
- List hard exclusions and intolerances to enforce as non-negotiable.
- Identify user preferences to prioritize in menu development.
- Define measurable targets: daily meals/snacks count, approximate budget ceiling, shopping frequency, prep time limits, and storage/reheating constraints.
- Note safety checks: cross-contact risk, label-reading rules, and substitutions policy for allergens and intolerances.
- Decide acceptable seasoning strategy: mild baseline with adjustable options and portion controls.

2. Plan & Structure
- Create a skeleton layout: day-by-day slots for breakfast/lunch/dinner/snack and a consolidated grocery list and prep notes section.
- Determine variety strategy (rotate proteins, starches, flavors) and balance (macro variety, simple snacks).
- Establish format conventions: how to present each meal (name, brief description, serving size, prep time, make-ahead notes, seasoning options).
- Define grocery list structure: grouped by store section, quantities/units, staples vs perishables, items for batch prep.
- Set prep-note templates: batch steps, storage, reheating, safety, and customizable seasoning guidance.

3. Draft -> Self-Review -> Finalize
- Draft: Populate the skeleton with placeholders for each meal, snacks, grocery items, and prep steps without final quantities yet.
- Self-review: Verify every item against exclusions and sensitivities, check for repetition, budget feasibility, prep time, and clarity of seasoning adjustments.
- Finalize: Convert placeholders into the final formatted deliverables (7-day plan, consolidated grocery list with quantities, succinct prep timeline and notes), and include a brief user-facing instruction on adjusting seasoning and substitutions.
