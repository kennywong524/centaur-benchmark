**Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow**

1. Requirements Check  
   - Carefully review all dietary restrictions, allergies, and intolerances mentioned.  
   - Note the specific food preferences, flavor profiles, and cuisines to emphasize.  
   - Identify all foods and ingredients to avoid, including both allergens and those causing intolerance.  
   - Confirm the need for mild seasoning and the avoidance of very salty or very sweet options due to super-taster sensitivity.  
   - Ensure understanding of required meal components: breakfast, lunch, dinner, snacks, grocery list, and preparation notes for each day.

2. Plan & Structure  
   - Outline a 7-day framework with dedicated sections for each day and meal (breakfast, lunch, dinner, snacks).  
   - Brainstorm meal ideas that align with the restrictions and preferences, ensuring variety and simplicity.  
   - Allocate cuisines and flavors (Mexican, Thai, Indian, etc.) thoughtfully across the week for balance.  
   - Prepare a master grocery list, grouping items logically (produce, proteins, pantry, etc.), and draft concise prep notes for each meal.  
   - Double-check all planned meals to confirm exclusion of restricted ingredients and compliance with seasoning guidelines.

3. Draft -> Self-Review -> Finalize  
   - Compose the full meal plan with clear meal and snack entries for each day, along with the grocery list and prep notes.  
   - Review the draft to ensure all requirements are met, checking for ingredient compliance, flavor moderation, and overall simplicity.  
   - Revise as needed for clarity, completeness, and user-friendliness.  
   - Finalize the document, confirming that all sections are present and organized for easy use.
