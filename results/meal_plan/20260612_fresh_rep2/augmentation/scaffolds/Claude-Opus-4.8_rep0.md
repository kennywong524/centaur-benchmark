**Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow**

**1. Requirements Check**
Begin by extracting every explicit constraint from the task and sorting them into categories: hard exclusions (allergens and intolerances), flavor preferences, sensory limits, budget/simplicity goals, and required deliverable components. Build a checklist you can revisit. Flag any constraints that may conflict or need substitution logic (e.g., ingredients commonly hidden in processed foods). Note the regional context and which meal slots and supporting sections must appear. Confirm coverage scope before generating any content.

**2. Plan & Structure**
Decide the overall layout: a per-day grid covering each required meal and snack slot, followed by the consolidated supporting sections. Plan how you'll ensure variety across the week while keeping ingredients overlapping enough to stay cheap and reduce waste. Establish a reusable filtering rule that screens each item against your exclusion checklist, and a strategy for keeping seasoning adjustable. Sketch the order in which you'll fill sections so the grocery list flows logically from chosen meals. Set a consistency standard for formatting and prep-note brevity.

**3. Draft → Self-Review → Finalize**
Draft each day against your structure, then pause to self-review systematically. Cross-check every ingredient against each hard exclusion individually—don't assume. Verify all meal/snack slots are filled, the grocery list matches what the plan actually uses, and prep notes stay simple. Confirm flavor preferences appear and sensory limits are respected. Check budget realism and internal consistency. Correct gaps or contradictions, tighten wording, then finalize once all checklist items pass.
