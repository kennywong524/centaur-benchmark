**Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow**

1. Requirements Check  
   - Carefully read the brief to extract all stated constraints (dietary restrictions, flavor preferences, budget limits, format requirements).  
   - List any ambiguities or missing details that could affect solution quality (e.g., target calorie range, equipment availability).  
   - Confirm understanding of the audience and tone (simple, cost-effective, mild seasoning).  
   - Ensure you know what deliverables are expected (daily meals, snacks, grocery list, prep notes).

2. Plan & Structure  
   - Sketch an overall outline: sections for each day’s breakfast, lunch, dinner, snacks; a consolidated grocery list; brief prep instructions.  
   - Decide on a logical layout (table, bullet points, day-by-day narrative) that supports easy readability and quick reference.  
   - Allocate distinct slots for recipe names, ingredients, prep time, and serving notes.  
   - Incorporate a mechanism for adjustable seasoning in each entry (e.g., “season to taste”) without specifying exact measurements.  
   - Map out word or character limits for each section to maintain brevity and clarity.

3. Draft -> Self-Review -> Finalize  
   - Draft all sections according to the structure, using placeholder labels rather than full recipes.  
   - Conduct a self-review checklist: verify all constraints are respected, check consistency in formatting, confirm clarity and simplicity.  
   - Revise to eliminate any accidental inclusion of prohibited items or overly complex language.  
   - Finalize by polishing headings, ensuring uniform style, and preparing the document for delivery.
