**Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow**

1.  **Requirements Check**
    Systematically parse all user constraints and preferences. Categorize them into non-negotiable health restrictions (allergies, intolerances), strong preferences (cuisine types, dislikes), and logistical parameters (budget, simplicity, 7-day scope). Verify that every meal component and grocery item will be cross-checked against the allergen and intolerance list. Confirm the plan includes all requested elements: three daily meals, snacks, a consolidated grocery list, and basic prep instructions.

2.  **Plan & Structure**
    Develop a high-level framework for the week. Allocate cuisine types (Mexican, Thai, Indian) to specific days to ensure variety while adhering to constraints. For each day, conceptualize simple, overlapping meals to minimize waste and effort (e.g., dinner leftovers for next-day lunch). Structure the final output by first outlining the seven-day meal and snack schedule, followed by a categorized grocery list derived from the planned recipes, and concluding with brief, actionable preparation notes.

3.  **Draft -> Self-Review -> Finalize**
    Compose a first draft following the established structure. Immediately conduct a compliance review, scrutinizing every ingredient for allergens and intolerances. Assess the plan for flavor balance, cost, and simplicity. Refine the draft by substituting any non-compliant items, simplifying complex steps, and ensuring seasonings are mild and adjustable. Finalize the text, ensuring clarity and that all user requirements are explicitly met before delivery.
