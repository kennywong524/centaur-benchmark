**Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow**

**1. Requirements Check**

Before generating any content, catalogue all constraints systematically:
- Identify every dietary restriction (allergens, intolerances) and create a clear exclusion list.
- Identify every preference (cuisine styles, protein types, specific ingredients) and create an inclusion list.
- Note sensory considerations (super-taster sensitivity, seasoning approach) and how they interact with cuisine preferences.
- Confirm the output components required: number of days, meal types per day, supplementary sections (grocery list, prep notes).
- Flag any potential conflicts between constraints and preferences that will need careful navigation throughout.

**2. Plan & Structure**

Establish the organizational framework before writing:
- Decide on a day-by-day layout and consistent formatting for each day's meals.
- Map cuisine themes or flavor profiles across the seven days to ensure variety without repetition.
- Sketch which meal slots will carry which cuisine influences, ensuring all preferred styles appear.
- Outline the grocery list structure (e.g., grouped by category) and determine the scope of prep notes.
- Verify the skeleton against the exclusion list before proceeding.

**3. Draft → Self-Review → Finalize**

- Write the full meal plan following the established structure.
- Self-review pass one: check every ingredient against the exclusion list line by line.
- Self-review pass two: assess budget-friendliness, simplicity, and sensory suitability of seasoning levels.
- Self-review pass three: confirm all required output sections are present and consistent.
- Make corrections, then produce the final clean version.
