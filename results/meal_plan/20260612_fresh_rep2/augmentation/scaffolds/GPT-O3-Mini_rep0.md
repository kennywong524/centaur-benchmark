Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow

Phase 1: Requirements Check
• Begin by reviewing the task requirements to ensure full understanding of the conditions, dietary restrictions, and flavor inclinations.  
• Identify all explicit constraints (e.g., allergens, food intolerances, flavor preferences) without incorporating final content specifics.  
• Note any structural elements required (daily schedule components, grocery list, preparation notes) and confirm all essential elements are acknowledged.  
• Check for any implicit guidelines and conditions that might impact the overall approach.

Phase 2: Plan & Structure
• Organize the components into a clear and logical structure: layout day-by-day meal segmentation for breakfast, lunch, dinner, and snacks, along with additional segments for the grocery list and preparation notes.  
• Establish a framework for including provisions on taste sensitivity adjustments while aligning with the flavor themes.  
• Outline sections for the weekly plan, ensuring each section can accommodate potential modifications once further details are reviewed.  
• Prepare a flexible strategy to manage the complex intersection of dietary restrictions and meal variety without diving into solution specifics.

Phase 3: Draft -> Self-Review -> Finalize
• Create an initial draft outline using the established structure, emphasizing process clarity and adherence to the constraints.  
• Conduct a thorough self-review of the draft to verify that all hard constraints and task expectations are met at a process level.  
• Adjust the outline based on review feedback, ensuring completeness before finalizing the workflow plan for implementation.
