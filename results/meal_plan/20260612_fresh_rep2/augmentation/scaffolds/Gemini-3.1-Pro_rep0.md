**Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow**

**1. Requirements Check**
- Target Audience: 31-year-old Australian adult.
- Dietary Restrictions (Strict): No shellfish, no gluten, no lactose, no onion.
- Preferences: Meat, potatoes, Mexican, Thai, Indian flavors.
- Aversions: Very salty, very sweet, strong/overwhelming flavors (super-taster sensitivity requires mild, adjustable seasoning).
- Budget/Complexity: Cheap and simple.
- Output Requirements: 7-day meal plan (breakfast, lunch, dinner, simple snacks daily), a grocery list, and brief prep notes.

**2. Plan & Structure**
- **Meal Plan Grid:** Outline 7 days. Assign simple, budget-friendly meals for breakfast, lunch, dinner, and snacks. Ensure every meal strictly adheres to the allergy and intolerance constraints while incorporating the requested flavor profiles (Mexican, Thai, Indian) in a mild, super-taster-friendly way.
- **Grocery List:** Categorize ingredients (e.g., Produce, Meat, Pantry) to match the meal plan. Verify all items are accessible in Australia, cheap, and compliant with all dietary restrictions (e.g., gluten-free grains, lactose-free alternatives, onion-free aromatics).
- **Prep Notes:** Draft a brief section on how to batch-cook or prepare ingredients efficiently to keep the plan simple. Address how to handle seasoning adjustments.

**3. Draft -> Self-Review -> Finalize**
- **Draft:** Write out the 7-day plan, the categorized grocery list, and the prep notes based on the structure. Focus on clarity and simplicity.
- **Self-Review:** Scrutinize every single ingredient against the strict constraints: Is there any hidden gluten, lactose, shellfish, or onion? Are the meals cheap and easy? Are the flavors represented but kept mild? Are all 7 days fully populated with three meals and snacks?
- **Finalize:** Polish the formatting for readability, ensuring clear headings and bullet points, before generating the final output.
