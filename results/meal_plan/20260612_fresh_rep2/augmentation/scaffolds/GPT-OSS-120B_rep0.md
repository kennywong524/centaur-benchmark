**Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow**

**1. Requirements Check**  
- Read the brief thoroughly; list every constraint (dietary restrictions, flavor preferences, nutritional balance, portion size, budget, preparation time).  
- Verify that all mandatory elements are identified (daily meals, snack options, grocery list, prep notes).  
- Flag any ambiguous points and note questions for clarification before proceeding.  
- Cross‑reference the constraints to ensure no prohibited ingredients or cooking methods will appear later.

**2. Plan & Structure**  
- Sketch a high‑level outline: seven days, each with breakfast, lunch, dinner, and two snack slots.  
- Decide on a consistent format for each day (e.g., heading, meal description, portion guidance).  
- Group similar dishes to reuse ingredients efficiently, supporting the “cheap” requirement.  
- Draft a separate section for the consolidated grocery list, organized by category (proteins, vegetables, pantry items, spices).  
- Add a brief “prep notes” block that outlines batch cooking, storage tips, and any quick‑assembly steps.  
- Ensure the outline leaves placeholders for adjustable seasoning notes to accommodate sensitivity.

**3. Draft → Self‑Review → Finalize**  
- Populate the outline with concise, original meal ideas, respecting all constraints.  
- After the first draft, perform a self‑review checklist: ingredient compliance, flavor variety, budget adherence, clarity of instructions, and readability.  
- Revise any sections that miss a requirement or could cause confusion; tighten language for brevity.  
- Conduct a final proofread for consistency (formatting, spelling, measurement units) and confirm the grocery list matches the meals.  
- Deliver the polished document in the agreed structure, ready for user consumption.
