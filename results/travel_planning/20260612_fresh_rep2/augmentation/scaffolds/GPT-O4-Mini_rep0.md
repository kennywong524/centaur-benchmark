**Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow**

1. Requirements Check  
- Identify and list all explicit constraints (trip duration, budget ceiling, exclusion items, departure point).  
- Determine traveler’s preferences and priorities (pace, interests, accommodation style, dietary needs).  
- Formulate clarifying questions to resolve ambiguities (exact travel dates, preferred activity types, flexibility in budget categories).  
- Document any assumptions needed to proceed (currency rates, average transportation costs, meal pricing tiers).

2. Plan & Structure  
- Sketch a high-level daily framework: morning/afternoon/evening segments for each day.  
- Allocate broad budget categories across days: lodging, local transport, meals, activities, incidentals.  
- Designate placeholders for estimated costs, clearly marking them as “estimates.”  
- Sequence locations or experiences to optimize travel flow and time management.  
- Reserve contingency buffers in schedule and budget for unexpected changes or opportunities.

3. Draft -> Self-Review -> Finalize  
- Draft a provisional itinerary using the structured outline and inserted cost estimates.  
- Conduct a self-review checklist: alignment with constraints, clarity of labels, consistency of assumptions, balanced pacing.  
- Revise any segments where budget overruns or timing conflicts appear.  
- Ensure each assumption and outstanding question is noted.  
- Produce the final deliverable with a concise summary of assumptions, clarifications, and a clearly budget-aware, day-by-day plan.
