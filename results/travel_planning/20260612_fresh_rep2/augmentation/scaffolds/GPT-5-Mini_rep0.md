**Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow**

1. Requirements Check
- Identify and list all required traveler inputs to collect before planning (dates, flexibility, travel style, mobility/health constraints, accommodation preferences, dietary/allergy needs, loyalty programs, must-see priorities, risk tolerance, and any restrictions).
- Prepare clarifying questions to ask the traveler; prioritize those that affect cost and schedule.
- Confirm constraints to respect (total budget, excluded costs, departure city, timing window).
- Determine necessary external checks (visa/entry rules, travel advisories, airline schedules, seasonal closures) and note these as items to verify rather than assumptions.
- Decide which assumptions you will need to state explicitly if the traveler does not answer (e.g., preferred class of travel, accommodation tier, daily activity pacing, contingency buffer).

2. Plan & Structure
- Define the deliverable structure: lead with clarifying questions and assumptions, then a provisional daily overview, followed by cost estimates by category and a short logistics/booking checklist.
- Break the itinerary into logical components: transport, lodging, daily activities, meals, local transport, misc. Reserve budget categories and indicate that all amounts will be labeled as estimates.
- Establish prioritization rules for spending (where to save vs. splurge) and a contingency buffer policy.
- Identify reliable sources and tools to consult for prices and availability; plan how to annotate each estimate with provenance and confidence level.

3. Draft -> Self-Review -> Finalize
- Draft the provisional plan: start with unanswered clarifying questions and explicit assumptions; present a day-by-day skeleton with brief activity blocks and estimated costs per category.
- Self-review checklist: verify internal consistency, confirm all assumptions are stated, ensure every estimate is labeled, check arithmetic and totals, flag uncertainties and alternatives, ensure clarity for a non-expert reader.
- Finalize: prepare a concise summary, include next steps and booking priorities, attach sources and confidence notes, and end with clear follow-up questions for the traveler.
