**Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow**

**1. Requirements Check**
Begin by parsing every explicit constraint in the task: trip duration, traveler count, timing, total budget and what it excludes, departure point, and destination. List each deliverable the prompt demands—stated assumptions, clarifying questions, a provisional plan, and labeled estimates. Note tone and formatting expectations (budget-aware, realistic). Flag any ambiguities (e.g., undefined preferences, fixed dates, lodging style) that should surface as clarifying questions rather than silent guesses. Confirm you understand the ordering the task requests: assumptions and questions appear before the plan.

**2. Plan & Structure**
Sketch a skeleton before writing. Decide section order: assumptions block, clarifying questions, then the day-by-day plan, then a budget summary. Plan how you'll allocate the budget across major categories so the total stays coherent—reserve a method, not the numbers. Decide how to label estimates consistently and where to acknowledge uncertainty. Determine a repeatable per-day template (timing, activity grouping, cost note) so the itinerary reads uniformly. Identify which figures must be cross-checked for internal consistency.

**3. Draft → Self-Review → Finalize**
Draft each section following your skeleton, marking all figures as estimates as you go. After drafting, self-review against the Requirements Check list: are assumptions explicit, questions present, estimates labeled, and the running total within budget? Verify arithmetic adds up and excluded items aren't counted. Check that the plan is genuinely budget-aware and realistic. Trim redundancy, ensure ordering matches the request, then finalize with a clean, scannable format.
