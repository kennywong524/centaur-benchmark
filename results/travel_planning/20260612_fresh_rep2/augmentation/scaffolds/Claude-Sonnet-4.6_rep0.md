**Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow**

**Phase 1: Requirements Check**
Before producing any content, audit the task prompt for ambiguities and missing inputs. Identify what is explicitly stated versus what must be assumed. Generate a concise list of clarifying questions covering areas such as travel dates, accommodation preferences, dietary needs, mobility considerations, interests, and any costs already secured. Clearly separate confirmed constraints from open variables. Document every assumption you intend to make so they can be surfaced transparently in the final output.

**Phase 2: Plan & Structure**
Decide on the logical architecture of the response before writing prose. Determine what sections are needed (e.g., assumptions block, clarifying questions block, budget breakdown framework, day-by-day structure). Establish the order and hierarchy of those sections. Decide how estimates will be labeled consistently throughout. Sketch the skeleton of the response—headings, sub-headings, and placeholder categories—without filling in content yet. Confirm the skeleton satisfies all stated requirements: single traveler, five days, origin city, total budget cap, budget-awareness, and estimate labeling.

**Phase 3: Draft → Self-Review → Finalize**
Write the full response following the approved structure. After drafting, conduct a self-review pass: verify assumptions are explicitly stated, clarifying questions appear before the provisional plan, all figures are labeled as estimates, the running total stays within the stated budget, and no section is missing. Check tone and clarity. Make targeted revisions, then produce the finalized response.
