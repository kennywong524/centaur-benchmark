**Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow**

**1. Requirements Check**
*   Verify the core parameters: 5-day duration, solo traveler, destination Tokyo, departure from San Francisco, travel date "next month."
*   Confirm the financial constraint: $2,500 USD total budget (excluding passport costs).
*   Ensure the required output components are noted: assumptions, clarifying questions, and a provisional 5-day itinerary.
*   Note the formatting and tone constraints: realistic estimates must be used and explicitly labeled as such, and the entire plan must be budget-aware.

**2. Plan & Structure**
*   **Introduction:** Plan to state the overarching assumptions (e.g., exchange rates, flight class, accommodation type) and list the clarifying questions (e.g., dietary restrictions, specific interests, pace of travel) before presenting the itinerary.
*   **Budget Breakdown:** Outline a strategy to allocate the $2,500 USD across major categories (flights from SFO, accommodation, food, local transport, activities, and a contingency fund).
*   **Itinerary Structure:** Structure the 5-day plan logically, dedicating each day to specific neighborhoods or themes to minimize local transit time and costs.
*   **Cost Tracking:** Plan to include estimated costs for each day or activity to demonstrate adherence to the budget constraint.

**3. Draft -> Self-Review -> Finalize**
*   **Draft:** Write the assumptions, clarifying questions, and the day-by-day itinerary. Integrate the estimated costs clearly throughout the text.
*   **Self-Review:** Check against the requirements. Is the budget strictly under $2,500 USD? Are flights from SFO accounted for? Are all costs explicitly labeled as estimates? Are the assumptions and questions positioned *before* the provisional plan? 
*   **Finalize:** Refine the formatting for readability, ensuring a professional and helpful tone. Verify that the math in the budget breakdown aligns with the daily estimates.
