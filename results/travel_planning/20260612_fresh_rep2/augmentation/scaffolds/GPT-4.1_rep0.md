**Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow**

1. Requirements Check  
- Carefully review the task instructions and constraints to ensure thorough understanding.  
- Identify all explicit requirements (e.g., duration, destination, budget, exclusions, origin city, timeline).  
- Determine any implicit requirements such as daily activity planning, transportation, accommodation, food, and sightseeing.  
- List any information gaps or ambiguities.  
- Formulate clarifying questions for the user regarding preferences, travel style, activity interests, dietary needs, or flexibility.  
- Clearly state any necessary assumptions that will guide the planning process (e.g., flight class, hotel standard, meal frequency).

2. Plan & Structure  
- Outline the overall itinerary structure, including allocation of time for travel, accommodation, meals, and activities for each day.  
- Develop a budget breakdown, estimating costs for flights, lodging, transportation within Tokyo, food, and attractions, labeling all as estimates.  
- Ensure that the plan is realistic and budget-conscious, adjusting allocations as needed to stay within the specified budget.  
- Organize the itinerary by day, balancing activity types and locations to optimize the traveler’s experience and minimize logistical inefficiencies.  
- Prepare to present all estimates transparently and justify the rationale behind choices.

3. Draft -> Self-Review -> Finalize  
- Draft the full itinerary, integrating all requirements, assumptions, estimates, and clarifying questions.  
- Review the draft for completeness, clarity, feasibility, and adherence to constraints.  
- Double-check that all assumptions and estimates are clearly labeled and that the total projected cost does not exceed the budget.  
- Finalize the response, ensuring it is well-organized, user-friendly, and ready for user feedback or follow-up clarification.
