**Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow**

1. Requirements Check
- Read the task constraints and capture non-negotiables (trip length, destination, traveler count, origin, total budget, exclusion of passport costs).
- Prepare a short set of clarifying questions to resolve unknowns before planning (dates and flexibility, preferred accommodation standard, activity interests, mobility or dietary limitations, priority tradeoffs between comfort and cost, baggage, loyalty programs, arrival/departure time windows).
- Note assumptions you will make if the user does not answer (currency, rounding conventions, what “budget-aware” means, acceptable daily buffer, cancellation/refund assumptions). Plan to label all such assumptions clearly in the provisional plan.

2. Plan & Structure
- Decide the deliverable structure: executive summary, stated assumptions & clarifying questions, per-day schedule, budget breakdown (by category and per day), and alternatives/options.
- Define budget categories (transport, lodging, local transit, food, activities, incidental/emergency buffer) and how you will present estimates (rounded, labeled “estimate”).
- Choose a consistent format for day entries (time windows, activity descriptions, transport notes, cost estimates) and for totals (per-category and grand total). Include contingency margin and note exclusion of passport fees.

3. Draft -> Self-Review -> Finalize
- Draft the provisional plan using concise daily entries and an itemized budget with all estimates explicitly labeled.
- Self-review checklist: check internal arithmetic consistency, ensure all assumptions are stated, verify clarifying questions are listed up front, confirm budget totals equal category sums, and confirm passport exclusion is noted.
- Finalize: refine language for clarity and tone, ensure readers can act on the plan, and append next steps (answer questions or provide alternatives) without adding unresolved assumptions.
