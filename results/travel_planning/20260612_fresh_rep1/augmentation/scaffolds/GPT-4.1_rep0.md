**Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow**

1. Requirements Check  
- Carefully read the task and extract all explicit requirements (e.g., destination, duration, budget, exclusions, origin city, timeline).  
- Identify any implicit or missing information that may affect the plan (e.g., traveler preferences, accommodation standards, dietary restrictions, interests).  
- Prepare a concise list of clarifying questions to address uncertainties or gather additional information.  
- Clearly state all assumptions that will be used in the absence of specific details, ensuring transparency.

2. Plan & Structure  
- Outline a logical structure for the itinerary, breaking it down by day and including major components (e.g., flights, accommodation, meals, daily activities, local transportation).  
- Allocate the available budget across key categories, using realistic estimates for each (make sure to label them as estimates).  
- Ensure the plan remains within budget and time constraints, adjusting allocations as needed.  
- Prioritize essential experiences and balance cost-effectiveness with traveler comfort and enjoyment.  
- Prepare a provisional itinerary framework, ensuring clarity and coherence.

3. Draft -> Self-Review -> Finalize  
- Draft the detailed itinerary, integrating the structured plan and all relevant estimates.  
- Double-check that all requirements, assumptions, and clarifying questions are clearly stated at the beginning.  
- Review the draft for completeness, clarity, and consistency with the stated budget and other constraints.  
- Revise as needed for accuracy, conciseness, and user-friendliness before finalizing the response.
