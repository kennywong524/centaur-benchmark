**Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow**

**1. Requirements Check**
- Confirm the destination (Tokyo) and duration (5 days).
- Note the traveler profile (solo) and departure location (San Francisco).
- Identify the timeframe (next month).
- Verify the total budget ($2,500 USD) and exclusions (passport costs).
- Ensure the output must include stated assumptions and clarifying questions *before* the provisional itinerary.
- Note the requirement to use and explicitly label realistic budget estimates.
- Confirm the overall tone and structure must remain budget-aware throughout the itinerary.

**2. Plan & Structure**
- **Introduction:** Acknowledge the prompt and set a welcoming tone.
- **Assumptions & Clarifying Questions:** 
  - Draft a list of assumptions necessary to build the provisional plan (e.g., flight booking status, accommodation preferences, travel style).
  - Draft a list of clarifying questions for the user to refine the itinerary later.
- **Budget Breakdown:** Create a high-level estimated budget allocation (Flights, Accommodation, Food, Activities, Transit, Miscellaneous) ensuring the total does not exceed the limit.
- **5-Day Itinerary:** Outline a day-by-day structure (Days 1-5). For each day, plan morning, afternoon, and evening activities, including estimated costs for each major item.
- **Conclusion:** Add a brief wrap-up encouraging the user to answer the clarifying questions to finalize the plan.

**3. Draft -> Self-Review -> Finalize**
- Draft the response following the structure outlined above.
- Review against constraints: Are assumptions and questions placed *before* the itinerary? Is the budget exactly $2,500 USD? Are all costs labeled as estimates? Is the departure from San Francisco accounted for?
- Refine the formatting for readability (use bolding, bullet points, and clear headings).
- Finalize the output ensuring a helpful, objective, and budget-conscious tone.
