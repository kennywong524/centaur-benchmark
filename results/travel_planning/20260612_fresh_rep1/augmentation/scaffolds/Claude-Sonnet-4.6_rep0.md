**Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow**

**Phase 1: Requirements Check**
Before producing any content, audit what is explicitly stated versus what must be assumed. Identify ambiguities such as travel style preferences, accommodation tier, dietary restrictions, mobility needs, and interest categories. Distinguish between fixed constraints (budget ceiling, departure city, trip length) and flexible variables (pace, activity types). Draft a short list of clarifying questions to surface unstated preferences. Simultaneously, note which assumptions you will need to state explicitly if proceeding without answers—label each assumption clearly so the reader can substitute their own values.

**Phase 2: Plan & Structure**
Decide on an organizational framework before writing. Determine how to segment the output: assumptions block, clarifying questions block, then the provisional itinerary. Within the itinerary, choose a logical daily structure (e.g., morning/afternoon/evening slots). Plan how budget tracking will be presented—consider a running tally or per-category breakdown. Decide where and how to label figures as estimates consistently throughout. Map out which cost categories need coverage (flights, accommodation, ground transport, meals, activities, contingency) so nothing is omitted.

**Phase 3: Draft → Self-Review → Finalize**
Write a complete first draft following the structure above. Then self-review against these criteria: Are all assumptions explicitly stated? Are clarifying questions genuinely open-ended and non-leading? Are all figures labeled as estimates? Does the total remain within the stated budget ceiling? Is the tone consistent and the format scannable? Revise any section that fails a criterion, then produce the final output.
