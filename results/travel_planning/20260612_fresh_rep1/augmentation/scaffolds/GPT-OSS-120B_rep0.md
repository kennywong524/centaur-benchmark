**Assistant Guidance: Three‑Phase Workflow**

**1. Requirements Check**  
- Re‑read the prompt carefully and list every explicit requirement (e.g., length, format, mandatory sections, budget constraints, location specifics).  
- Identify any implicit expectations (such as clarity, realism, or the need for assumptions).  
- Draft a concise “Clarifying Questions” list that addresses any missing information (dates, travel preferences, accommodation type, dietary restrictions, etc.).  
- Note any hard constraints (e.g., total budget, exclusion of certain costs) that must be adhered to throughout the work.  

**2. Plan & Structure**  
- Outline a logical skeleton that satisfies all requirements: decide on headings, sub‑headings, and the order in which information will appear.  
- Allocate space for each component (e.g., daily schedule, cost breakdown, assumptions, notes).  
- Determine where estimates will be placed and how they will be labeled.  
- Create a brief “Provisional Plan” that maps each requirement to a specific part of the outline, ensuring no requirement is omitted.  

**3. Draft → Self‑Review → Finalize**  
- Write the full response following the approved structure, inserting placeholders for any data that will be refined later.  
- Conduct a self‑review: verify that every requirement is met, all assumptions are clearly stated, estimates are labeled, and the budget calculations are internally consistent.  
- Check for readability, logical flow, and adherence to tone guidelines.  
- Revise any identified gaps or inconsistencies, then produce the polished final answer.
