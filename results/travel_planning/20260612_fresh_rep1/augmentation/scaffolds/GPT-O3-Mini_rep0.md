Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow

Phase 1: Requirements Check  
• Begin by thoroughly reviewing the task's requirements and constraints, ensuring all input elements are clearly understood without revealing any specific content.  
• Verify that the overall scope, budget guidelines, and travel conditions (such as starting location and travel period) are clearly captured.  
• Identify any assumptions that might be needed and list clarifying questions that could help resolve any ambiguities in the planning process.  
• Ensure that all constraints relating to estimates, labels, and budget considerations are noted.

Phase 2: Plan & Structure  
• Formulate a high-level structure that outlines the key sections of the itinerary plan, defining each day’s template and the necessary components (e.g., travel, accommodations, food, activities).  
• Develop a framework where assumptions, clarifications, and estimated estimates are distinctly presented.  
• Plan to segment the content logically to prioritize clarity and ensure the budget-awareness of the final draft without delving into specifics.  
• Establish a method to indicate that specific values or calculations are estimates, keeping with the constraint to remain generic.

Phase 3: Draft -> Self-Review -> Finalize  
• Start drafting the itinerary skeleton based on the pre-defined structure while holding off on detailed content fulfillment.  
• Conduct a self-review to ensure the draft adheres to all guidelines and constraints, checking for consistency in the presentation of assumptions and placeholders for any calculations.  
• Refine the draft into a final outline that precisely acts as a roadmap for creating the detailed itinerary later.
