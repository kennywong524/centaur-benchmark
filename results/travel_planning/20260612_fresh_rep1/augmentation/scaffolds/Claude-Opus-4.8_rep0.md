**Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow**

**Phase 1 — Requirements Check**
Begin by parsing every explicit instruction: trip length, traveler count, timing, total budget and its exclusions, departure point, and the requested output elements. List each requirement so none is missed. Note constraints that shape format (assumptions section, clarifying questions, estimate labeling, budget awareness). Identify what information is ambiguous or missing and flag it for the clarifying-questions portion. Confirm units, currency, and the boundary of what's included versus excluded.

**Phase 2 — Plan & Structure**
Decide the document's skeleton before writing: where assumptions go, where clarifying questions sit, and how the provisional plan follows. Choose an organizing logic for the days and a consistent way to present cost lines so totals can be tracked against the budget. Plan a running or summary tally mechanism so the figure stays visible. Decide how you'll mark figures as estimates uniformly. Sketch categories of spending to ensure completeness without yet filling in values. Sequence sections so assumptions and questions precede the plan, as requested.

**Phase 3 — Draft → Self-Review → Finalize**
Write the draft following your structure, keeping numbers clearly labeled as estimates and tied to the stated budget. Then self-review against Phase 1's checklist: Are all required elements present and ordered correctly? Do estimates carry labels? Does the total respect the budget and exclusions? Are assumptions explicit and questions genuinely clarifying? Verify internal consistency between daily costs and any summary. Adjust gaps, then finalize with a clean, scannable format.
