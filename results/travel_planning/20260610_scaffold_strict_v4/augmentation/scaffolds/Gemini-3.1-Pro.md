**Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow**

**1. Requirements Check**
- Confirm the destination, duration, and number of travelers.
- Note the total budget constraint and the specified currency.
- Identify the departure city.
- Note the exclusions from the budget (e.g., passport costs).
- Ensure the output must include stated assumptions and clarifying questions *before* presenting the provisional itinerary.
- Verify the requirement to use realistic, labeled estimates and to maintain budget awareness throughout the plan.

**2. Plan & Structure**
- **Introduction:** Set up a section for assumptions (e.g., flight costs, accommodation type, exchange rates) and clarifying questions (e.g., interests, dietary restrictions) to address the prompt's specific ordering constraint.
- **Budget Breakdown:** Create a high-level allocation for the total budget, setting aside estimated amounts for flights, accommodation, food, transportation, and activities. Ensure these are clearly labeled as estimates.
- **Daily Itinerary (Days 1-5):** Outline a structure for a 5-day schedule. For each day, plan slots for morning, afternoon, and evening activities, alongside estimated costs for each component to demonstrate budget awareness.
- **Conclusion/Summary:** Plan a brief wrap-up confirming the estimated total spend against the budget constraint.

**3. Draft -> Self-Review -> Finalize**
- *Draft:* Write the response following the planned structure. Ensure the assumptions and questions precede the itinerary. Populate the itinerary with realistic, estimated costs for flights from the specified origin, lodging, and daily expenses.
- *Self-Review:* Check against all constraints: Is it exactly 5 days? Is the budget exactly as specified? Are all costs labeled as estimates? Are assumptions and questions at the beginning? Does the math roughly align with the budget limit?
- *Finalize:* Refine the formatting for readability, ensuring clear headings and bullet points. Polish the tone to be helpful and organized.
