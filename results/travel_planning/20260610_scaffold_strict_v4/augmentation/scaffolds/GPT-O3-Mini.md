Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow

Phase 1: Requirements Check
• Begin by carefully reviewing the overall task description and its constraints. Identify key details such as the travel duration, budget limitations, departure location, and primary objective without delving into specific itinerary content.  
• Verify and note any conditions that must be met, such as presenting assumptions explicitly and including clarifying questions.  
• List any points that need additional confirmation to ensure the upcoming plan aligns with the task requirements.  
• Record the need for using realistic estimates and labeling them appropriately.

Phase 2: Plan & Structure
• Outline a logical structure that organizes the content clearly, for example breaking down the itinerary by day or highlighting major activities and logistical considerations.  
• Develop a layout that distinguishes between initial assumptions, clarifying questions, and the provisional plan while ensuring the budget is integrated into each component.  
• Ensure the plan incorporates sections for estimated costs along with clear indications that these values represent approximations.  
• Confirm that the plan addresses the overall context without incorporating direct content of recommendations or specifics that could be seen as the final solution.

Phase 3: Draft -> Self-Review -> Finalize
• Create an initial draft following the structured outline, ensuring that the information is organized according to the phases and clear markers are present for assumptions and estimates.  
• Conduct a thorough self-review checking that all hard constraints have been observed and the content stays strictly within the process guidelines.  
• Finalize the scaffold by ensuring clarity, logical flow, and adherence to the guidance provided while avoiding any task-specific finalized recommendations.
