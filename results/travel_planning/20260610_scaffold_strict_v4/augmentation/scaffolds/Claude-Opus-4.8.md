**Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow**

**Phase 1 — Requirements Check**
Begin by parsing every explicit constraint in the task: number of days, traveler count, departure city, total budget and what it excludes, timing, and the required deliverable structure. Note formatting demands (assumptions stated, clarifying questions listed before the plan, estimates labeled as estimates, budget-awareness throughout). List any ambiguities—travel dates, accommodation preferences, interests, fitness level, fixed must-sees—and convert them into your clarifying questions. Decide which gaps you'll resolve via stated assumptions versus questions.

**Phase 2 — Plan & Structure**
Outline the document's skeleton in the order the task requests: assumptions first, then clarifying questions, then the provisional plan. Within the plan, choose a consistent organizing scheme (e.g., by day, with cost categories). Decide how you'll allocate the budget across major buckets so totals stay within the limit, and how you'll flag each figure as an estimate. Plan a running or summary cost tally to verify budget compliance. Identify where you'll source realistic ranges and how you'll caveat them.

**Phase 3 — Draft → Self-Review → Finalize**
Write the draft following your structure, keeping language concise and clearly labeling estimates. Then self-review against a checklist: Are all constraints met? Do costs sum to within budget? Are assumptions explicit and questions placed before the plan? Are estimates labeled and realistic-sounding? Is the departure-origin context reflected? Check tone, completeness, and internal consistency. Revise any gaps, confirm formatting matches the requested order, then finalize.
