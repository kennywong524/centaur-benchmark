**Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow**

1. Requirements Check
- Gather all input variables that must be confirmed (dates, duration, total budget, origin, traveler preferences, non-negotiables, mobility/health constraints, visa/passport notes) and list which are missing.
- Compile a concise set of clarifying questions to present to the user before any plan is drafted.
- Identify hard constraints and deliverables (what must be included, labeling of items as estimates, where costs are excluded).
- Note sources and data points needed for realistic estimates (flight, lodging, local transport, activities, meals, fees, contingency) and confirm which sources are acceptable.

2. Plan & Structure
- Sketch a modular day-by-day skeleton: morning/afternoon/evening slots and transport segments; mark which slots will hold higher-cost items.
- Define budget categories and a method for allocating the total across categories; decide how to round and present estimates (per-item and cumulative).
- Establish assumptions to state up front (format and labeling) and decide how to mark anything tentative.
- Choose research approach and allowed websites/tools for price checks, plus a date to freeze prices for the provisional plan.
- Create decision rules for substitutions and cost-tradeoffs (e.g., cheaper transport vs. extra activity).

3. Draft -> Self-Review -> Finalize
- Produce a first draft that begins with clarifying questions and stated assumptions, then the provisional day-by-day plan with all costs labeled as estimates and a budget summary.
- Use a checklist for self-review: arithmetic consistency, realistic timing, labels for estimates, adherence to constraints, tone and clarity.
- Cross-check sources, update any volatile price fields, add contingency and notes on optional adjustments.
- Finalize formatting, include a brief methodology note, and list outstanding questions for the user.
