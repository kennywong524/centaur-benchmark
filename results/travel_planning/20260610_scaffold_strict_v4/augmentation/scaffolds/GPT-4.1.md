**Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow**

1. Requirements Check  
Carefully review the task instructions to identify all explicit requirements (e.g., trip duration, budget, origin and destination, exclusions like passport costs). Note any implicit needs, such as the need for cost estimates and labeling. Identify areas needing clarification—such as travel preferences, accommodation standards, interests, dietary restrictions, or specific must-see sites. Prepare a list of clarifying questions to present before drafting the itinerary. Confirm the timing (next month) and consider seasonal/weather factors.

2. Plan & Structure  
Outline the overall structure of the itinerary and budget. Decide how to divide the five days (e.g., arrival, sightseeing, departure). Allocate the total budget into categories (flights, accommodation, food, transportation, activities, contingency). Identify reliable sources or typical price ranges for each category to inform realistic estimates. Determine how to clearly label all estimates. Plan to state all key assumptions (e.g., average hotel class, public transport usage, meal types). Ensure all elements are budget-conscious and feasible for a single traveler.

3. Draft -> Self-Review -> Finalize  
Draft the itinerary day-by-day, integrating activities, logistics, and budget breakdowns. Present clarifying questions and state all assumptions before the provisional plan. Clearly label each cost as an estimate and maintain budget awareness throughout. After drafting, self-review for clarity, completeness, and adherence to constraints. Check that all requirements are satisfied, estimates are reasonable, and assumptions are transparent. Revise as needed, then finalize the answer for submission.
