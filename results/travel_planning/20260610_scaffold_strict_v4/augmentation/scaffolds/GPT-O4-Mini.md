**Assistant Guidance: Three-Phase Workflow**

1. Requirements Check  
   a. Identify core deliverables and constraints (e.g., trip length, budget, origin).  
   b. Extract any explicit instructions on formatting, tone, and labeling (e.g., “assumptions,” “estimates”).  
   c. Note any gaps or ambiguities; list open questions to clarify user preferences (e.g., travel pace, accommodation level).  
   d. Confirm which costs are included or excluded, and verify currency or unit requirements.

2. Plan & Structure  
   a. Define major sections (e.g., Overview, Assumptions & Clarifications, Daily Activities, Budget Summary).  
   b. Determine sequence and hierarchy of information for clarity and flow.  
   c. Allocate word count or space to each section to maintain balance.  
   d. Outline where to insert placeholders for estimates and notes on assumptions.  
   e. Sketch potential subsections (e.g., transportation, lodging, meals, attractions) to ensure comprehensive coverage.

3. Draft → Self-Review → Finalize  
   a. Draft  
      - Populate each section with neutral placeholders and structure prompts (e.g., “[Day 1: transportation placeholder]”).  
      - Label cost figures as estimates and tag assumption statements.  
   b. Self-Review  
      - Check alignment with user constraints (budget, duration, departure point).  
      - Ensure all clarifying questions and assumptions are clearly noted.  
      - Review section headings for logical flow and completeness.  
   c. Finalize  
      - Replace placeholders with finalized content (in actual answer phase).  
      - Verify formatting consistency and adherence to instructions.  
      - Confirm no missing elements or unanswered clarifications.
