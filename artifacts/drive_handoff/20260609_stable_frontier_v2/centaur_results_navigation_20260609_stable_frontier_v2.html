Gemini-3.1-Pro was excluded from every aggregate leaderboard because its rubric JSON parse pass rate was below the 90% threshold.

How To Navigate The Workbook

  • README_index: map of each task/mode to its local source files and judge inclusion notes.
  • rank_aug_aggregate and rank_auto_aggregate: main paper-facing model ranks by task. Lower rank is better.
  • win_aug_aggregate and win_auto_aggregate: aggregate pairwise win rates by task. Higher is better.
  • all_leaderboards: long-format cross-task table used to build aggregate heatmaps.
  • leaderboards_by_judge: same as above but separated by judge.
  • per_task_aggregate: each task/mode aggregate leaderboard stacked together.
  • rubric_scores_summary: dimension-level scores by judge, model, task, and mode.

Headline Reading

The framework produces model profiles rather than one universal leaderboard. Automation and augmentation rankings diverge, which supports the paper's central claim that direct task completion and scaffolding ability are empirically distinct capabilities.

What To Make Of It

  • Augmentation: DeepSeek-V3.1, GPT-4.1, Claude-Opus-4.8, and GPT-O4-Mini are the strongest average performers, but task-level winners vary a lot.
  • Automation: DeepSeek-V3.1 has the strongest average rank in this run, followed by GPT-OSS-120B and Claude-Sonnet-4.6. GPT-3.5-Turbo is consistently weak as a direct automator, which is expected for the baseline worker.
  • Task specificity matters: model rankings move substantially across counseling, market trends, menu planning, OR, tax, travel, and tutoring. This is exactly why the results should be presented as a taxonomy/profile rather than a single score.
  • Judge caveat: these are n_evals=1 panel results with Gemini excluded for parse reliability. They are good for analysis and paper drafting, but a future robustness run should repeat pairwise judging and/or repair Gemini parsing.
